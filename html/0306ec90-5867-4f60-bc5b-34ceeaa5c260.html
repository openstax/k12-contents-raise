Here are three graphs representing three exponential functions: \(f\), \(g\), and \(h\).

The functions \(f\) and \(h\) are given by \(f(x)=10 \cdot 2^x\) and \(h(x)=20 \cdot 4^x\). Which of the following could define function \(g\)? Explain your reasoning.

Equation A: \(g(x)=20 \cdot (1.5)^x\)

Equation B: \(g(x)=20 \cdot (2.5)^x\)

Equation C: \(g(x)=10 \cdot (3.5)^x\)

Equation D: \(g(x)=20 \cdot (4.5)^x\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to check your work.

Compare your answer:

Equation B. The graph of \(g\) has the same \(y\)-intercept as the graph of \(h\), which is 20. It grows more quickly than \(f\) but more slowly than \(h\), so the growth factor must be greater than 2 but less than 4.
