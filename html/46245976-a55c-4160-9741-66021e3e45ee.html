Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Here is a graph of \(p\), an insect population, \(w\) weeks after it was first measured. The population grows exponentially. [Teacher note: Graph is drawn to scale, but students may have trouble since at \(t=0\), \(p=300\), and that is not marked on the graph. Students are forced to find the growth factor and work backward.]
    1. What is the weekly factor of growth for the insect population?

ANSWER: 3

    1. What was the population when it was first measured?


ANSWER: 300

    1. Write an equation relating \(p\) and \(w\).
      1. \(p=300+3^w\)
      2. \(p=300 \cdot 3^w\)
      3. \(p= \frac{300}{3^w}\)
      4. \(p=-300 \cdot 3^w\)

ANSWER: b. \(p=300 \cdot 3^w\)
