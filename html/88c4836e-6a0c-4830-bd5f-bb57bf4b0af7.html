Solve Application Problems Using Direct Variation

In applications using direct variation, we will generally know values of one pair of the variables and will be asked to find the equation that relates \( x \) and \( y \). Then we can use that equation to find values of \( y \) for other values of \( x \).

Recall how to model an equation using direct variation for application problems:

Step 1

Write the equation for direct variation.

Step 2

Identify the variables.

Step 3

Substitute the given values for the variables.

Step 4

Solve for the constant of variation.

Step 5

Write the equation that relates \( x \) and \( y \) using the constant of variation.

Step 6

Substitute the value for the variable.

Step 7

Solve.


Let’s look at an application problem:

If the cost of a pizza varies directly with its diameter, and an 8-inch pizza costs $12, how much would a 6-inch pizza cost?

Step 1

Write the equation for direct variation.

\( y = kx \)

Step 2

Identify the variables.

\( y \): cost of pizza;
\( x \): diameter

Step 3

Substitute the given values for the variables.

\( 12 = k(8) \)

Step 4

Solve for the constant of variation.

\( k=\frac{12}{8}=\frac{3}{2} \)

Step 4

Write the equation that relates \( x \) and \( y \) using the constant of variation.

\( y=\frac{3}{2}x \)

Step 4

Substitute the value for the variable.

\( y=\frac{3}{2}(6) \)

Step 4

Solve.

\( y = 9  \)


What does this mean in the context of the problem?

A pizza that has a diameter of 6 inches costs $9.


Try It: Solve Application Problems Using Direct Variation

The number of buckets of water, \(b\), filled by a spring varies directly with the number of minutes, \(m\), that have passed. The spring fills 3 buckets of water in 14 minutes.

How many buckets would be filled in 42 minutes?

Write down your answer, and then select the Solution button to check your work.


Here is how to model this application problem of direct variation:

Step 1

Write the formula for direct variation.

\( y = kx \)
\( b=km \)

Step 2

Identify the variables.

\( b \): number of buckets;
\( m \): number of minutes

Step 3

Substitute the given values for the variables.

\( 3=k(14) \)

Step 4

Solve for the constant of variation.

\( k= \frac{3}{14} \)

Step 5

Write the equation that relates \( m \) and \( b \) using the constant of variation.

\( b=\frac{3}{14}m \)

Step 6

Substitute the value for the variable.

\( b=\frac{3}{14}(42) \)

Step 7

Solve.

\( b= 9  \) buckets
