Switching Between Definitions of Sequences

A recursive formula defines each term of a sequence using the previous terms.

An explicit or nth term formula defines the terms of the sequence using their position in the sequence, without knowing any other terms in the sequence.

Sequence

Recursive Formula

Explicit or nth Term Formula

Arithmetic

\(F_1=a_1\), \(F_n=F_{n-1}+d\), where \(a_1\) is the first term and \(d\) is the common difference.

\(a_n=a_1+(n-1)d\), where \(a_1\) is the first term, \(n\) is the term you want, and \(d\) is the common difference.

Geometric

\(H_1=a_1\), \(H_n=H_{n-1} \cdot r\), where \(a_1\) is the first term and \(r\) is the common ratio.

\(a_n=a_1r^{n-1}\), where \(a_1\) is the first term, \(n\) is the term you want, and \(r\) is the common ratio.

Find the explicit formula for each sequence given in recursive form:

  1. \(a_1=5\), \(a_n=a_{n-1}+6\), \(n\geq2\)

Write the recursive formula.

\(a_1=5\), \(a_n=a_{n-1}+6\), \(n\geq2\)

Identify the first term.

\(a_1=5\)

Identify the common difference.

\(d=6\)

Write the explicit formula.

\(a_n=a_1+(n-1)d\)

Substitute \(a_1\) and \(d\) into the formula.

\(a_n=5+(n-1)6\)

Check by finding the first 3 terms using each formula:

\(a_1=5\), \(a(n)=a_n=a_{n-1}+6\), \(n\geq2\)

\(a_1=5\)

\(a_2=5+6=11\)

\(a_3=11+6=17\)

\(a_n=5+(n-1)6\)

\(a_1=5\)

\(a_2=5+(2-1)6=11\)

\(a_3=5+(3-1)6=17\)

Both formulas produce the same first 3 terms: 5, 11, 17.

  1. \(b_1=4\), \(b_n=b_{n-1} \cdot 5\), \(n\geq2\)

Write the recursive formula.

\(b_1=4\), \(b_n=b_{n-1} \cdot 5\), \(n\geq2\)

Identify the first term.

\(a_1=4\)

Identify the common ratio.

\(r=5\)

Write the explicit formula.

\(a_n=a_1r^{n-1}\)

Substitute \(a_1\) and \(r\) into the formula.

\(a_n=4(5)^{n-1}\)

Check by finding the first 3 terms using each formula:

\(b_1=4\), \(b_n=b_{n-1} \cdot 5\), \(n\geq2\)

\(a_1=4\)

\(b_2=b_{2-1} \cdot 5=4 \cdot 5=20\)

\(b_3=b_{3-1} \cdot 5=20 \cdot 5=100\)

\(a_n=4(5)^{n-1}\)

\(a_1=4(5)^{1-1}=4\)

\(a_2=4(5)^{2-1}=20\)

\(a_3=4(5)^{3-1}=100\)

Both formulas produce the same first 3 terms: 4, 20, 100.

  1. \(a_n=21+(n-1)(-2)\)

Write the explicit formula.

\(a_n=21+(n-1)(-2)\)

Identify the first term.

\(a_1=21\)

Identify the common difference.

\(d=-2\)

Write the recursive formula.

\(F_1=a_1\), \(F_n=F_{n-1}+d\), \(n\geq2\)

\(F_1=21\), \(F_n=F_{n-1}-2\), \(n\geq2\)

  1. \(a_n=3 \cdot (\frac14)^{n-1}\)

Write the explicit formula.

\(a_n=3 \cdot (\frac14)^{n-1}\)

Identify the first term.

\(a_1=3\)

Identify the common ratio.

\(r= \frac14\)

Write the recursive formula.

\(H_1=a_1\), \(H_n=H_{n-1} \cdot R\), \(n\geq2\)

Substitute \(a_1\) and \(r\) into the formula.

\(H_1=3\), \(H_n=H_{n-1} \cdot \frac14\), \(n\geq2\)

Both formulas produce the same first three terms: 3, \(\frac34\), \(\frac{3}{16}\).
