Activity

Here are three types of situations. Answer the questions about each situation.

Bank Accounts

1. A customer opens a checking account and a savings account at a bank. They will deposit a maximum of $600, some in the checking account and some in the savings account. (They might not deposit all of it and keep some of the money as cash.)

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.

Enter your answer here:

\(x+y\leq600\), where x represents the amount deposited in the checking account and y the amount deposited in the savings account.



b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

(500,100). $500 is deposited in the checking account and $100 in the savings account. 

The total is $600, which is the maximum amount the customer plans to deposit.



d. If the customer deposits $200 in their checking account, what can you say about the amount they deposit in their savings account?

Enter your answer here:

The customer deposits $400 or less in savings.


2. A customer opens a checking account and a savings account at a bank. The bank requires a minimum balance of $50 in the savings account. It does not matter how much money is kept in the checking account.

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.


Enter your answer here:

\(y\geq50\), where y is the balance in the savings account.


b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

75. The customer deposits $75 in savings, which is more than $50.


d. If the customer deposits no money in the checking account but is able to maintain both accounts without penalty, what can you say about the amount deposited in the savings account?

Enter your answer here:

The customer deposits at least $50 in the savings account.


Concert Tickets

1. Two kinds of tickets to an outdoor concert were sold: lawn tickets and seat tickets. Fewer than 400 tickets in total were sold.

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.

Enter your answer here:

\(L+S<400\), where L represents the number of lawn tickets and S represents the number of seat tickets.



b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

(100,200). 100 lawn tickets and 200 seat tickets were sold. The total is 300 tickets, which is fewer than 400.



d. If you know that exactly 100 lawn tickets were sold, what can you say about the number of seat tickets?

Enter your answer here:

Fewer than 300 seat tickets were sold.


2. Lawn tickets cost $30 each and seat tickets cost $50 each. The organizers made at least $14,000 from ticket sales.

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.

Enter your answer here:

\(30L+50S\geq14,000\), where L represents the number of lawn tickets and S represents the 

number of seat tickets.



b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

100 lawn tickets and 250 seat tickets. The ticket sales would be 15,500, which is more 

than 14,000.


d. If you know that exactly 200 seat tickets were sold, what can you say about the number of lawn tickets?

Enter your answer here:

At least 134 lawn tickets must be sold to make at least $14,000.


Advertising Packages

An advertising agency offers two packages for small businesses who need advertising services. A basic package includes only design services. A premium package includes design and promotion. The agency's goal is to sell at least 60 packages in total.

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.

Enter your answer here:

\(b+p\geq60\), where b represents the number of basic packages and p represents the number of premium packages.


b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

(50,30). 50 basic packages and 30 premium ones are sold. The total is 80, which is more than 60.


d. If the agency sells exactly 45 basic packages, what can you say about the number of premium packages it needs to sell to meet its goal?

Enter your answer here:

The agency needs to sell at least 15 premium packages to meet its goal.


2. The basic advertising package has a value of $1,000 and the premium package has a value of $2,500. The goal of the agency is to sell more than $60,000 worth of small-business advertising packages.

If you know that exactly 10 premium packages were sold, what can you say about the number of basic packages the agency needs to sell to meet its goal?

a. Write an inequality to describe the constraints. Specify what each variable represents.

Enter your answer here:

\(1,000b+2,500p>60,000\), where b represents the number of basic packages and p represents the number of premium packages.


b. Use graphing technology to graph the inequality. Sketch the solution region on the coordinate plane and label the axes.


Enter your answer here:


c. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.

Enter your answer here:

(10,30). 10 basic packages and 30 premium packages are sold. The total value is $85,000, which is more than $60,000.


d. If you know that exactly 10 premium packages were sold, what can you say about the number of basic packages the agency needs to sell to meet its goal?

Enter your answer here:

The agency needs to sell more than 35 basic packages.
