Warm-Up Activity

Use the slope formula, \( m =\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\), to find the slope of the line through the given points.

1. \( (1, -2) \) and \( (3, 4) \)

Enter your answer here:

Check your answer:

\( m= 3 \)

First, name \( (1, −2) \) as point 1 and \( (3, 4) \) as point 2.

\( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \)

\( (1,−2) \) and \( (3, 4) \)

Use the slope formula.

\( m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \)

Substitute the values: \( y_2 = 4, y_1 = -2, x_2 = 3, x_1 = 1 \).

\( m=\frac{4-(-2)}{3-(1)} \)

Simplify.

\( m=\frac{6}{2} \)

\( m=3 \)


2. \( (-2, -3) \) and \( (-7, 4) \)

Check your answer:

\( m=-\frac{7}{5} \)

First, name \( (−2, −3) \) as point 1 and \( (−7, 4) \) as point 2.

\( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \)

\( (−2,−3)\) and \( (−7, 4) \)

Use the slope formula.

\( m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \)

Substitute the values: \( y_2 = 4, y_1 = −3, x_2 = −7, x_1 = −2 \).

\( m= \frac{4-(-3)}{-7-(-2)} \)

Simplify.

\( m= \frac{7}{-5} \)

\( m= -\frac{7}{5} \)
