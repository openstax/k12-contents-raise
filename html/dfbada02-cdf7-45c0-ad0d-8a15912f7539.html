Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student-Facing Statement

  1. A sequence is defined by \(f_1=-3\), \(f_n=f_{n-1}+0.5\) for \(n\geq2\). Diego writes an equation for the nth term of the sequence as \(a_n=-3+(n-1)(0.5)\). Is he correct? Explain how you know.
  1. How would you describe the representation \(f_1=-3\), \(f_n=f_{n-1}+0.5\) for \(n\geq2\) for Diego’s sequence?
  1. How would you describe the representation of Diego’s sequence, \(a_n=-3+(n-1)(0.5)\)?

Student Response

  1. Yes. Sample response: Both definitions create a sequence that starts at –3 and increases by 0.5 each term.

  2. It is the recursive definition of the sequence.

  3. This is the nth term of the sequence.
