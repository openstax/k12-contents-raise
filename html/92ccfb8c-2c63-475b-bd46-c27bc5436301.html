Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1–4, use the following:

Function \(h\) describes the height of a ball, in inches, after \(n\) bounces and is defined by the equation \(h(n)=120 \cdot (\frac{4}{5})^n\).

  1. What is \(h(3)\)? Round to the nearest integer. Think about what it represents in this situation.

ANSWER: 61

  1. Could \(h(n)\) be 150? 

ANSWER: No

  1. Which ball loses its height more quickly: This ball, modeled by \(h(n)=120 \cdot (\frac{4}{5})^n\), or a tennis ball whose height in inches after \(n\) bounces is modeled by the function \(f\) where \(f(n)=50 \cdot (\frac{5}{9})^n\)?

ANSWER: The ball modeled by \(f(n)\)

  1. How many bounces would it take before the ball bounces less than 12 inches from the surface?

ANSWER: 11
