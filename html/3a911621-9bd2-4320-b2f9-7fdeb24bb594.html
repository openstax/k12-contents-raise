When the quadratic paths of pumpkins in the Punkin Chunkin are graphed, their intercepts and other key points describe important parts of the path such as where the pumpkin was launched from and how far it travels.

Standard and factored forms can not only help identify the intercepts, but a third form, the vertex form, helps to identify the vertex. The intercepts and vertex help to identify key characteristics such as maximum height and distance traveled before landing.
