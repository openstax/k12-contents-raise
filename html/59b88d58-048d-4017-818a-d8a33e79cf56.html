To help students’ understanding of key points from the lesson, pose the following questions:

  • What is unique about the first and last terms in a perfect square trinomial? (They are perfect squares.)
  • What is the pattern of the middle term in a perfect square trinomial? \((\pm2ab)\)
  • To what binomial does the trinomial \(a^2 − 2ab + b^2\) factor? \([ (a − b)^2 ]\)
  • What do you call the binomials \((a − b)\) and \((a + b)\)? (conjugate pair)
  • What is the pattern used to factor a difference of squares? \([ a^2 − b^2 = (a − b)(a + b) ]\)
  • Can you factor \(a^2 + b^2\)? (No, it is prime.)
