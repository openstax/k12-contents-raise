Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Here are the first two terms of some different arithmetic sequences. What is the 5th term of each sequence?
    1. –2, 4, . . .

Answer: 22

    1. 11, 111, . . .

Answer: 411

    1. 5, 7.5, . . .

Answer: 15

    1. 5, –4, . . .

Answer: –31

  1. For each sequence, decide whether it could be arithmetic, geometric, or neither.
    1. 200, 40, 8, . . .

Answer: geometric

    1. 2, 4, 16, . . .

Answer: neither

    1. 10, 20, 30, . . .

Answer: arithmetic

    1. 100, 20, 4, . . .

Answer: geometric

    1. 6, 12, 18, . . .

Answer: arithmetic

  1. For each arithmetic sequence with missing terms, find the common difference that might help determine the missing terms.
    1. What is the common difference for the arthmetic sequence: –3, –2, ___, ___, 1

Answer: 1; The missing terms are 1, 0.

    1. What is the common difference for the arthmetic sequence: ___, 13, 25, ___, ___

Answer: 12; The sequence should be 1, 13, 25, 37, 49.

    1. What is the common difference for the arthmetic sequence: 1, 0.25, ___, –1.25, ___

Answer: –0.75; The sequence is 1, 0.25, –0.5, –1.25, –2.

    1. What is the common difference for the arthmetic sequence: 92, ___, ___ ,___, 80

Answer: –3; The sequence is 92, 89, 86, 83, 80.

  1. Find the common difference for each of the following arithmetic sequences. HINT: It may be helpful to complete the sequence with the missing terms.
    1. ___, 6, 10, ___, 18

    1. –1, ___, –11, –16, ___

    1. 10, 5, ___, ___, –10

Answer: –5; The sequence is 10, 5, 0, –5, –10.

    1. ___, ___, 4.5, 6, ___
