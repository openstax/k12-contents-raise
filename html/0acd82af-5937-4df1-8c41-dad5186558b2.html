Remember that \((a − b)\) and \((a + b)\) are a conjugate pair. When you multiply them together, you end up with the difference of squares, \(a^2 − b^2\).

Let’s look at the pattern used to factor a difference of squares:

\(a^2 − b^2 = (a − b)(a + b)\)

Work with a partner to factor using the difference of squares.

As you factor each binomial, take turns with your partner, alternating the completion of each step one at a time. If you do not know the next step, ask your partner for help.
