Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. To support a local senior citizens center, a student club sent a flier home to the \(n\) students in the school. The flier said, “Please bring in money to support the senior citizens center. Paper money and coins accepted!” Their goal is to raise \(T\) dollars.

Choose the expression, equation, or inequality, from the list, that describes the given quantity.

  • \(T+50\)
  • \(0.5T\)
  • \(0.25n\)
  • \(0.5n\)
  • \(T-0.5n\)
  1. The dollar amount the club would have if they reached half of their goal.

Answer: \(0.5T\)

  1. The dollar amount the club would have if every student at the school donated 50 cents to the cause.

Answer: \(0.5n\)

  1. The dollar amount the club could donate if they made $50 more than their goal.

Answer: \(T+50\)

  1. The dollar amount the club would still need to raise to reach its goal after every student at the school donated 50 cents.

Answer: \(T-.05n\)

  1. The dollar amount the club would have if half of the students at the school each gave 50 cents.

Answer: \(0.25n\)

  1. Each of the 10 students in the baking club made 2 chocolate cakes for a fundraiser. They all used the same recipe, using \(C\) cups of flour in total.

Write an expression that represents the amount of flour required for one cake.

ANSWER: \(\frac{C}{20}\)

  1. A student club started a fundraising effort to support animal rescue organizations. The club sent an information flier home to the \(n\) students in the school. It says, “We welcome donations of any amount, including any change you could spare!” Their goal is to raise \(T\) dollars and to donate to a cat shelter and a dog shelter.

Choose the expression, equation, or inequality, from the list, that describes the given quantity.

  • \(\frac{\;3}4n⋅\frac12\)
  • \(\frac14T\)
  • \(T-\frac14n\)
  • \(\frac{\;3\;}4T\)
  • \(\frac{\;1\;}4n\)
  1. The dollar amount the club would have if they reached one-fourth of their goal.

Answer: \(\frac14T\)

  1. The dollar amount the club would have if every student at the school donated a quarter to the cause.

Answer: \(\frac{\;1\;}4n\)

  1. c. The dollar amount the club could donate to the cat shelter if they reached their goal and gave a quarter of the total donation to a dog shelter.

Answer: \(\frac{\;3\;}4T\)

  1. The dollar amount the club would still need to raise to reach its goal after every student at the school donated a quarter.

Answer: \(T-\frac14n\)

  1. e. The dollar amount the club would have if three-fourths of the students at the school each gave 50 cents.

Answer: \(\frac{\;3}4n⋅\frac12\)

  1. Determine if each is an expression or an equation:
  • \(3(x - 7) = 27\)
  • \(5(4y - 2) - 7\)
  • \(y^3 ÷ 14\)
  • \(4x - 6 = 22\)

ANSWER

  • equation
  • expression
  • expression
  • equation

For questions #5-8, choose the algebraic expression that represents the English phrase

  1. the difference of \(14x\) ² and 13

Answer: \(14x^2-13\)

  1. the quotient of \(12x\) and 2

Answer: \(\frac{12x}2\)

  1. the sum of \(17y^2\) and 19

Answer: \(17y^2+19\)

  1. the product of 7 and \(y\)

Answer: \(7y\)

For questions #10-11, choose the algebraic expression that represents the scenario.

  1. Eric has rock and classical CDs in his car. The number of rock CDs is 3 more than the number of classical CDs. Let \(c\) represent the number of classical CDs. Write an expression for the number of rock CDs.

Answer: \(c + 3\)

  1. Greg has nickels and pennies in his pocket. The number of pennies is seven less than twice the number of nickels. Let \(n\) represent the number of nickels. Write an expression for the number of pennies.

Answer: \(2n - 7\)
