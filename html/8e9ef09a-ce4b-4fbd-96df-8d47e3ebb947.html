  1. A new bicycle sells for $300. It is on sale for \(\frac14\) off the regular price. Select all the expressions that represent the sale price of the bicycle in dollars.
    1. \(300 \cdot \frac14\)
    2. \(300 \cdot \frac34\)
    3. \(300 \cdot (1-\frac{1}{4})\)
    4. \(300 -\frac14\)
    5. \(300-\frac{1}{4} \cdot 300\)

ANSWER: b. \(300 \cdot \frac{3}{4}\), c. \(300 \cdot (1-\frac{1}{4})\), e. \(300-\frac{1}{4} \cdot 300\)

  1. A piece of paper is folded into thirds multiple times. The area, \(A\), of the piece of paper in square inches, after \(n\) folds, is \(A=90 \cdot (\frac{1}{3})^n\).

    1. What is the value of \(A\) when \(n=0\)? What does this mean in this situation?

ANSWER: 90. That is the area of the paper in square inches before it is folded.

    1. How many folds are needed before the area is less than 1 square inch?

ANSWER: 5

  1. The area of another piece of paper in square inches, after \(n\) folds, is given by \(A=100 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^n\). What does the number 100 mean in this situation?
    1. The area after the paper is folded
    2. The area before the paper is folded
    3. The number of folds to have an area of 0
    4. The growth factor of the situation

ANSWER: b. The area before the paper is folded
