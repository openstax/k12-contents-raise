Activity (20 minutes)

This activity has students creating tables of values from verbal descriptions. Students will then work to use the tables of values to write linear equations. To see the connection to linear equations, students will write their own scenarios and then have a partner create a representative table of values. They will then plot the coordinates from the tables and see the linear pattern.

Launch

Ask students to think about a job they or a friend has ever had. How much did they get paid after 1 hour? Two hours? Three hours? What if they earned a $1 raise? Then what would they receive after 1 hour? Two hours? Three hours? What do you notice?

As students work, be sure to circulate and prompt students to work through the situations to create the next values in the table.

Anticipated Misconceptions

When writing an equation from a table, students often switch the slope and \(y\)-intercept in the slope-intercept form of an equation. Continue to emphasize the \(y\)-intercept is the starting value when \(x=0\) and the slope is the rate of change.

Activity Synthesis

Have pairs of students share their situations and matching tables of values. Discuss what shape the graphs are all taking and why. As students plot their points, they should recognize that a linear pattern forms. Help students connect the linear pattern to there being the same slope between points.


Support for English Language Learners:

As they write their situations, monitor ELL students to ensure that they learn relationships between sounds and letters of the English language to represent sounds when writing in English.

As they share and discuss with partners, have ELL students practice producing sounds of newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters to pronounce English words in a manner that is increasingly comprehensible in vocabulary words such as slope and intercept.

During collaboration with partners, monitor ELL students to ensure that they are distinguishing sounds and intonation patterns of English with increasing ease. They should recognize elements of the English sound system in newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters. Additionally, they need to learn relationships between sounds and letters of the English language and decode (sound out) words using a combination of skills such as recognizing sound-letter relationships and identifying cognates, affixes, roots, and base words in words such as linear, graphing, and plotting


1.13.2: Self Check

After the activity, students will answer the following question to check their understanding of the concepts explored in the activity.

QUESTION:

Which of the following equations could be represented by this table of values?

\(x\) 0 1 2 3
\(y\) 20 23 26 29

ANSWERS FEEDBACK
\(y = 20x\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: The \(y\)-intercept is 20, not the slope. The answer is \(y = 3x +20\).
\(y = \frac{1}{3}x + 20\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: The slope is found by the change in \(y\) over the change in \(x\). The answer is \(y = 3x +20\).
\(y = 3x +20\) That’s correct! Check yourself: The \(y\)-intercept, when \(x = 0\), is at 20. The slope, or change in \(y\) over change in \(x\), is \(\frac31\) or 3.
\(y =20x +3\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This rule does not work for each column in the table. The answer is \(y = 3x +20\)

Writing Linear Equations from Tables

When writing linear equations from tables, it is important to identify the slope and the \(y\)-intercepts from the table.

Example

Given the table, write an equation in slope-intercept form.

\(x\) 0 1 2 3 4
\(y\) 12 16 20 24 28

Step 1 - Identify the \(y\)-intercept.

The \(y\)-intercept is the starting value, when \(x=0\).
Looking at the table, when \(x = 0\), the \(y\)-value is 12, so this is the \(y\)-intercept.

Step 2 - Identify the slope.

Slope is the change in \(y\) over the change in \(x\).
The \(y\)-values change by 4 each time. The \(x\)-values change by 1 each time.

The slope is \(\frac41\) or 4.

Step 3 - Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

Slope intercept form is \(y = mx +b\), where \(m\) is the slope and \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept.
Substitute the values, \(y = 4x +12\).
