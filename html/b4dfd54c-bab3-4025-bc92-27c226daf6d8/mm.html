Warm Up Activity

A big misconception about learning is that people should get everything right on the first try. If you answer correctly on the first try, think about how you might challenge yourself further.

These expressions each define the same function.

  • \(x^2+6x+8\)
  • \((x+2)(x+4)\)
  • \((x+3)^2−1\)

Without graphing or doing any calculations, determine where the following features would be on a graph that represents the function.

  1. The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex (of a graph).

Enter the \(x\)-coordinate.

Correct! The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is -3.

The correct answer is -3. The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is -3.


  1. The \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex (of a graph).

Enter the \(y\)-coordinate.

Correct! The \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex is -1.

The correct answer is -1. The \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex is -1.


----------------------------------------
  1. Select both \(x\)-intercepts.

Correct! The \(x\)-intercepts are \((-2,0)\) and \((-4,0)\).

The correct answers are: \((-2,0)\), \((-4,0)\). The \(x\)-intercepts are \((-2,0)\) and \((-4,0)\).

------------------------------------

  1. The \(y\)-coordinate of the \(y\)-intercept \((0, \_\_\_\_\_)\)

Enter the \(y\)-coordinate of the \(y\)-intercept.

Correct! The \(y\)-intercept is \((0,8)\).

The correct answer is 8. The \(y\)-intercept is \((0,8)\).
