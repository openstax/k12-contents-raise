Part 2: Use Synthetic Division

You are now going to solve the same division problem using synthetic division.

2. \((x^2-13x+42) \div (x-6)\)

Write down your answer, then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

Solution:

\((x^2-13x+42) \div (x-6)\)

Step 1
Write the dividend with decreasing powers of \(x\). Make sure there is no degree missing from the highest degree down to a degree of \(0\).
\(x^2-13x+42\)

Step 2
Write the coefficients of the terms as the first row of the synthetic division.

Step 3
Write the divisor as \(x - c\) and place \(c\) in the synthetic division in the divisor box.

Step 4
Bring down the first coefficient to the third row.

Step 5
Multiply that coefficient by the divisor and place the result in the second row under the second coefficient.

Step 6
Add the second column, putting the result in the third row.

Step 7
Multiply that result by the divisor and place the result in the second row under the third coefficient.

Step 8
Add the final column, putting the result in the third row.

Step 9
The division is complete. The numbers in the third row give us the result. The \(1\) \( −7\) in the third row are the coefficients of the quotient. The quotient is \(x - 7\). The \(0\) in the box in the third row is the remainder.
