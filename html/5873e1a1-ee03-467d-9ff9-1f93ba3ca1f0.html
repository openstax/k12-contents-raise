  1. For an experiment, a scientist designs a can, 20 cm in height, that holds water. A tube is installed at the bottom of the can allowing water to drain out.

At the beginning of the experiment, the can is full. Every minute after the start of the experiment, \(\frac23\) of the water is drained.

The height of the water, \(h\), in cm, is a function \(f\) of time \(t\) in minutes since the beginning of the experiment, \(h=f(t)\). Which is an expression for \(f(t)\)?

    • \( f(t)=20+ \frac{1}{3}t\)
    • \( f(t)=20(\frac{1}{3})^t\)
    • \(f(t)=20(\frac{2}{3})^t\)
    • \(f(t)=20+\frac{2}{3}t\)

    Answer: \( f(t)=20(\frac{1}{3})^t\)

    1. A bacteria population is 10,000. It triples each day. The bacteria population, \(b\), is a function of the number of days, \(d\). Which equation relates \(b\) and \(d\)?

    Use this equation for problems 7â€“10: \(f(t)=50 \cdot 2^t\)

    1. What is the initial value?

    Answer: The initial value is 50. When \(t=0\), \(f(t)=50 \cdot 2^0=f(t)=(50)(1)=50\).

    1. What is the growth factor?

    Answer: The initial value is 2. The growth factor refers to the constant multiplier that determines how the function grows or decays as the independent variable changes. In this equation, the growth factor is 2.

    1. What is the independent variable?

    Answer: \(t\), The independent variable is the variable that is manipulated or controlled and is represented by \(t\) in this equation.

    1. What is the dependent variable?

    Answer: \(f(t)\), The dependent variable is the variable that depends on or is influenced by the independent variable. In this equation, the dependent variable is \(f(t)\), which represents the output or value of the function.
