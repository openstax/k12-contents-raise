In questions 1–5, find an equation of a line that is parallel to the given line and contains the given point. Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

  1. Line \(y = 4x + 2\)
    Point \((1, 2)\)
  • \(y=4x+2\)
  • \(y=4x-2\)
  • \(y=-¼ x-2\)
  • \(y=- ¼ x +2\)

Answer: \(y = 4x -2\)

The slope of a line parallel to \(y = 4x + 2\) is 4. The line contains the point (1,2).

\(\begin{aligned}y − y_1 &= m(x − x_1)\\ y − (2) &= 4(x − (1))\\ y − 2 &= 4(x − 1)\\ y − 2 &= 4x − 4\\ y &= 4x − 2 \end{aligned}\)

TThe slope of a line perpendicular to \(y = −2x + 3\) is \(\frac12\). The line contains the point (2,2).
