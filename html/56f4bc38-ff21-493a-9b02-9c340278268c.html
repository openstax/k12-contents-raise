The \(x\)-intercepts are where the \(y\)-values are 0.

This occurs at \((-3,0)\) and \((0,0)\).

The vertex occurs where the function will create a mirror image, or be symmetrical for all points. This often occurs halfway between the two zeros (when there are two zeros).

For this function, the vertex is at \((-1,-2)\).

Here is how to determine the vertex from the table:

The zeros are located at \((5, 0)\) and \((9, 0)\). The vertex will be halfway between the two zeros. This occurs at \(x = 7\).

The point in the table at \(x = 7\) is \((7, 8)\).
