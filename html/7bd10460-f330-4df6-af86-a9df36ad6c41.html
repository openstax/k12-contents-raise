Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. The height of water in a bathtub, \(w\), is a function of time, \(t\). Let \(P\) represent this function. Height is measured in inches and time in minutes.
    1. \(P(0) = 0\)

Answer: The bathtub starts out with no water.

    1. \(P(4) = 10\)

Answer: The height of the water is 10 inches after 4 minutes.

    1. \(P(10) = 4\)

Answer: After 10 minutes, the height of the water is 4 inches.

    1. \(P(20) = 0\)

Answer: After 20 minutes, the bathtub is empty.

  1. Function \(C\) takes time for its input and gives a student’s Monday class for its output. Use function notation to represent: A student has English at 10:00.

Answer: \(C\) (10:00) = English

  1. Function \(f\) gives the distance of a dog from a post, in feet, as a function of time, in seconds, since its owner left.
    1. Find the value of \(f(20)\).

ANSWER: \(f(20)=3.1\)

  1. Find the value of \(f(140)\).

Answer: \(f(140)=3.5\)

  1. Looking at the graph below, what does the point \((1980, 80)\) mean?

Answer: In 1980, the world population increased by 80 million people.

  1. Function \(R\) gives the number of inches of rain after \(h\) hours. What does the statement \(R(6)=5\) mean?

Answer: After 6 hours, there was 5 inches of rain.

  1. Function \(R\) gives the number of inches, \(T\), of rain after \(h\) hours. Choose which function notation represents this situation.
  • \(R=T(h)\)
  • \(h=R(T)\)
  • \(h=T(R)\)
  • \(T=R(h)\)

Answer: \(T=R(h)\)

  1. Use function notation to represent that the number of miles, \(m\), a car travels as a function of time, \(t\).
  • \(f=m(t)\)
  • \(m=f(t)\)
  • \(t=m(f)\)
  • \(t=f(m)\)

Answer: \(m=f(t)\)
