Lesson Narrative

In this lesson, students recall how to calculate the slope between two points when given two coordinates or a table, using the slope formula; or with a graph, using knowledge of rise over run. Students will then use this knowledge to write equations of lines in slope-intercept form, point-slope form, and standard form. Equations will be written from two points or one point and a slope. Students practiced finding slope from a graph in 1.10 but have not formally found the slope when given two coordinates or from a table. Also in 1.10, students explored writing and graphing equations using standard form. In 1.11.3, students had the opportunity to write equations from standard form to slope-intercept form by solving for \(y\) to identify \(m\) and \(b\). They will still need practice converting equations into all three different, equivalent forms.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Write the slope of a line given two points from coordinates, a table, or a graph.
  • Write equations in slope-intercept form, standard form, and point-slope form.
  • Write equations given two points or one point and the slope of a line.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Write the slope of a line.
  • Write the equation of a line in different forms.
