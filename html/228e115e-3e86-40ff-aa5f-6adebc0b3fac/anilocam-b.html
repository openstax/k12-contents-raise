The Department of Streets of a city has a budget of $1,962,800 for resurfacing roads and hiring additional workers this year.

The cost of resurfacing a mile of 2-lane road is estimated at $84,000. The average starting salary of a worker in the department is $36,000 a year.



  1. Write an equation that represents the relationship between the miles of 2-lane roads the department could resurface, \( m \), and the number of new workers it could hire, \( p \), if it spends the entire budget.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\( 84,000m + 36,000p = 1,962,800 \)


  1. Use the equation you wrote in the first question to solve for \(p\).

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(p=\frac{1,962,800-84,000m}{36,000}\)
