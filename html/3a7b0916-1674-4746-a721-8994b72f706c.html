Galileo Galilei, an Italian scientist, and other medieval scholars studied the motion of free-falling objects. The law they discovered can be expressed by the equation \(d=16t^2\), which gives the distance fallen in feet as a function of time, \(t\), in seconds.

An object is dropped from a height of 576 feet.

  1. How many feet does it fall in 0.5 seconds?

Enter your answer here:

Correct! It falls 4 feet in 0.5 seconds because \(16 (0.5)^2=4\).
