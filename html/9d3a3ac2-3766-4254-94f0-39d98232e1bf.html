  1. Mike owns a grass cutting business. The table below shows how many yards, \(Y\), he can cut in \(d\) days.
Number of days, d 1 2 3 4
Number of yards cut, Y 6 12 18 24

Which of the following equations is a linear model representing the number of yards, \(Y\), Mike can cut in \(d\) days.

  • \( Y = 6d \)
  • \( Y=6d + 6 \)
  • \( Y= \frac16 d\) 
  • \( Y= \frac16 d +6 \)

Answer: \(Y =6d\)

Since the change in \(y\) is 6 and change in \(x\) is 1, the slope is 6. Working backwards, when \(d = 0\), \(Y=0\) so the equation becomes \(Y=6x+0\) or \(Y = 6d\).
