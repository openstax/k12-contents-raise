Write Equations Given a Slope and \( y \)-Intercept

We can determine the slope and intercept of a line if the equation is written in slope-intercept form, \( y=mx+b \). Now we will do the reverse—we will start with the slope and \( y \)-intercept and use them to find the equation of the line.

Example: 

Find the equation of a line with slope \( −1 \) and \( y \)-intercept \( (0,−3) \).

Solution: 

Name the slope.

\( m = -1 \)

Name the \( y \)-intercept.

\( b = -3 \)

Substitute the values into \( y = mx + b \).

\( y = -1x + -3 \) 
\( y = -x-3 \)


The same information can be picked up from a graph of a line. 

Example: 

Find the equation of the line shown in the graph.

 Solution:

Find the slope by counting the rise and run.

\( m=\frac{rise}{run} \) 

From \( (0, -4) \) to \( (3,-2) \) the slope is \

\(m = \frac{2}{3} \).

Find the \( y \)-intercept.

\( y \)-intercept \( (0, -4) \)
\( b = -4 \)

Substitute the values into \( y = mx + b \).

\( y = mx +b \)
\( y = \frac{2}{3} x - 4 \)
