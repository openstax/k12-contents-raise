In questions 1-2, find the equation of a line with given slope and \( y \)-intercept. Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

  1. slope 3 and \( y \)-intercept \( ( 0,5) \)
  • \(y=5x+3\)
  • \(y=3x+0\)
  • \(y=3x+5\)
  • \(y=0x+3\)

Answer: \( y=3x+5 \)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}y-y_1&=&m(x-x_1)\\y-(5)&=&3(x-(0))\\y-5&=&3(x)\\y-5&=&3x\\y&=&3x+5\\&&\end{array}\)

  1. slope \(−3 \) and \( y \)-intercept \( (0,−1)\)
  • \(y=-x-3\)
  • \(y=-3x-1\)
  • \(y=-x+0\)
  • \(y=0x-3\)

Answer: \( y=-3x-1 \)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}y-y_1&=&m(x-x_1)\\y-(-1)&=&-3(x-(0))\\y+1&=&-3(x)\\y+1&=&-3x\\y&=&-3x-1\end{array}\)
