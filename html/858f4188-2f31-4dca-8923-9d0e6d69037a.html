The scatter plot shows the sale price of several food items, \(y\), and the cost of the ingredients used to produce those items, \(x\), as well as a line that models the data. The line is also represented by the equation \(y = 3.48x + 0.76\).

  1. What is the predicted sale price of an item that has ingredients that cost $1.50? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

$5.98 from the equation \((3.48⋅1.5+0.76=5.98)\), or approximately $6 from the scatter plot graph



  1. What is the predicted ingredient cost of an item that has a sale price of $7? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

$1.79 from the equation \(\left(\frac{7-0.76}{3.48}\;=\;1.79\right)\), or approximately $1.75 from the scatter plot graph



  1. What is the slope of the linear model? What does that mean in this situation?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

3.48. This means that for every extra dollar spent on ingredients, the sale price of the food item is expected to increase by about $3.48.



  1. What is the \(y\)-intercept of the linear model? What does this mean in this situation? Does this make sense?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

0.76. This means that a food item that is made with free ingredients would still be sold for approximately 76 cents. Compare your answer:

  • This doesn’t seem to make sense because something that is free to make should not cost anything for the customer.
  • This might make sense since it would still cost some money to either run the machines or pay the people to make the item.
