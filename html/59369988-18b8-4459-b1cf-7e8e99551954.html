The equations \(y=x^2+6x+8\) and \(y=(x+2)(x+4)\) both define the same quadratic function. 


  1. Without graphing, identify the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph. Explain how you know.

Compare your answer:

\((-2,0)\),\((-4,0)\); The constants in the factors of an equation in factored form give a clue about where the \(x\)-intercepts are when the equation is graphed. In the equation \(y=(x+2)(x+4)\), the \(x\)-intercepts are \((-2,0)\) and \((-4,0)\).


  1. Without graphing, identify the \(y\)-value of the \(y\)-intercept of the graph. Explain your reasoning.

8; The \(y\)-intercepts of the graph can be seen when the equation is in standard form. The \(y\)-coordinate is the number without the variable. In the equation \(y=x^2+6x+8\), it is the 8 so the \(y\)-intercept is \((0,8)\).
