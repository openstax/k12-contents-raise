Activity 

A teacher purchased 20 calculators and 10 measuring tapes for her class and paid $495. Later, she realized that she didn’t order enough supplies. She placed another order of 8 of the same calculator and 1 of the same measuring tape and paid $178.50. 


This system represents the constraints in this situation:

\( \left\{ \begin{array}{c l} 20c + 10m = 495\\ 8c + m = 178.50 \end{array}\right. \)

1. Discuss with a partner:

a. In this situation, what do the solutions to the first equation mean?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The solutions to \(20c + 10m = 495\) are all possible combinations of unit prices for the two items that make a total of $495 when buying 20 calculators and 10 measuring tapes.

b. In this situation, what do the solutions to the second equation mean?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The solutions to \(8c + m = 178.50\) are all possible combinations of unit prices for the two items that make a total of $178.50 when buying 8 calculators and 1 measuring tape.

c. For each equation, how many possible solutions are there? Explain how you know.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

Many possible solutions. For example, in the first equation, \(c = 20\) and \(m = 9.50\) would make the equation true, as would \(c = 15\) and \(m = 19.50\).

d. In this situation, what does the solution to the system mean?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The price for a calculator and the price for a measuring tape that would make both equations true (or satisfy both constraints).

2. Find the solution to the system. Explain or show your reasoning.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(c = 21.50\) and \(m = 6.50\). (You may solve by graphing or by substitution.)

3. To be reimbursed for the cost of the supplies, the teacher recorded: “Items purchased: 28 calculators and 11 measuring tapes. Amount: $673.50.”

a. Write an equation to represent the relationship between the numbers of calculators and measuring tapes, the prices of those supplies, and the total amount spent.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(28c + 11m = 673.50\)

b. How is this equation related to the first two equations?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

It is the sum of the first two equations.

c. In this situation, what do the solutions of this equation mean?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

Sample response: The solutions are all possible combinations of prices for a calculator and for a measuring tape that make a total of $673.50 when 28 calculators and 11 measuring tapes are bought.

d. How many possible solutions does this equation have? How many solutions make sense in this situation? Explain your reasoning.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

Sample response: It has many possible solutions, but only one solution makes sense. The prices of a calculator and of a measuring tape in this equation are the same as those in the other two equations. The new equation simply shows the total numbers of supplies and the total amount paid.
