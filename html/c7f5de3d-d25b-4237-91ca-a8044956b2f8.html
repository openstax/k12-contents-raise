Lesson Narrative 

This lesson begins with reviewing how to identify the vertex of a quadratic from the vertex form. The lesson also provides students with the opportunity to practice writing quadratic functions in vertex form, standard form, and factored form. When working to rewrite the equations through the different forms, students will strengthen their skills in the distributive property, completing the square, and using inverse operations.

In the cool down of the lesson, students will connect transformations of the quadratic parent function with the vertex and standard forms.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Identify the vertex of a quadratic function when it is in vertex form.
  • Write quadratics in equivalent vertex, factored, and standard forms using completing the square, distributive property, and inverse operations.
  • Write quadratic functions in vertex form given a vertex and another point.

Learning Targets (Student Facing) 

  • Identify the vertex of a quadratic function from an equation.
  • Write quadratics in vertex, factored, and standard forms.
  • Write quadratic functions in vertex form given a vertex and another point.
