Cool Down (10 minutes)

Activity

With a partner, use what you have learned to write an equation in each form from two points.

  1. Find the slope of the line that goes through \((2,3)\) and \((6,-4)\).

Answer: \(-\frac74\)

\(m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}=\frac{-4-3}{6-2}=-\frac74\)

  1. Write the equation of the line in point-slope form that goes through \((2,3) \)and \((6,-4)\).

Answer: \(y-3=-\frac{7}{4}(x-2)\) or \(y+4=-\frac{7}{4}(x-6)\)

Using the point \((2,3)\):

\(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\)

\(y-3=-\frac{7}{4}(x-2)\)

Using the point \((6, -4)\):

\(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\)

\(y-(-4)=-\frac{7}{4}(x-6)\)

\(y+4=-\frac{7}{4}(x-6)\)

  1. Write the equation of the line in slope-intercept form that goes through \((2,3)\) and \((6,-4)\). (Hint: use the equation from number 2)

Answer: \(y=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{13}{2}\)

\(y-3=-\frac{7}{4}(x-2)\)

\(y-3=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{14}{4}\)

\(y-3=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac72\)

\(y-3+3=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{7}{2}+3\)

\(y=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{7}{2}+\frac{6}{2}\)

\(y=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{13}{2}\)

  1. Write the equation of the line in standard form that goes through \((2,3)\) and \((6,-4)\).

(Hint: use the equation from number 3.)

Answer: \(7x+4y=26\)

\(4(y=-\frac{7}{4}x+\frac{13}{2})\)

\(4y=-7x+26\)

\(4y+7x=-7x+7x+26\)

\(7x+4y=26\)
