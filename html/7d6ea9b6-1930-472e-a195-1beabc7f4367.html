Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Functions \(R\) and \(D\) give the height, in feet, of a toy rocket and a drone, \(t\) seconds after they are released.

Here are the graphs of \(R\) (for the rocket) and \(D\) (for the drone).

Decide whether the following statements are true or false.

  1. \(R(3)>D(3)\)

Answer: True

  1. \(R(0)=D(0)\)

Answer: False

  1. \(R(4.5)=D(4.5)\)

Answer: True

  1. \(R(0)=D(0)+25\)

Answer: True

  1. \(D(7) > R(5)\)

Answer: False

  1. \(A(t)\) is the average high temperature in Aspen, Colorado, \(t\) months after the start of the year. \(M(t)\) is the average high temperature in Minneapolis, Minnesota, \(t\) months after the start of the year. Temperature is measured in degrees Fahrenheit. Which function had the higher average rate of change between the beginning of January and middle of March?

Answer: Minneapolis

  1. Here are two graphs representing functions \(f\) and \(g\).

Select all statements that are true about functions \(f\) and \(g\).

  1. \(f(0)>g(0)\)
  2. There are values of \(x\) where \(f(x)=g(x)\).
  3. \(f(-1)>g(-1)\)
  4. \(f(-3)>g(4)\)

Answer: There are values of \(x\) where \(f(x)=g(x)\), \(f(-1)>g(-1)\), \(f(-3)>g(4)\)
