Lesson Narrative


This lesson provides students an introduction to factoring expressions and polynomials. Students will master finding the greatest common factor (GCF) of two or more expressions by factoring the expressions into their prime factors and finding all their common factors. Finding the GCF of expressions is an essential tool used in factoring. Factoring the GCF from a polynomial is often the first step in factoring. It is important to simplify in this way before trying other ways to factor polynomials.

Students will learn to factor a polynomial by grouping in this lesson. They will use the “reverse” of the Distributive Property, and more complicated ways of factoring by grouping will be taught in later lessons. Finally, students will start to recognize when a polynomial is prime and cannot be factored any further.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Factor an expression into all of its prime factors.
  • Find the GCF of two or more expressions by factoring each expression into its prime factors and multiplying all of the common factors shared by each expression.
  • Factor the greatest common factor from a polynomial by using the “reverse” of the Distributive Property.
  • Use a grouping method to factor a polynomial when the terms of that polynomial do not share a common factor.
  • Recognize when a polynomial cannot be factored using grouping.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Find the greatest common factor of two or more expressions.
  • Factor the greatest common factor from a polynomial.
  • Factor by grouping.
