Student Activity

For questions 1–3, what could be added to each expression to make it a perfect square?

  1. \(x^2+12x\)

Answer: 36
The constant term \(c=36\) can be added to make the perfect square \(x^2+12x+36\).

  1. \(x^2−6x+1\)

Answer: 8
8 can be added to the constant term \(c=1\) to make the perfect square \(x^2-6x+9\).

  1. \(x^2+14x−10\)

Answer: 59
59 can be added to the constant term \(c=-10\) to make the perfect square \(x^2+14x+49\).

  1. Solve the equation \(x^2−16x=-60\) by completing the square. Show your reasoning.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer:
\(\begin{aligned} x^{2}-16 x &=-60 \\ x^{2}-16 x+64 &=-60+64 \\ x^{2}-16 x+64 &=4 \\ (x-8)^{2} &=4 \\ \sqrt{(x-8)^{2}} &=\sqrt{4} \\ x-8 &=\pm 2 \\ x-8 &=2 \\ x-8 &=-2 \\ \\ x=6 & \text { or } x=10 \end{aligned}\)
