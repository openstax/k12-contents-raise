Distributive Property

If \(a\), \(b\), \(c\) are real numbers, then:

\(a(b+c)=ab+ac\)

Other forms:

\(a(b-c)=ab-ac\)

\((b+c)a=ba+ca\)


Example 1

Simplify \(3(x + 4)\)

Step 1 - Distribute the 3 to both the \(x\) and the 4.

Step 2 - Multiply the terms.
\(3(x) + 3(4)\)

Step 3 - Simplify.
\(3x + 12\)

Example 2

Simplify \(m(7 - 2m)\)

Step 1 - Distribute the \(m\) to both the 7 and the \(2m\).

Step 2 - Multiply the terms. Recall \(m \cdot m=m^2\).
\(m(7) - m(2m)\)

Step 3 - Simplify.
\(7m-2m^2\)

Example 3

Simplify \(5x(x-\frac{2}{5})\)

Step 1 - Distribute the \(5x\) to both the \(x\) and the \(\frac25\).

Step 2 - Multiply the terms. Recall \(x \cdot x=x^2\).
\(5x(x)-5x(\frac{2}{5})\)

Step 3 - Simplify.
\(5x^2-2x\)
