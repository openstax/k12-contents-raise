Cool Down Activity

Everyone starts from a different place. Focus on growing your own skills through these lessons and not on how others are doing.

Use what you have learned in the lesson to solve the following quadratic equations.


  1. \(5m^2= 80\)

Enter the solution to the equation.

Compare your answer:

\(m=4\) and \(m=-4\)

\(5m^2=80\)

\(\frac{5m^2}{5}=\frac{80}{5}\)

\(m^2=16\)

\(m=\pm \sqrt{16}\)

\(m=4\) and \(m=-4\)


  1. \((m−6)^2=100\)

Enter the solution to the equation.

Compare your answer:

\(m=16\) and \(m=-4\)

\((m−6)^2=100\)

\((m−6)=\pm \sqrt{100}\)

\(m−6=10\;\;\;\;m−6=-10\)

\(m =16\) and \(m =-4\)


  1. \(\frac{2}{3}y^2−8=−2\)

Enter the solution to the equation.

Compare your answer:

\(y=3\) and \(y=-3\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\frac23y^2-8&=&-2\\\frac23y^2&=&6\\\frac32\left(\frac23y^2\right)&=&\frac32\left(6\right)\\y^2&=&9\\y&=&\pm\sqrt9\end{array}\)
\(\;\;\;\;\;\;y=3\) and \(y=-3\)


  1. \(\frac{1}{6}(x+3)^2−8=16\)

Enter the solution to the equation.

Compare your answer:

\(x=9\) and \(x=-15\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\frac16\left(x+3\right)^2-8&=&16\\\frac16\left(x+3\right)^2&=&24\\\left(x+3\right)^2&=&144\\\left(x+3\right)&=&\pm\sqrt{144}\end{array}\)
\(\;\;\;\;\;\;\;x+3=12\;\;\;\;\;\;x+3=-12\)
\(\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;x=9\) and \(x=-15\)
