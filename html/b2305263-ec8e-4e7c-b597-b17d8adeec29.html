7. Now, write an equation of a line that is parallel to \( y = \frac{1}{3}x -2 \) that goes through \( (9, 2) \).

Enter your answer here:

Check your answer:

\( y=\frac{1}{3}x-1 \) is an equation that is parallel to \( y = \frac{1}{3}x -2 \) and goes through \( (9, 2) \).

First, identify the slope of the line: 

\( m =\frac{1}{3} \)

Next, use the slope and given point \( (9, 2) \) in point-slope form to find the parallel line that goes through the point:

\( y-2=\frac{1}{3}(x-9) \)

Now put the equation in slope-intercept form (solve for \( y \)).

\( y=\frac{1}{3}x-1 \)
