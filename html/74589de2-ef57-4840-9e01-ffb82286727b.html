Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

At a fabric store, fabrics are sold by the yard. A dressmaker spent $36.35 on 4.25 yards of silk and cotton fabrics for a dress. Silk is $16.90 per yard, and cotton is $4 per yard.

Here is a system of equations that represents the constraints in the situation.

\(\displaystyle \begin{cases} \begin {align} x + \hspace{2.2mm} y &= \hspace{2mm} 4.25\\ 16.90x + 4y &= 36.35 \end{align} \end{cases} \)

  1. What does the solution to the system represent?

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample. The solution represents the combination of lengths of silk and cotton (in yards) that meet both constraints: they add up to 4.25 yards, and the cost of buying both is $36.35.

  1. Find the solution to the system of equations. 

Answer: 1.5 yards of silk and 2.75 yards of cotton.

  1. Explain or show how you know.

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample.

  • The graphs of the equations intersect at 
  • \((1.5,2.75)\).
  • Solving by using a table gives \(x=1.5\) and \(y=2.75\).
