Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

The function \(f\) is given by \(f(x)=(x-2)(x+4)\). 

Without using graphing technology, answer questions 1–4.

  1. What are the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph representing \(f\)?

    Answer: \(x\)-intercepts for function \(f\): \((2,0)\) and \((-4,0)\)
  1. What is the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph?

    Answer: -1
  1. What is the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph?

    Answer: -9
  1. What is the \(y\)-intercept?

    Answer: The \(y\)-intercept is \((0,-8)\), because \((0-2)(0+4)=(-2)(4)=-8\).
  1. Use the graphing tool or technology outside the course. Sketch a graph that represents \(f\). 

(Students were provided access to Desmos.)

Answer: The graph opens upward with vertex at \((-1,-9)\) and \(x\)-intercepts at \((2,0)\) and \((-4, 0)\).

Response to Student Thinking

Points to Emphasize

If students struggle to identify the \(x\)- and \(y\)-intercepts, use the Card Sort in Lesson 7.12 to highlight key ideas based on student work in the cool down.
