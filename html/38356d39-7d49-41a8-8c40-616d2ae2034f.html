Activity (5 minutes)

The activities in this lesson center on polynomials and rectangles. This warm up activates students’ knowledge of multiplying to find areas of rectangles with variable side lengths and using the Distributive Property.

Launch

  1. Arrange students in groups of 2.
  2. Give students time to complete the table.
  3. Follow with a brief whole-class discussion.

Student Activity

Complete the table with the length, width, and area of each rectangle. Two dimensions are entered in the table.

B  \(2\)

Student Response
Rectangle Length (units) Width (units) Area (square units)
A \(a + 4\) \(5\) \(5a + 20\)
B \(2t + 5\) \(2\) \(4t + 10\)
C \(\frac32\) \(m\) \(\frac32m\)
D \(v + 3\) \(v\) \(v^2 + 3v\)
E \(3a\) \(4\) \(12a\)

Activity Synthesis

Invite students to share their answers and the methods they used to complete the table. For each length, students must add and combine like terms when possible. To find the area for rectangles A, B, and D, they used the Distributive Property. To find the area for rectangle D, they used the Properties of Exponents.
