  1. Mai and Jada are solving the equation \(2x^2−7x=15\) using the quadratic formula.

Answer the following questions about the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) if this equation is written in standard form, \(ax^2+bx+c=0\).

  1. What is the value of \(a\)?

Answer: 2

  1. What is the value of \(b\)?

Answer: -7

  1. What is the value of \(c\)?

Answer: -15

  1. Mai and Jada are solving the equation \(2x^2−7x=15\) using the quadratic formula but found different solutions.

    2. Mai wrote:

    \(\begin{array}{l} x=\frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{(7)^{2}-4(2)(-15)}}{2(2)} \\ x=\frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{49-(-120)}}{4} \\ x=\frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{169}}{4} \\ x=\frac{-7 \pm 13}{4} \\ x=\frac{-7+13}{4}=\frac{6}{4}=\frac{3}{2} \\ x=\frac{-7-13}{4}=\frac{-20}{4}=-5 \end{array}\)

    Jada wrote:

    \(\begin{array}{l} x=\frac{-(-7) \pm \sqrt{(-7)^{2}-4(2)(-15)}}{2(2)} \\ x=\frac{7 \pm \sqrt{-49-(-120)}}{4} \\ x=\frac{7 \pm \sqrt{71}}{4} \end{array}\)

    Who has the correct solution?

  • Mai
  • Jada
  • Both of them
  • Neither one

Answer: Neither one
Mai used 7 for \(b\) instead of -7. For \(b^2\) in the formula, Jada computed \(-7^2\) instead of \((-7)^2\), so she wrote -49 instead of 49.

  1. The equation \(h(t)=-16t^2+80t+64\) represents the height, in feet, of a potato \(t\) seconds after it was launched from a mechanical device.
  1. Choose the equation that would allow us to find the time the potato hits the ground.
  • \(0=16t^2+80t+64\)
  • \(0=-t^2-5t+4\)
  • \(0=-16t^2+80t+64\)
  • \(0= t^2-5t-4\)

Answer: \(0=-16t^2+80t+64\)

  1. Solve the equation without graphing and write it down. Enter your solution for the approximate time the potato hits the ground rounded to one decimal place.

Answer: 5.7

\(t \approx 5.7\). Using the quadratic formula, with \(a=-16\), \(b=80\), and \(c=64\), the potato hits the ground about 5.7 seconds after being launched. (The negative solution has no meaning in this situation.)

  1. A frog jumps in the air. The height, in inches, of the frog is modeled by the function \(h(t)=60t−75t^2\), where \(t\) is the time after it jumped, measured in seconds.
  1. Select two solutions to the equation \(60t−75t^2=0\).
  • 0
  • \(\frac{4}{5}\)
  • 5
  • 1
  • \(-\frac {1}{5}\)

Answer: 0, \(\frac{4}{5}\)

\(t=0\), \(t=\frac{4}{5}\)

  1. What do the solutions tell us about the jumping frog? Select two that apply.
  • The frog landed 1 seconds after it jumped and was on the ground again.
  • The frog landed \(\frac{4}{5}\) seconds after it jumped and was on the ground again.
  • The height of the frog was 5 inches before it jumped.
  • The negative solution shows how far down the frog was before it jumped.
  • The height of the frog was 0 inches before it jumped.

Answer: The frog landed \(\frac{4}{5}\) seconds after it jumped and was on the ground again. The height of the frog was 0 inches before it jumped.

  1. A tennis ball is hit straight up in the air, and its height, in feet above the ground, is modeled by the equation \(f(t)=4+12t−16t^2\), where \(t\) is measured in seconds since the ball was thrown.
  1. Select two solutions to the equation \(0=4+12t−16t^2\).
  • \(\frac{1}{4}\)
  • 4
  • 16
  • 1
  • \(-\frac{1}{4}\)

Answer: 1, \(-\frac{1}{4}\)

\(t=1\), \(t=-\frac{1}{4}\)

  1. What do the solutions tell us about the tennis ball? Select two that apply.
  • The ball hits the ground \(\frac{1}{4}\) second after it is hit.
  • The negative solution doesn't have meaning because a negative value for time doesn't make sense.
  • The ball hits the ground 1 second after it is hit.
  • The negative solution is a result of the bounce of the ball.
  • The ball hits the ground \(-\frac{1}{4}\) second after it is hit.
