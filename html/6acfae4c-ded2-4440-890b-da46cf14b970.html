The English Language Proficiency Standards (ELPS) Framework Section C includes the cross-curricular second language acquisition essential knowledge and skills standards. Five standards guide the integration of language acquisition into all content areas. The Texas Education Agency (TEA) requires that each standard and student's expectations be covered within the subject-level curriculum.

These standards focus on language acquisition. Listening, speaking, reading, and writing are the hallmarks of the skills needed to master the English language. Many student expectations can quickly and easily be integrated into a mathematics classroom, but some focus on skills we don’t often associate with math content.

Differentiation of Learning

The English Language Learner (ELL) students in your classroom will have varying levels of English language skills that are necessary to develop an understanding of mathematical language, knowledge, and skills. As a classroom teacher, you will have preliterate newcomers in need of developmental reading instruction in your math classroom. You will need to provide these newcomers the time to develop their English familiarity and skills. You can help this by building their vocabulary base before beginning math content instruction.

It’s important to have multiple levels and types of scaffolding to support learning new concepts. Teachers should use their professional judgment about which routines to use and when based on their knowledge of students' individual needs in their classroom.

Based on their observations of student language, teachers can make adjustments to their teaching and provide additional language support where necessary. When making decisions about how to support access for English learners, teachers should take into account the language demands of the specific activity and the language needed to engage the content more broadly, in relation to their students’ current ways of using language to communicate ideas as well as their students’ English language proficiency.

One way that you can differentiate learning is by providing students dual-language support for learning new vocabulary. Preliterate, beginner, and newcomer English language learners benefit from receiving mathematical language instruction in their home language before learning it in English. For this reason, we have created vocabulary supports in both English and Spanish. A dual lingual glossary is located in the Additional Resources for students in the Getting Started section. Quizlet vocabulary cards are located on the Lesson Overview pages in each Teacher Guide.

The OpenStax Algebra I curriculum provides opportunities for English language learners to develop an awareness of their own learning processes.

Within the Algebra I curriculum, look for the following opportunities to support ELPS 1: Learning Strategies.

  • Vocabulary: Explicitly teach unit and lesson-level vocabulary through different learning strategies connecting meaning to prior knowledge. (ELPS 1A, 1C, 1E)
  • Oral Presentation: Encourage students’ oral presentation of content knowledge and worked solutions. (ELPS 1D, 1E, 1F, 1G)
  • Assessment: Provide rubrics for assignments and assessments that include language learning strategies. (ELPS 1A through 1H)
  • Vocabulary: Incorporate into vocabulary definitions and explanations in a warm-up activity (ELPS 1A, 1C)
  • Speaking and writing: Provide accountable conversation opportunities for students to interact with peers and revisit work, self-correct language errors, and expand language. (ELPS 1B, 1E, 1F)
  • Speaking and writing: Provide accountable conversation opportunities for students to interact with peers using informal/formal language and expand both written and oral language. (ELPS 1G)
  • Speaking: Provide sentence stems or visual support within the classroom and opportunities for students to share and expand their language. (ELPS 1D, 1E, 1F)
  • Speaking and writing: Provide accountable conversation opportunities for students to interact with peers with a focus on patterns within language and expand both written and oral language. (ELPS 1H)
ELPS 1: Learning Strategies
The student is expected to:
(1A) use prior knowledge and experiences to understand meanings in English
(1B) monitor oral and written language production and employ self-corrective techniques or other resources
(1C) use strategic learning techniques such as concept mapping, drawing, memorizing, comparing, contrasting, and reviewing to acquire basic and grade-level vocabulary
(1D) speak using learning strategies such as requesting assistance, employing non-verbal cues, and using synonyms and circumlocution (conveying ideas by defining or describing when exact English words are not known)
(1E) internalize new basic and academic language by using and reusing it in meaningful ways in speaking and writing activities that build concept and language attainment
(1F) use accessible language and learn new and essential language in the process
(1G) demonstrate an increasing ability to distinguish between formal and informal English and an increasing knowledge of when to use each one commensurate with grade-level learning expectations
(1H) develop and expand repertoire of learning strategies such as reasoning inductively or deductively, looking for patterns in language, and analyzing sayings and expressions commensurate with grade-level learning expectations

The OpenStax Algebra I curriculum integrates opportunities for learners to listen to a variety of speakers to gain new content knowledge and comprehension.

Within the Algebra I curriculum, look for the following opportunities to support ELPS 2: Listening:

  • Vocabulary: Explicitly teach math vocabulary, including what the words sound like. (ELPS 2A through 2I)
  • Vocabulary: Incorporate teacher questions and provide sentence stems to students focusing on intonation sounds. Ask questions like, “Did I ask a question or make a statement?”, “How did you know?” (ELPS 2A)
  • Vocabulary: Incorporate a classroom word wall and word sorts into your lessons. Focus students on sounds within key vocabulary words and phrases. (ELPS 2B, 2C, 2E)
  • Listening Strategies: Encourage students to reflect on others’ oral presentation of content knowledge. (ELPS 2D, 2E)
  • Listening Strategies: Create instructional materials that provide support for understanding complex algebraic language and processes. (ELPS 2E)
  • Listening Strategies: Select a variety of digital media as support materials. (ELPS 2F)
  • Writing: Ask students to take notes from teacher demonstrations or videos to capture the general meaning, main points, and key details. Provide a summarization frame if additional support is needed. (ELPS 2F, 2G, 2H
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Provide opportunities for both teacher and peer interactions to repeat and rephrase key concepts of the lesson. (ELPS 2D)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Provide opportunities to respond to questions independently or within predetermined peer groups. Provide sentence stems to increase the complexity of shared language. (ELPS 2I)
ELPS 2: Listening
The student is expected to:
(2A) distinguish sounds and intonation patterns of English with increasing ease
(2B) recognize elements of the English sound system in newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters
(2C) learn new language structures, expressions, and basic and academic vocabulary heard during classroom instruction and interactions
(2D) monitor understanding of spoken language during classroom instruction and interactions and seek clarification as needed
(2E) use visual, contextual, and linguistic support to enhance and confirm understanding of increasingly complex and elaborated spoken language
(2F) listen to and derive meaning from a variety of media such as audiotape, video, DVD, and CD-ROM to build and reinforce concept and language attainment
(2G) understand the general meaning, main points, and important details of spoken language ranging from situations in which topics, language, and contexts are familiar to unfamiliar
(2H) understand implicit ideas and information in increasingly complex spoken language commensurate with grade-level learning expectations
(2I) demonstrate listening comprehension of increasingly complex spoken English by following directions, retelling or summarizing spoken messages, responding to questions and requests, collaborating with peers, and taking notes commensurate with content and grade-level needs

The OpenStax Algebra I curriculum provides multiple speaking opportunities for learners to demonstrate mastery of algebraic knowledge and skills. English language learners are supported during formal and informal speaking with vocabulary fluency and accuracy.

Within the Algebra I curriculum, look for the following opportunities to support ELPS 3: Speaking:

  • Vocabulary: Explicitly teach unit and lesson-level vocabulary, including how to pronounce sounds. (ELPS 3A, 3B)
  • Vocabulary: Support students by providing high-frequency word lists and/or visuals to guide and extend their work. (ELPS 3B, 3C, 3D, 3F))
  • Assessment: Provide speaking guidance on assignments and assessments through the inclusion of oral skills on instructions and rubrics. (ELPS 3C, 3D)
  • Assessment: Provide a variety of ways to assess student learning, including oral presentation. (ELPS 3H, 3I, 3J)
  • Speaking and writing: Support students by providing sentence stems with specific grammatical structures and/or visuals to guide and extend their work. (ELPS 3C)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Create opportunities for group discussions in cooperative learning environments. (ELPS 3E, 3G, 3I)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Have students write or share orally their steps when solving problems. In predetermined groups or pairs, students share their steps and agree/disagree with the step and can work cooperatively to modify responses and resolve if needed. (ELPS 3E, 3G, 3H)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Incorporate teacher questions and provide sentence stems for students. Allow students opportunities to practice producing sounds for key vocabulary as they demonstrate/extend their understanding. (ELPS 3A)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Provide accountable conversation and writing opportunities for students to interact with peers and solve or create real-world story problems using informal/formal language using both oral and written language. (ELPS 3B, 3C, 3I, 3J)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Have students work in predetermined pairs/groups and discuss their work, being sure to encourage use of content-specific vocabulary. (ELPS 3D)
ELPS 3: Speaking Student Expectations
The student is expected to:
(3A) practice producing sounds of newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters to pronounce English words in a manner that is increasingly comprehensible
(3B) expand and internalize initial English vocabulary by learning and using high-frequency English words necessary for identifying and describing people, places, and objects, by retelling simple stories and basic information represented or supported by pictures, and by learning and using routine language needed for classroom communication
(3C) speak using a variety of grammatical structures, sentence lengths, sentence types, and connecting words with increasing accuracy and ease as more English is acquired
(3D) speak using grade-level content area vocabulary in context to internalize new English words and build academic language proficiency
(3E) share information in cooperative learning interactions
(3F) ask and give information ranging from using a very limited bank of high-frequency, high-need, concrete vocabulary, including key words and expressions needed for basic communication in academic and social contexts, to using abstract and content-based vocabulary during extended speaking assignments
(3G) express opinions, ideas, and feelings ranging from communicating single words and short phrases to participating in extended discussions on a variety of social and grade-appropriate academic topics
(3H) narrate, describe, and explain with increasing specificity and detail as more English is acquired
(3I) adapt spoken language appropriately for formal and informal purposes
(3J) respond orally to information presented in a wide variety of print, electronic, audio, and visual media to build and reinforce concept and language attainment

The OpenStax Algebra I curriculum integrates reading strategies to support comprehending and solving word problems and real-life scenarios.

Within the Algebra I curriculum, look for the following opportunities to support ELPS 4: Reading:

  • Vocabulary: Incorporate into vocabulary definitions and explanations in a warm up activity. (ELPS 4C)
  • Vocabulary: Explicitly teach unit and lesson-level vocabulary, including breaking words down by cognates, affixes, roots, and base words. (ELPS 4A, 4C)
  • Vocabulary: Incorporate into vocabulary definitions and explanations when introducing word walls, using warm up activities, comparing polysemous words, etc. Example: Have students respond to lesson images using the stem: "This picture is about ___” (ELPS 4D)
  • Word Problems: Provide longer word problems for students to read, markup, interpret, and solve algebraic equations. (ELPS 4A through 4K)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Have students write sentences using highlighted vocabulary. Have students switch sentences with a partner and read each other’s sentences and decide if they agree or disagree with each other’s examples. (ELPS 4F)
  • Partner & Group Collaboration: Pair reading of extended real-world reading scenarios and word problems to jointly determine solutions. Provide models and sentence stems as needed. (ELPS 4G)
  • Word Problems: Guide students during lessons that involve real-world word problems especially within 1:1 support. (ELPS 4B, 4K)
  • Word Problems: Provide extended real-world reading scenarios where students have to identify the correct numbers for problem-solving. Scenarios should get increasingly complex. (ELPS 4H, 4I, 4J, 4K)
  • Writing: Provide alternate questions that remove extraneous information, replace word numbers with numerals and simplify the overall language. (ELPS 4E)
ELPS 4: Reading
The student is expected to:
(4A) learn relationships between sounds and letters of the English language and decode (sound out) words using a combination of skills such as recognizing sound-letter relationships and identifying cognates, affixes, roots, and base words
(4B) recognize directionality of English reading such as left to right and top to bottom
(4C) develop basic sight vocabulary, derive meaning of environmental print, and comprehend English vocabulary and language structures used routinely in written classroom materials
(4D) use prereading supports such as graphic organizers, illustrations, and pretaught topic-related vocabulary and other prereading activities to enhance comprehension of written text
(4E) read linguistically accommodated content area material with a decreasing need for linguistic accommodations as more English is learned
(4F) use visual and contextual support and support from peers and teachers to read grade-appropriate content area text, enhance and confirm understanding, and develop vocabulary, grasp of language structures, and background knowledge needed to comprehend increasingly challenging language
(4G) demonstrate comprehension of increasingly complex English by participating in shared reading, retelling or summarizing material, responding to questions, and taking notes commensurate with content area and grade-level needs
(4H) read silently with increasing ease and comprehension for longer periods
(4I) demonstrate English comprehension and expand reading skills by employing basic reading skills such as demonstrating understanding of supporting ideas and details in text and graphic sources, summarizing text, and distinguishing main ideas from details commensurate with content area needs
(4J) demonstrate English comprehension and expand reading skills by employing inferential skills such as predicting, making connections between ideas, drawing inferences and conclusions from text and graphic sources, and finding supporting text evidence commensurate with content area needs
(4K) demonstrate English comprehension and expand reading skills by employing analytical skills such as evaluating written information and performing critical analyses commensurate with content area and grade-level needs

The OpenStax Algebra I curriculum integrates purposeful writing into instructional and evaluative activities for different audiences.

Within the Algebra I curriculum, look for the following opportunities to support ELPS 5: Writing:

  • Vocabulary: Explicitly instruct on vocabulary new to the learner at the unit and lesson level. (ELPS 5B)
  • Vocabulary: Add words to a class word wall. (ELPS 5A, 5B, 5C, 5D)
  • Writing: Incorporate a teaching philosophy of writing across the curriculum to help learners gain a deeper conceptual understanding of mathematical knowledge and process (ELPS 5A through 5G)
  • Assessments: Provide writing guidance on assignments and assessments through the inclusion of writing skills on instructions and rubrics, including writing skills of spelling, grammar, and language use. (ELPS 5C, 5D, 5E, 5F, 5G)
  • Writing: Ask learners to write rationales for answers, corrections, or process descriptions. (ELPS 5B,5C, 5D, 5E, 5F, 5G)
  • Journaling: Incorporate into journaling reflections by emphasizing/correcting/encouraging the use of newly acquired vocabulary and relationship of letters and sounds. Provide sentence stems or close sentences that will aid in extending student language acquisition. (ELPS 5B,5C, 5D, 5E, 5F, 5G)
  • Word Problems: Provide feedback on writing as a part of explanation-based student responses. (ELPS 5A, 5B, 5C)
  • Word Problems: Incorporate into journaling reflections by emphasizing/correcting/encouraging the use of correct grammatical structures. (ELPS 5D, 5E)
ELPS 5: Writing
The student is expected to:
(5A) learn relationships between sounds and letters of the English language to represent sounds when writing in English
(5B) write using newly acquired basic vocabulary and content-based grade-level vocabulary
(5C) spell familiar English words with increasing accuracy, and employ English spelling patterns and rules with increasing accuracy as more English is acquired
(5D) edit writing for standard grammar and usage, including subject-verb agreement, pronoun agreement, and appropriate verb tenses commensurate with grade-level expectations as more English is acquired
(5E) employ increasingly complex grammatical structures in content area writing commensurate with grade-level expectations, such as:
(5Ei) using correct verbs, tenses, and pronouns/antecedents
(5Eii) using possessive case (apostrophe s) correctly
(5Eiii) using negatives and contractions correctly
(F) write using a variety of grade-appropriate sentence lengths, patterns, and connecting words to combine phrases, clauses, and sentences in increasingly accurate ways as more English is acquired
(G) narrate, describe, and explain with increasing specificity and detail to fulfill content area writing needs as more English is acquired
