Activity (10 minutes)

We can determine the slope and intercept of a line if the equation is written in slope-intercept form, \( y = mx + b \), where \(m\) is the slope and \( b \) is the \( y \)-intercept. Now we will do the reverse—we will start with the slope and \(y\)-intercept and use them to write the equation of the line.

Launch

Students found the slope of a line using the formula. Use a graph to demonstrate that the slope of the line is the ratio of rise over run, change in \( y \) over change in \( x \). Ask students:

  • How can you identify the \( y \)-intercept from a graph? (It is the point where the line crosses the \( y \)-axis.)
  • What is the value of \( x \) where the line crosses the \( y \)-axis? (0)

Activity Synthesis

The slope-intercept form of the equation of a line is the most commonly used form. You can identify the slope and the \( y \)-intercept, and it is the form used to graph the line using a graphing calculator. The equation \( y=mx+b \) may look to some students like there are 4 variables. Point out the \( m \) and \( b \) are numbers that you are given to substitute into the equation. The value of \( m \) is the coefficient of \( x \), and the value of \( b \) is the constant in the equation of the line.

Students may be confused about the value of \( b \) as the \( y \)-intercept. Elicit that the \( y \)-intercept is the point where the graph crosses the \( y \)-axis. The \( x \)-value of the \( y \)-intercept is zero. In the slope-intercept form, the \( b \) represents the \(y\)-value of the \( y \)-intercept. Sometimes the \( y \)-intercept is identified as just the \( y \)-value. For example, the \( y \)-intercept is 2 really means the \( y \)-intercept is \( (0, 2) \). To write the equation of a line in slope-intercept form, substitute 2 for \( b \): \( y=mx+2 \).

It may help students to practice writing equations with the same slope and different \(y\)-intercepts. For example, have students write the equation of a line with slope 2 and \( y \)-intercept \( (0, 4) \) \( (y=2x+4) \); slope 2 and \( y \)-intercept \( (0, -1) \) \( (y=2x-1) \); slope 2 and \( y \)-intercept \((0, 3)\) \( (y=2x+3) \). Then have students write the equation of a line with different slopes and the same \( y \)-intercept. For example, have students write the equation of a line with slope 4 and \( y \)-intercept 5; slope \( \frac{2}{3} \) and \( y \)-intercept 5; slope \( -\frac{1}{2} \) and \( y \)-intercept 5.

Write Equations Given a Slope and \( y \)-Intercept

We can determine the slope and intercept of a line if the equation is written in slope-intercept form, \( y=mx+b \). Now we will do the reverse—we will start with the slope and \( y \)-intercept and use them to find the equation of the line.

Example 1

Find the equation of a line with slope \( −1 \) and \( y \)-intercept \( (0,−3) \).

Solution:

Step 1 - Name the slope.
\( m = -1 \)

Step 2 - Name the \( y \)-intercept.
\( b = -3 \)

Step 3 - Substitute the values into \( y = mx + b \).
\( y = -1x + -3 \)
\( y = -x-3 \)


The same information can be picked up from a graph of a line.

Example 2

Find the equation of the line shown in the graph.

Solution

Step 1 - Find the slope by counting the rise and run.
\( m=\frac{rise}{run} \)
From \( (0, -4) \) to \( (3,-2) \), the slope is \( m = \frac{2}{3} \).

Step 2 - Find the \( y \)-intercept.
\( y \)-intercept \( (0, -4) \)
\( b = -4 \)

Step 3 - Substitute the values into \( y = mx + b \).
\( y = mx +b \)
\( y = \frac{2}{3} x - 4 \)
