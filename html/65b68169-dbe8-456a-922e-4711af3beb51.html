Cool Down (5 minutes)

Activity

Here is the quadratic formula:

\(x=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2- 4ac}}{2a}\)

Use the formula to solve the equation \(x^2+9x+8=0\).

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer: The solutions are -8 and -1.

Apply the quadratic formula for \(a=1\), \(b=9\), \(c=8\):

\(x=\frac{-9 \pm \sqrt{(9)^2- 4(1)(8)}}{2(1)}=\frac{-9 \pm \sqrt{49}}{2}\), which means

\(x=\frac{-9-7}{2}=-8\) and \(x=\frac{-9+7}{2}=-1\).
