Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. If \( y \) varies directly as \( x \) and \( y=14 \),​ when \( x=3 \). find the equation that relates \( x \) and \( y \).

    1. \(x=\frac{14}3y\)
    2. \(y=\frac3{14}x\)
    3. \(3x=14y\)
    4. \(y=\frac{14}3x\)

    ANSWER:       \( y= \frac{14}{3}x \)

  2. The price, \( P \), that Eric pays for gas varies directly with the number of gallons, \( g \), he buys. It costs him $50 to buy 20 gallons of gas.

    Choose the equation that relates \(P\) and \(g\).

    1. \(P=2.5g\)
    2. \(P=\frac{2.5}g\)
    3. \(g=\frac{2.5}P\)
    4. \(P=\frac g{2.5}\)

    ANSWER: \(P= 2.5g\)
  3. Joseph is traveling on a road trip. The distance, \( d \), he travels before stopping for lunch varies directly with the speed, \( v \), he travels. He can travel 120 miles at a speed of 60 mph. How many miles would he travel before stopping for lunch at a rate of 65 mph? Only enter integers.

    ANSWER:     130


  4. The mass of a liquid, \( M \), varies directly with its volume, \( V \). A liquid with mass 16 kilograms has a volume of 2 liters. 

    What is the volume, in liters, of this liquid if its mass is 128 kilograms? Enter integers only.

    ANSWER:      16

  5. The length, \( L \), that a spring stretches varies directly with a weight, \( W \), placed at the end of the spring. When Sarah placed a 10-pound watermelon on a hanging scale, the spring stretched 5 inches.

    What weight of watermelon, in pounds, would stretch the spring 6 inches? Enter integers only.

    ANSWER: 12


  6. The maximum load a beam, \( M \), will support varies directly with the square of the diagonal of the beam’s cross-section, \( d^2 \). A beam with diagonal 6 inch will support a maximum load of 108 pounds. 

    What load, in pounds, will a beam with a 10-inch diagonal support? Enter integers only.

    ANSWER: 300

  7. The number of calories, \( c \), burned varies directly with the amount of time, \( t \), spent exercising. Arnold burned 312 calories in 65 minutes exercising. 

    How many calories would he burn if he exercises for 90 minutes? Enter integers only.

    ANSWER: 432
