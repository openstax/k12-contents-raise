To help students summarize the skills they learned dividing polynomials, emphasize the following points:

  • Divide monomials by monomials by dividing the coefficients and then dividing the variables using the Quotient Property for exponents.
  • Divide a polynomial by a monomial by distributing the division of the monomial to each term in the polynomial dividend.
  • The solution to your division problem can alway be checked by multiplying the solution by the divisor to see if the product matches the original dividend.
  • To divide a polynomial by another polynomial, use long division as you have previously learned using numbers.
  • An efficient method to divide a polynomial by a binomial is synthetic division.
    • The divisor must be in the form \(x - c\), where \(c\) is a constant.
    • Use a placeholder with a \(0\) coefficient, such as \(0x^2\), for any term that is missing in the dividend from the term with the highest degree down to a degree of \(0\).
    • The bottom-right number in the third row of the synthetic division is the remainder.
  • Polynomial functions can be divided using the same methods as with other polynomials.
  • Use the Remainder Theorem to easily find the value of a remainder in any polynomial division problem.
  • Use the Factor Theorem to determine if a divisor is a factor of a larger polynomial.
