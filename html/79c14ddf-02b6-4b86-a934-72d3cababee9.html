Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student-Facing Statement

The college student who is looking for a data plan for her cell phone found a third option, which also offers a 2-gigabyte monthly allowance for data.

The monthly cost of this option can be represented by function \(C\), defined by \( C(x)=30x+5\), where \(x\) is the gigabytes of data used over the monthly allowance.

  1. Find \(C(0.7)\) and explain what it means in this situation.
  2. Her budget is still $50 a month. To find out how many gigabytes of data she could use if she chose this plan, she writes \(C(x)=50\) and solves for \(x\). What value of \(x\) makes this equation true? Explain your reasoning.

Student Response

  1. \(C(0.7)=26\) because \(30(0.7)+5=26\). Here is a possible explanation: It means that the cost would be $26 if she used 0.7 gigabyte over the 2-gigabytes allowance in a month.
  2. \(x=1.5\). Here is a possible explanation: Because \( C(x)=30x+5\), we can write \(C(x)=50\) as \(30x+5=50\) and solve for \(x\), which gives 1.5.

Response to Student Thinking

Press Pause

At this point, students need to be able to evaluate and interpret equations given in function notation. If students continue to struggle, select examples of cool-downs from this unit to highlight and clarify misconceptions, and then have students work to revise cool-downs and reflect on their misconceptions. Practice problems 1, 3, 4, and 5 from this lesson all provide opportunities for practice and additional formative assessment.
