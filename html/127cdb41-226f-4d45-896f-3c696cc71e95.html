Where to Start

To view the lessons:

  • Go to "My courses" then click your Algebra course tile (Image 1)
  • View lessons by clicking on the unit you would like to see. You can view the lessons in every unit by clicking "Expand all" (Image 2)

Beginning a Unit

Overview and Readiness: Before starting a new unit we recommend taking the overview and readiness lesson. This will help you review topics for the unit and prepare for the new material you will be learning.

Sections: After the overview and readiness lesson, the rest of the lessons are organized into sections.

Quizzes: Opportunities to test out your new algebra skills! In each unit you will find Section Quizzes, a Unit Quiz, and a STAAR Review.

Lesson Walk-Through

Lesson Overview: This is the first page of a lesson where you will learn about what topics will be covered and the activities you will complete in the lesson. You can jump to any of the activities on this page by clicking the right-hand menu.

Lesson Activities

As you progress through the lesson, you will do different types of activities:

  • Warm-up
  • Primary activities (These are unique and need extra focus. We’ll take a close look at them in the next part of this guide)
  • Cool-down
  • Practice

Primary Activities: Here’s an example of one of these unique types of activities, each lesson has 2-3 of these. After you have finished the activity you will take a multiple-choice question called, "Self Check".

Are you ready for more?: This button opens additional questions for you to expand your thinking. This section is optional, unless otherwise stated by your teacher.

Next: After completing an activity, be sure to press the "Next" button at the bottom of the page. This will take you to the self-check question.

Self Check: This is a self-check question. If you get the answer correct, congratulations you get to move on to the next activity! If you get the answer incorrect, you will be directed to an additional resource page where you can review the topics and retake the self-check question.

Lesson Summary

Lesson Summary: Similar to the overview page you will review the topics covered and the activities you completed. You will also have an opportunity to check-in on your progress with the learning goals from the lesson.

End of Lesson: Once you have completed the lesson be sure to press “End of Lesson” so your teacher knows you have completed the lesson.
