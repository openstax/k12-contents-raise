Student Activity

A movie theater models the revenue from ticket sales in one day as a function of the ticket price, \(p\). Here are two expressions defining the same revenue function.

\(p(120 − 4p)\)

\(120p − 4p^2\)

  1. According to this model, how high would the ticket price have to be for the theater to make $0 in revenue? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: $30. If \(p(120 − 4p) = 0\), then either \(p = 0\) or \(120 − 4p = 0\). If the latter is a true equation, \(p\) must be 30.

  1. What equation can you write to find out what ticket price(s) would allow the theater to make $600 in revenue?

Answer: \(p(120−4p) = 600\) or \(120p − 4p^2 = 600\)
