Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

1. What is the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph that represents \(y=(x−2)^2−8\)?

ANSWER: 2. The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is found by using the squared term of the vertex form. The value of the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph is 2

2. What is the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph that represents \(y=(x−2)^2−8\)?

ANSWER: -8. In vertex form, the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex is the constant. The value of the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph is -8.

3. Where is the \(y\)-intercept for the equation \(y=(x−2)^2−8\) located?

ANSWER: -4. Substitute 0 for \(x\) and you get \(y=(0−2)^2−8=4−8=-4\).

4. Which of the following is another point on the graph of the equation \(y=(x−2)^2−8\)?

  • \((4,-4)\)
  • \((-4,4)\)
  • \((4,4)\)
  • \((-4,-4)\)

ANSWER: \((4,-4)\)

5. Which graph is a representation of \(y=(x - 6)^2 - 5\)?

ANSWER: Graph A

6. The function \(v\) is defined by \(v(x)=12(x+5)^2−7\).

Without graphing, determine if the vertex of the graph representing \(v\) shows the minimum or maximum value of the function.

  • The vertex shows the minimum value.
  • The vertex shows the maximum value.

ANSWER: The vertex shows the minimum value.
