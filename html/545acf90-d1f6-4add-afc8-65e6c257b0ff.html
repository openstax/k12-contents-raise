In this lesson, students work to graph functions in slope-intercept form using transformations. They will identify vertical shifts, vertical stretches and compressions, and vertical rotations.

Through exploration with graphing, students will recognize patterns for the transformations and understand how the constants in the slope-intercept form determine the transformations needed to graph the function.

Students will also be able to describe how one function transforms from the parent function, f(x)=x and how one function transforms to another.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Use slope-intercept form and transformations to graph linear functions.
  • Recognize the shifts made to a parent linear function to form another linear function.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Use slope-intercept form and transformations to graph linear functions.
  • Recognize the shifts made to a parent linear function to form another linear function.
