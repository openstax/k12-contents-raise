Creating a Sequence

For a sequence: 1, 3, . . .

Write the next two terms that would make the sequence

  1. geometric
  2. arithmetic

Solution

  1. To make the sequence geometric, there must be a common ratio. \(1\cdot3=3\), so use a common ratio of 3 to multiply each term by. The sequence becomes: 1, 3, 9, 27 . . .
  1. To make the sequence arithmetic, there must be a common difference. \(3-1=2\), so use a common difference of 2 to add to each term. The sequence becomes: 1, 3, 5, 7 . . .

Here is how to create an arithmetic sequence given two terms:

Find the common difference by subtracting the first term from the second term:

\(6-2=4\)

The common difference is 4.

Add 4 to the previous term to find the next terms.

The sequence becomes: 2, 6, 10, 14 . . .
