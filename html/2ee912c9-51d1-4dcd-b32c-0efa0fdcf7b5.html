Extending Your Thinking

Below are the equations from above. Each equation is in the form \( Ax + By = C \). 

\( -4x + 3y = 3 \)
\( 12x - 4y = 8 \)
\( 8x + 2y = 16    \)
\( -x + \frac{1}{3}y = \frac{1}{3} \)
\( -4x + 3y = -6 \)

1. For each equation, graph the equation and, on the same coordinate plane, graph the line passing through \( (0,0) \) and \( (A,B) \). What do you notice about each pair of lines? For example, for the first equation pair, graph \( -4x + 3y = 3 \) and also the line through \( (0,0) \) and \( (-4, 3) \).

They are perpendicular.


2. Find the \( x \)-intercept and \( y \)-intercept of each equation. Do you notice a pattern? What are the coordinates of the \( x \)-intercept and \( y \)-intercept in terms of A, B, and C?
