Finding The Correlation Coefficient Using Technology

Let’s find the correlation coefficient for a given data set.

Step 1 - Click the \(+\) symbol in the top left and select a table.

Step 2 - Enter the data set as shown. Press Enter.

\(x_1\) \(y_1\)
-6 -1
-4 1
0 -2
1 -3
3 0

The data set will populate the graph as shown here.



Step 3 - On the left side, click in box #2 for a new item. Enter the command corr(\(x_1, y_1\)). Remember from the activity that Desmos will automatically place the 1 as subscript after an \(x\) or a \(y\).

As soon as you enter the correlation command, Desmos will display the correlation coefficient after an equal sign in the bottom right corner of the same box.



Step 3 - Round to the nearest thousandth. The correlation coefficient is -0.299.

Step 4 - Determine the strength of the linear relationship. This correlation coefficient shows a weak negative linear relationship.
