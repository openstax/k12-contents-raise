Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. A rocket is launched in the air and its height, in feet, is modeled by the function \(h\).

Here is a graph representing \(h\).

Select three true statements about the situation.

    • The rocket is launched from a height less than 20 feet above the ground.
    • The rocket is launched from about 20 feet above the ground.
    • The rocket reaches its maximum height after about 3 seconds.
    • The rocket reaches its maximum height after about 160 seconds.
    • The maximum height of the rocket is about 160 feet.

ANSWER: The rocket is launched from a height less than 20 feet above the ground. The rocket reaches its maximum height after about 3 seconds. The maximum height of the rocket is about 160 feet.

  1. A small ball is dropped from a 100-foot-tall building. Which equation could represent the ball’s height, \(h(t)\), in feet, relative to the ground, as a function of time, \(t\), in seconds?
    • \(h(t)=100-16t\)
    • \(h(t)=100-16t^2\)
    • \(h(t)=100+16t^2\)
    • \(h(t)=100-16t\)

ANSWER: \(h(t)=100-16t^2\)
