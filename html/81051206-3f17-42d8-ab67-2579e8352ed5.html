Lesson Narrative

In earlier lessons, students wrote and solved linear inequalities in one variable. In this lesson, they transition to linear inequalities in two variables.

Previously, students learned that the solutions to an equation in two variables are all pairs of values that make the equation true, and that, when graphed, the solutions are points on a line. Here, they learn that the solutions to inequalities in two variables also involve pairs of values. When graphed, the solutions are no longer points on a single line, but comprise a region that is bounded by a line. This region consists of all points in a plane on one side of a boundary line. That boundary line is the graph of an equation related to the inequality.

Students begin by noticing that the plots of solutions and non-solutions occupy different parts of a coordinate plane. Next, they think about the boundary line between the two regions and whether it is a part of the solution. Finally, students write some inequalities given graphs that represent solution regions.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Determine the solution region to an inequality in two variables by testing the points on the line and on either side of the line, given the graph of a related equation.
  • Explain that the solutions to a linear inequality in two variables are represented graphically as a half-plane bounded by a line.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Determine which side of the line the solutions to the inequality will fall, given a two-variable inequality and the graph of the related equation.
  • Describe the graph that represents the solutions to a linear inequality in two variables.

Required Materials

  • Colored pencils
  • Optional: graphing calculator

Required Preparation

The colored pencils are optional. If used in the first activity, each student needs 2 different colors. As an alternative to 2 colors, students could just write two different symbols.

Lesson Vocabulary

During this lesson, it is important to:

  • Familiarize students with the vocabulary words they will see throughout the lesson. Encourage students to look for these words and notice their use and meanings.
  • Encourage students to use key vocabulary words in “math talk” and their written and oral explanations.
