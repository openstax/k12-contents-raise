  1. A company sells running shoes. If the price of a pair of shoes in dollars is \(p\), the company estimates that it will sell \(50,000-400p\) pairs of shoes.

Which expression represents the revenue in dollars from selling running shoes if a pair of shoes is priced at \(p\) dollars?

    • \((50,000-400p) \cdot p\)
    • \((50,000p-400) \cdot p\)
    • \((50,000p-400p)\)
    • \((49,600p) \cdot p\)

Use the following to answer questions 7–9.

The function \(f\) represents the revenue in dollars the school can expect to receive if it sells \(220-12x\) coffee mugs for \(x\) dollars each.

Here is the graph of \(f\).

  1. Select three statements that describe this situation.
  • At \($2\) per coffee mug, the revenue will be \($196\).
  • The school expects to sell \(160\) mugs if the price is \($5\) each.
  • The school will lose money if it sells the mugs for more than \($10\) each.
  • The school will earn about \($1,000\) if it sells the mugs for \($10\) each.
  • The revenue will be more than \($700\) if the price is between \($5\) and \($14\).
  • The expected revenue will increase if the price per mug is greater than \($10\).

Answer: The school expects to sell \(160\) mugs if the price is \($5\) each. The school will earn about \($1,000\) if it sells the mugs for \($10\) each. The revenue will be more than \($700\) if the price is between \($5\) and \($14\).

  1. To the nearest hundred dollars, what is the maximum revenue for the mugs?

Answer: \(1,000\)
