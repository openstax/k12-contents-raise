Warm Up (5 minute)

The purpose of this warm-up is to help students recall what a proportion is and how to solve a proportion. Elicit student strategies, such as scaling up/down numerators and denominators, and if students don’t bring up cross-multiplication on their own, make sure to cover that strategy.

Launch

Remind students that they may have learned more than one way to solve these equations, and it is okay to use a variety of strategies.

Student Facing Statement

Solve each of the following proportions:

  1. \( \frac{x}{56} = \frac{7}{8} \)
  2. \( \frac{56}{72} = \frac{y}{9} \)
  3. \( \frac{98}{154} = \frac{-7}{p} \)

Student Response

  1. \( x=49 \) 
  2. \( y=7 \)
  3. \( p= -11 \)

Activity Synthesis

  • Show different work examples. Who used cross-multiplication? Who used their multiplication facts to find the missing value?
  • Can you think of situations in real life where you use proportions?
