Activity (20 minutes)

This activity provides students practice in adding and subtracting different polynomial expressions. Students will first add and subtract monomials.

Students will then move to adding and subtracting larger polynomial expressions by focusing on combining like terms within the polynomials. For an additional challenge, have students simplify the polynomial expression under the Extending Your Thinking heading.

Launch

Answer the first two questions in the activity as a class. As students analyze the terms in question 1, \(6x^4y^2\) and \(8x^4y^2\), stress that only like terms, with the same variables having the same degree, can be added and subtracted.

Allow a moment for students to think about question 2. After soliciting the class for an answer, emphasize that when like terms are added or subtracted, the variables and exponents remain the same while the coefficients are added or subtracted.

Allow students to complete the remaining questions individually.

Student Activity

Solve these two problems as a class.

  1. Do these terms have the same variables with the same exponents? Explain your answer.

    \(6x^4y^2\)         \(8x^4y^2\)

ANSWER: Yes. Both terms have the same variables raised to the same degrees: \(x\) is raised to the fourth power, and \(y\) is squared.

  1. Add the terms together: \(6x^4y^2+8x^4y^2\). What is the sum?

ANSWER: \(14x^4y^2\)

Student-Facing Extension

Are you ready for more?

Simplify.

\((6a^3-2a^2b)-2(ab^2+3b^3)+(2a^2b+2ab^2)\)

ANSWER: \(6a^3-6b^3\)

Here is how to solve this problem:
\((6a^3-2a^2b)-2(ab^2+3b^3)+(2a^2b+2ab^2)\)

Step 1
Distribute the multiplication over addition.
\(6a^3-2a^2b-2ab^2-6b^3+2a^2b+2ab^2\)

Step 2
Use the Commutative Property to rearrange like terms together.
\(6a^3-2a^2b+2a^2b-2ab^2+2ab^2-6b^3\)

Step 3
Combine like terms.
\(6a^3-6b^3\)

Anticipated Misconceptions

Students may think that they can combine any terms that share the same variables. Emphasize that each variable in the terms must also share the same degree for the terms to be added or subtracted.

Activity Synthesis

As students add and subtract to simplify the polynomial expressions, emphasize that the degree of the term does not change. When \(5x^2\) is added to \(6x^2\), the sum still has a degree of two, \(11x^2\). The same is true for the difference between two like terms.

Invite students to share any of the problems that gave them difficulty. Work through the addition or subtraction as a class.

Adding and Subtracting Monomials

We have learned how to simplify expressions by combining like terms. Remember, like terms must have the same variables with the same exponent. Since monomials are terms, adding and subtracting monomials is the same as combining like terms. If the monomials are like terms, we just combine them by adding or subtracting the coefficients. Remember that like terms must have the same variables with the same exponents.

These terms have the same variables and the same exponents:

  • \(5m^3n^2\)          \(2m^3n^2\)

So we can add the monomials:

  • \(5m^3n^2 + 2m^3n^2 = 7m^3n^2\)

The variables and exponents of the monomials do not change when we add or subtract them.

Try It: Adding and Subtracting Monomials

Add or subtract.

  1. \( 25y^2+15y^2\)
  2. \(16pq^3-(-7pq^3)\)

Write down your answers. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Solution

Here is how to add and subtract:

Combine like terms to simplify the expressions.

  1. \( 25y^2+15y^2=40y^2\)
  1. \(\begin{array}{rcl}16pq^3-(-7pq^3)&=&16pq^3+7pq^3\\&=&23pq^3\end{array}\)

Adding and Subtracting Polynomials

We can think of adding and subtracting polynomials as just adding and subtracting a series of monomials. Look for the like terms—those with the same variables and the same exponent. The Commutative Property allows us to rearrange the terms to put like terms together.

Let’s look at an example.

Find the sum: \((7y^2-2y+9)+(4y^2-8y-7)\).

Step 1
Identify like terms by finding those with the same variable and same exponent.
\(({\style{color:red}7}{\style{color:red}y}^{\style{color:red}2} - {\style{color:blue}2}{\style{color:blue}y} + 9) + ({\style{color:red}4}{\style{color:red}y}^{\style{color:red}2} - {\style{color:blue}8}{\style{color:blue}y} - 7)\)

Step 2
Use the Commutative Property to rearrange like terms together. Be sure to take the appropriate sign with each term \((+/-)\).
\({\style{color:red}7}{\style{color:red}y}^{\style{color:red}2} + {\style{color:red}4}{\style{color:red}y}^{\style{color:red}2} - {\style{color:blue}2}{\style{color:blue}y} - {\style{color:blue}8}{\style{color:blue}y} + 9 - 7\)

Step 3
Combine like terms.
\({\style{color:red}1}{\style{color:red}1}{\style{color:red}y}^{\style{color:red}2} - {\style{color:blue}1}{\style{color:blue}0}{\style{color:blue}y} + 2\)


Try It: Adding and Subtracting Polynomials

Find the sum and difference. If necessary, be careful with the signs as you distribute while subtracting the polynomials.

  1. \((7x^2-4x+5)+(x^2-7x+3)\)
  2. \((9w^2-7w+5)-(2w^2-4)\)

Write down your answers. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Solution

Here is how to find the sum and difference:

  1. \((7x^2-4x+5)+(x^2-7x+3)=8x^2-11x+8\)

  2. \(\begin{array}{rcl}(9w^2-7w+5)-(2w^2-4)&=&(9w^2-7w+5)-2w^2+4\\&=&7w^2-7w+9\end{array}\)
