Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1-3 choose an equivalent equation.

  1. Which equation is equivalent to \(3x + 6 = 4x + 7\)?

Answer:

\(3x-1=4x\)

  1. Which equation is equivalent to \(3(x + 6) = 4x + 7\)?

Answer:

\(3x+18=4x+7\)

  1. Which equation is equivalent to \(4x + 3x = 7-6\)?

Answer:

\(7x=1\)

  1. Select three equations that have 0 as a solution.

Answer:

\(2x+10=4x+10\), \(4x=9x\), and \(2x-3+4x=4-7\)

  1. Which equation is equivalent to 6(x-3)=24?

Answer:

\(x-3=4\)

  1. Which of the following sets of equations are equivalent to each other?

Answer:

10(x+9) = 50 and 2(x+9)=10. Each side of the equation was divided by 5. So the equations are equivalent.

  1. Using the distributive property, choose which equation would be an equivalent equation to 3(x-2) =7.

Answer:

3x - 6 =7. It is one of the solutions that could be equivalent to 3(x-2) =7
