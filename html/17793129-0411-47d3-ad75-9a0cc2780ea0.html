In this lesson, you learned how to:

  • Describe the effect of changing \(a\) and \(b\) on a graph that represents \(f(x)=a \cdot b^x\).
  • Use equations and graphs to compare exponential functions.

Here are the activities that helped you reach those goals:

  • 5.12.1: Modeling Exponential Decay
    • In this activity, you practiced modeling a situation characterized by exponential decay with an equation.
  • 5.12.2: Equations and Their Graphs
    • In this activity, you examined how the numbers \(a\) and \(b\) influence the graph representing a function \(f\) defined by an equation of the form \(f(x)=a \cdot b^x\).
    • 5.12.2: Self Check
    • 5.12.2: Additional Resources 
  • 5.12.3: Graphs Representing Exponential Decay
    • In this activity, you explored how the parameter \(b\) influences the graph of an exponential function, which prepared you to make sense of and model situations involving repeated percentage.
    • 5.12.3: Self Check
    • 5.12.3: Additional Resources 
  • 5.12.4: Possible Equation for a Function on a Graph
    • In this activity, you determined the graphed equation that defined a given function.
