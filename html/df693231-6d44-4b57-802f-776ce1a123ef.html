Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Facing Statement

At the end of the school year, all students in the ninth grade are invited to an ice cream party. Use the skills you learned from planning a pizza party to answer the following:

  1. Identify and describe at least one letter that you will use to represent the cost of something you need to buy for the party.

Answer:

Your answer may vary, but here are some examples:

  • t = number of different toppings one student selects
  • s = number of different scoops of ice cream one student requests
  • n = total number of ninth grade students at party
  • p = cost of paper products like napkins, bowls, and spoons
  1. Write an expression that could represent an estimated cost for the party using that letter. Note, you may use other letters, too, if needed.

Answer:

Your answer may vary, but here are some examples:

  • \((0.75s + 1.5t)n =\) total cost
  • \(4.00n + 15 =\) total cost
  • \(1.50n+0.08(1.50n) =\) total cost
  • \(\frac{125}{6} \cdot 5 + 6(3) + p =\) total cost
  1. Describe your expression in words.

Answer:

Your answer may vary, but here are some examples:

  • Each scoop of ice cream costs $0.75 and the cost of each topping is $1.50 which sum to the cost of one bowl of ice cream. Now we multiply the cost of one bowl of ice cream by the number of students in the class.
  • A flat cost of $4 per bowl of ice cream for each student plus a charge of $15 for the bowls, spoons, and napkins.
  • There are \(n\) students in the ninth grade. A packaged ice cream cone is about $1.50, and tax is about 8%.
  • There are 125 students in the ninth grade. Suppose a tub of ice cream costs $5 and is enough for 6 students. A pack of 24 cones costs about $3, and 6 packs are needed. A pack of 150 paper napkins costs \(p\) dollars.

Response to Student Thinking

More Chances

Students will have more opportunities to understand the mathematical ideas in this cool-down, so there is no need to slow down or add work to the next lessons. Instead, use the results of this cool-down to provide guidance for what to look for and emphasize over the next several lessons to support students in advancing their current understanding.
