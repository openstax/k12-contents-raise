The solutions to this equation are \(x = \frac{3}{4}\) and \(x=\frac{1}{4}\). Show how to find those solutions by completing the square. \(x^2−x+\frac{3}{16}=0\)

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(\begin{array}{rcl}x^2-x+\frac3{16}&=&0\\x^2-x+\frac14&=&-\frac3{16}+\frac14=-\frac3{16}+\frac4{16}=\frac1{16}\\x^2-x+\frac14&=&\frac1{16}\\\sqrt{\left(x-\frac12\right)^2}&=&\sqrt{\frac1{16}}\\x-\frac12&=&\pm\frac14\\x-\frac12&=&\frac14\\x-\frac12&=&-\frac14\end{array}\)
\( \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;x=\frac{1}{4} \text { and } x=\frac{3}{4}\)
