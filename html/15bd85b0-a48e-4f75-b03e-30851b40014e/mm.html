Warm Up Activity

A good method for studying is to make sure that you give yourself reminders about things you have learned before. Can you remember what you learned in the previous algebra lesson?

In each equation, what expression could be substituted for \(a\) so the equation is true for all values of \(x\)?

  1. \(x^2=a^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=x\).


  1. \((3x)^2=a^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=3x\).


  1. \(a^2=7x\cdot7x\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=7x\).


  1. \(25x^2=a^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=5x\).


  1. \(a^2= \frac {1}{4}x^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=\frac {1}{2}x\).


  1. \(a^2=(x+1)^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=x+1\).


  1. \((2x−9)(2x−9)=a^2\)

Enter the expression that could be substituted for \(a\).

Compare your answer:

The correct answer is \(a=2x-9\).
