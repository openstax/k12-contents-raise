To summarize the key concepts from the lesson, pose these sample questions again to see if students have learned all of these situations:

  • Which strategy is applicable to factoring binomials? (difference of squares; factoring the GCF)
  • Which strategy is applicable to factoring trinomials of the form \(x^2 + bx +c\)? (undoing FOIL)
  • Which strategy is applicable to factoring trinomials of the form \(ax^2 + bx +c\)? (trial and error, the “\(ac\)” method)
  • Which strategy is applicable to factoring trinomials of the form \(ax^2 \pm 2ab + b^2\)? (perfect square trinomials)
  • Which strategy is applicable to factoring polynomials with 4 expressions? (grouping)
  • Which strategy is applicable to polynomials that don’t seem to factor easily? (substitution, grouping, factoring the GCF)
