Compare your answer:

Drawing a Diagram:


\(x\) \(-1\)
\(x\) \(x^2\) \(-x\)
\(-1\) \(-x\) \(1\)

The sum of the partial products are \(x^2+-x+-x+1\) or \(x^2-2x+1\).

Applying the Distributive Property:

Applying the distributive property to \((x-1)(x-1)\) gives \(x^2+x \cdot (-1)+(-1) \cdot x+(-1)^2= x^2-x-x+1=x^2-2x+1\).
