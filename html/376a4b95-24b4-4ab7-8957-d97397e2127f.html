Lesson Narrative

This lesson focuses on factoring two different special products. Perfect square trinomials follow the pattern \(a^2 \pm 2ab + b^2\) and factor to \((a + b)^2\) or \((a − b)^2\), depending on the sign of the middle term in the trinomial.

The difference of squares is represented by the binomial \(a^2 − b^2\) and factors to the conjugate pair \((a − b)(a + b)\).

Recognizing the patterns of these special products will allow for the easy factoring of these particular cases.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Recognize the patterns of perfect square trinomials and factor them into the form \((a + b)^2\) or \((a − b)^2\).
  • Recognize the difference of squares and factor them into conjugate pairs.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Factor perfect square trinomials.
  • Factor differences of squares.
