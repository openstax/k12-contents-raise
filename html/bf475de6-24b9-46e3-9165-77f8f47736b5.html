Activity

Recall the process of dividing \(960 \div 20\) as a class using long division. Use this same method to divide the polynomials below.


  1. Find the quotient using long division.

    \((m^2+9m+20) \div (m+4)\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

\(m + 5\);

  1. Find the quotient using long division.

    \((p^2+2p-8) \div (p^2-7p+10)\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(1+ \frac{9p-18}{p^2-7p+10}\)



  1. Find the quotient using long division.

    \((5x+5) \div (x+5)\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(5- \frac{20}{x+5}\)


Notice in the next problem, there is no \(x^3\) term in the dividend. Add \(0x^3\) as a placeholder when using long division.


  1. Find the quotient using long division.

    \((x^4-11x^2-7x-6) \div (x+3)\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(x^3-3x^2-2x-1-\frac{3}{x+3}\)
