Student Activity

  1. Which of these polynomials has a greatest common factor of 1?
  • \(10q^2 + 14q + 20\)
  • \(12x^3 − 2x\)
  • \(12x^3y − 7g^8h^3 + 11s^2\)
  • \(−2x − 4\)

Answer:  \(12x^3y − 7g^8h^3 + 11s^2\)

Determine if the expression can be factored by grouping. If it can, factor the expression. If not, answer “prime.”

  1. \(cd + 6c + 4d + 24\)

Answer: \((d + 6)(c + 4)\)

Answer: prime

Response to Student Thinking

Points to Emphasize

If students are still struggling to identify GCFs, encourage them to write out the factors of each term in the polynomial and use a highlighter or other type of annotation to identify anything that is common amongst the terms. It may also be helpful to return to the steps provided in activity 6.4.2.

Students will have opportunities to review and practice how to factor polynomial expressions again in Unit 8 when they use factoring to solve quadratic equations.
