The graphs show the populations of California and Texas over time.

  1. Estimate the average rate of change in the population in California between 1970 and 2010. Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The population of California was about 20 million in 1970 and 37 million in 2010. The average rate of change is: \(\frac{37-20}{2010-1970}=\frac{17}{40}=0.425\) million per year.


  1. In this situation, what does the rate of change mean for California?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

On average, the population of California was growing by 425,000 people per year.
