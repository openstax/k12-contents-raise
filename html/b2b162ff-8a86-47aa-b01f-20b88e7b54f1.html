Cool Down (5 minutes)


Student Activity

Jesse’s car gets 30 miles per gallon of gas.

  1. Write an equation using direct variation for distance, d, and number of gallons of gas, g for this problem.

Answer: Use the equation for direct variation \( y=kx \). So, \( d=kg \).

  1. If Jesse travels 150 miles he uses 5 gallons of gas. Find the constant of variation.

Answer: Use the equation for direct variation \( y=kx \). So, \(150=k(5)\). The constant of variation is \( k=30 \).

  1. Now, write the equation if Jesse travels d miles with a car that uses 30 miles per gallon of gas.

Answer: \( d=30g \)

  1. If Las Vegas is 285 miles away, how many gallons of gas are needed to get there?

Answer: Substitute 285 miles in for \( d \) in the equation. So, \( 285=30g \) and \( g=9.5 \).
