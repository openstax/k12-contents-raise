Project Activity (25 minutes)

In this activity, students find areas and missing dimensions of rectangles. The dimensions are binomials, and the areas are polynomials.

Students apply their understanding of multiplication, factoring, and division of polynomials to find the missing dimensions and areas of the rectangles.

Launch

Arrange students in groups of 2. Give students time to complete the table. Follow with a brief whole-class discussion.

Activity Synthesis

Invite students to share their answers and the methods they used to solve each rectangle. 

Here are some possible questions for discussion:

  • How did you find the length when given the width and area? (Possible answer: multiply)
  • How did you find the length and width given the area? (Possible answer: factor)
  • Which rectangles represent perfect square trinomials? (Possible answers: C, G, I)
  • Which rectangles represent the difference of squares? (Possible answers: B, F, K)
  • Which rectangle(s) required grouping? (Possible answer: L)
