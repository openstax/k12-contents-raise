Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

The equation \(p=5,000\cdot2^t\) represents the population of an invasive fish species in a large lake, \(t\) years since 2005, when the fish population in the lake was first surveyed.

  1. What was the population in 2005?
    Answer: 5,000, because \(5,000\cdot 2^0=5,000\).

  2. What is this value of the population in 2005 in scientific notation?
    Answer: \(5.0\times 10^3\)

  3. For this model, what does it mean when \(t\) is –2?
    Answer: It means 2 years before 2005, which is 2003.

  4. For \( t=-2\), is the fish population more or less than 1,000? How do you know?
    Answer: More than 1,000. For \(t=-2\), \(5,000\cdot 2^{-2}=1,250\).

  5. Write the value of the fish population when \(t=-2\) in scientific notation.
    Answer: \(1.25\times 10^3 \)

Response to Student Thinking

Points to emphasize

If students struggle to interpret negative exponents, use student work from this cool-down to guide a brief discussion on the topic in a subsequent lesson.
