Launch

Graphing technology should not be used in this cool-down.

Student Facing Statement

A factory produces “widgets” and “zurls.” The combined number of widgets and zurls made each day cannot be more than 12. The maximum number of widgets the factory can produce in a day is 4.

Let \(x\) be the number of widgets and \(y\) be the number of zurls.

  1. Select all the inequalities that represent this situation.
    1. \(x<4\)
    2. \(x\leq4\)
    3. \(x>4\)
    4. \(x+y>12\)
    5. \(x+y\leq12\)
  2. Here are graphs of \(x=4\) and \(x+y=12\).
    Complete the graphs (by shading regions and adjusting line types as needed) to show all the allowable numbers of widgets and zurls that the factory can produce in one day.

  3. Does each ordered pair represent an allowable combination of widgets and zurls produced in one day?

    \((4,5)\)

    \((11,1)\)

    \((4,12)\)

    \((3,9)\)

Student Response

  1. \(x\leq4\) and \(x+y\leq12\)
  2. See graph.
  3. Yes, no, no, yes
