In questions 1-4, determine if the following points are solutions to the given system of equations.

  1. \(\left\{\begin{array}{l}2x-6y=0\\3x-4y=5\end{array}\right.\)
  • \((3,1)\)
  • \((-3,4)\)
  • \((\frac{8}{7}, \frac{6}{7})\)
  • \((1, \frac{3}{4})\)

Answer: \((3,1)\)

Substitute in the points
\((3,1)\) for \(x\) and \(y\) into each equation to see if it is a solution.
\(2(3)-6(1)=0\), \(6-6=0\), \(0=0\).
\(3(3)-4(1)=5\), \(9-4=5\), \(5=5\).

Solve questions 11-12 by graphing.

  1. A bakery charges $2 per croissant and $4 per muffin. One day, they charge $42 in total and sell 15 baked goods. How many croissants and muffins did they sell?
  1. 5 croissants and 10 muffins
  2. 9 croissants and 6 muffins
  3. 10 croissants and 5 muffins
  4. 8 croissants and 7 muffins

Answer: 9 croissants and 6 muffins

Choose variables, \(x\) for the croissants and \(y\) for the muffins.
Set up the equation: \(x+y=15\) to represent the total number of baked goods sold on this day.
Set up the equation: \(2x+4y=42\) to represent the total amount of money made on this day.
Graph the equations.
Your graph should show the equations intersect at point \((9,6)\).
\(x=9\) which represents the croissants and \(y=6\) which represents the muffins. They sold 9 croissants and 6 muffins.

  3. 7 mice and 10 gerbils
  4. 6 mice and 11 gerbils

Answer: 8 mice and 9 gerbils

Choose variables, \(x\) for the mice and \(y\) for the gerbils.
Set up the equation: \(x+y=17\) to represent the total number of animals sold on this day.
Set up the equation: \(7x+9y=137\) to represent the total amount of money made on this day.
Graph the equations.
Your graph should show the equations intersect at point \((8,9)\).
\(x=8\) which represents the mice and \(y=9\) which represents the gerbils.
They sold 8 mice and 9 gerbils.
