Creating Perfect Square Trinomials

A perfect square trinomial is created by multiplying a binomial by itself.

Example

Write \((x-2)^2\) as an expanded polynomial in standard form:

Step 1
Multiply the binomial by itself.
\((x-2)(x-2)\)

Step 2
Use the distributive property to expand (FOIL).
\(x^2-2x-2x+4\)

Step 3
Combine like terms.
\(x^2-4x+4\)
