In this lesson, you will begin to rewrite quadratic expressions from standard to factored form. You will look at expressions of the form \((x + m)(x + n)\) and \((x − m)(x − n)\) where \(m\) and \(n\) are positive.

When you finish this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Explain how the numbers in a quadratic expression in factored form relate to the numbers in an equivalent expression in standard form.
  • Rewrite a quadratic expression in standard form when given the quadratic expression in factored form.
  • Rewrite a quadratic expression in factored form when given the quadratic expression in the form of \(x^2+ bx + c\).
