The square root of a number \(x\) is another number, which when multiplied by itself a given number of times, equals \(x\).


SQUARE ROOT PROPERTY

If \(x^2 = k\), then

\(x=\sqrt{k} \quad \text { and } \quad x=-\sqrt{k} \rightarrow x=\pm \sqrt{k}\).


Each of these equations has two solutions. Use your knowledge of square roots and the square root property. What are the solutions? Explain or show your reasoning.

  1. \(n^2 + 4=404\)

Enter the solutions and your reasoning.

Compare your answer:

\(n=20\) and \(n=-20\)

If \(n^2 + 4 = 404\), then \(n^2 = 400\).
Using the square root property, \(n= \pm \sqrt{400}\).
Therefore, \(n=20\) and \(n=-20\).
