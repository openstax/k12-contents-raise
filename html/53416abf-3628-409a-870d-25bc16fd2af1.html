Farms To Grow founders Gordon Reed and Gail Myers work with Black farmers and other underserved farmers to help them keep their farms operational. Statistical models can help the farmers they work with make decisions about how to sustain their farm. They can use statistics to decide the best way to package and ship their products. They can use statistics relating to the best types of soil to use and how it affects their crop.

In this section, you will continue to study linear models. You will use technology to calculate the correlation coefficient for a set of data to quantify the goodness of fit for a linear model. The section closes with an exploration of the difference between correlation and causal relationships.

This section includes 3 lessons:

  • The Correlation Coefficient
  • Using the Correlation Coefficient
  • Causal Relationships
