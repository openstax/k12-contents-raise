Activity (20 minutes)

This activity reinforces the use of Vertical Alignment and distribution to multiply a binomial by a larger polynomial. Emphasize that the FOIL method will only work when you are multiplying two binomials, not with a trinomial or a larger polynomial.

Launch

Allow students to work individually on the exercises provided. Allow extra time to answer questions as needed.

Anticipated Misconceptions

If students are struggling with filling in the blanks to complete the polynomial multiplication, encourage them to use what they know about the Distributive Property to complete the first line. The following lines can be found by continuing to solve by distribution.

Activity Synthesis

Ask students to consider the advantages and disadvantages of each method used in the activity. Consider the following:

  • Using Vertical Alignment allows you to align like terms so that they can be easily combined into the final product.
  • How does the time spent using Vertical Alignment compare to the time spent using the Distributive Property?
  • Which method do you find easier to use to double-compare your work?

After the activity, students will answer the following question to check their understanding of the concepts explored in the activity.


QUESTION:

Multiply.

\((d+4)(2d^2-3d+5)\)

ANSWERS

FEEDBACK

\(2d^3-11d^2-17d+20\)

Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: Check the signs of the terms you found when multiplying and combining terms. The answer is \(2d^3+5d^2-7d+20\).

\(2d^3+5d^2-7d+20\)

That’s correct! Check yourself: Use the Distributive Property or Vertical Alignment to compare your work.

\(2d^3-3d^2+5d\)

Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This is only \((d)(2d^2-3d+5)\). Multiply \(2d^2-3d+5\) by 4 and combine like terms of both parts. The answer is \(2d^3+5d^2-7d+20\).

\(8d^2-12d+20\)

Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This is only \((4)(2d^2-3d+5)\). Multiply \(2d^2-3d+5\) by \(d\) and combine like terms of both parts. The answer is \(2d^3+5d^2-7d+20\).

Multiplying a Polynomial by a Polynomial

We have multiplied monomials by monomials, monomials by polynomials, and binomials by binomials. Now we’re ready to multiply a polynomial by a polynomial. Remember, FOIL will not work in this case, but we can use other representations of the Distributive Property such as Vertical Alignment or distributing individual terms or polynomials.
