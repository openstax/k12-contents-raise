Student Activity

Here is someone's solution to the equation \(3x^2−4x=20\).

Step 1
\(3x^2−4x=20\)

Step 2
\(a=3\), \(b=-4\), \(c=20\)

Step 3
\(x=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2- 4ac}}{2a}\)

Step 4
\(x=\frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2- (4)(3)(20)}}{2(3)}\)

Step 5
\(x=\frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{-16-240}}{6}\)

Step 6
\(x=\frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{-256}}{6}\)

No Solutions

Identify as many errors as you can and briefly explain each error.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer:

  • \(b\) is -4, so in Step 4, the value \(-b\) should be -(-4), which is 4.
  • In Step 1, the equation should be rewritten as \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), so the constant term \(c\) is -20, not 20. So, in Step 4, the last part of the numerator should be \(\sqrt{(-4)^2-4(3)(-20)}\).
  • In Step 4, \((-4)^2\) should be 16. Squaring a negative number gives a positive number.
  • If the earlier issues were fixed, then the values under the square root symbol would be \(16+240\) or 256.
