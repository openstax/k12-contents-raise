Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

Work with a partner. For each trinomial, explain which method you would use to factor. When you have discussed your reasoning, factor the trinomial and compare solutions.

  1. \( x^2 − 14x − 32\)

Answer: trial and error; for example, \((x − 16)(x + 2)\)

  1. \(6r^2 + 30r + 36\)

Answer: the “\(ac\)” method; for example, \(6(r + 3)(r + 2)\)

  1. \((x + 3)^2 − 9(x + 3) − 36\)

Answer: substitution; for example, \((x − 9)(x + 6)\)

Response to Student Thinking

Points to Emphasize

If students are still struggling to identify which method to use, encourage them to develop a set of questions they can pose to themselves like a checklist. For instance, “Is there a coefficient on the squared term?” or “Is there a GCF that can be factored out?” Remind them that the “ac” method works for any trinomial of the form \(ax^2 + bx + c.\)

Students will have opportunities to review and practice how to factor polynomial expressions again in Unit 8 when they use factoring to solve quadratic equations.
