1. Activity

A vendor at the Saturday Market makes $9 profit on each necklace she sells and $5 profit on each bracelet.

  1. Find a combination of necklaces and bracelets that she could sell and make:
    1. exactly $100 profit
    2. more than $100 profit

Enter your answer here:

Possible responses:

  1. 10 necklaces and 2 bracelets
  2. 0 necklaces and 21 bracelets


  1. Write an equation whose solution is the combination of necklaces \((n)\) and bracelets \((b)\) she could sell and make exactly $100 profit.

Enter your answer here:

Sample answer: \(9n+5b=100\), where n represents the number of necklaces sold and b represents the number of bracelets.


  1. Write an inequality whose solutions are the combinations of necklaces \((n)\) and bracelets \((b)\) she could sell and make more than $100 profit.

Enter your answer here:

\(9n+5b>100\)
