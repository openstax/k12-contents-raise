Here is a formula called the quadratic formula.

\(x=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2- 4ac}}{2a}\)

The formula can be used to find the solutions to any quadratic equation in the form of \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are numbers and \(a\) is not 0.

This example shows how it is used to solve \(x^2−8x+15=0\), in which \(a=\)1, \(b=-8\), and \(c=15\).

Step 1
State the original equation.
\(x^2−8x+15=0\)

Step 2
Identify the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).
\(a=1\), \(b=-8\), and \(c=15\)

Step 3
Substitute the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) into the formula.
\(x=\frac{-(-8) \pm \sqrt{(-8)^2-(4)(1)(15)}}{2(1)}\)

Step 4
Evaluate each part of the expression.
\(x=\frac{8 \pm \sqrt{64-60}}{2}\)
\(x=\frac{8 \pm \sqrt{4}}{2}\)
\(x=\frac{8 \pm 2}{2}\)

Step 5
Write each equation separately and solve.
\(x=\frac{8+2}{2}\) and \(x=\frac{8-2}{2}\)

Step 6
Simplify.
\(x=\frac{10}{2}\) and \(x=\frac{6}{2}\) 

Step 7
Find the solutions.
\(x=5\) and \(x=3\)
