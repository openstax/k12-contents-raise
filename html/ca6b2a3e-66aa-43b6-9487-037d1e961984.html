The nth Term of Arithmetic Sequences

GENERAL TERM (NTH TERM) OF AN ARITHMETIC SEQUENCE

The general term of an arithmetic sequence with first term \(a_1\) and common difference \(d\) is: \(a_n=a_1+(n-1)d\).

We will use this formula in the next example to find the 15th term of a sequence.

Find the 15th term of a sequence where the first term is 3 and the common difference is 6.

To find the fifteenth term, \(a_{15}\), use the
formula with \(a_1=3\) and \(d=6\).

\(a_n=a_1+(n−1)d\)

Substitute in the values.

\(a_{15}=3+(15−1)6\)

Simplify.

\(a_{15}=3+(14)6\)

\(a_{15}=87\)



Try It: The nth Term of Arithmetic Sequences


Find the 27th term of a sequence where the first term is 7 and the common difference is 9.

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to check your work.

Here is how to find the 27th term of the sequence:

Write the formula.

\(a_n=a_1+(n−1)d\)

Substitute \(a_1=7\), \(d=9\), \(n=27\).

\(a_{27}=7+(27-1)9\)

Simplify inside the parentheses.

\(a_{27}=7+(26)9\)

Simplify to find \(a_{27}\).

\(a_{27}=241\)
