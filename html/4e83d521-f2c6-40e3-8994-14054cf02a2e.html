  1. The following two equations are equivalent.

factored form \(y=(x+2)(x+3)\) 
standard form \(y=x^2+5x+6\) 

  1. Which equation helps find the \(x\)-intercepts most efficiently?
    • factored form
    • standard form

ANSWER: factored form

  1. Which equation helps find the \(y\)-intercept most efficiently?
    • factored form
    • standard form

ANSWER: standard form

  1. There are three equations represented on this graph:

\(y=x^2\) (black graph, at the top, opening upward)
\(y=x^2-4\)
\(y=-x^2+5\)

Use the graph to answer questions a and b.

  1. Which graph represents the equation \(y=x^2-4\)?
    • Green graph (at the bottom, opening downward)
    • Blue graph (in the middle, opening upward)

ANSWER: Blue graph (in the middle, opening upward)

  1. Which graph represents the equation \(y=-x^2+5\)?
    • Green graph (at the bottom, opening downward)
    • Blue graph (in the middle, opening upward)

ANSWER: Green graph (at the bottom, opening downward)

  1. Select two equations whose graphs have a positive \(y\)-intercept.
    • \(y=x^2+3x-2\)
    • \(y=x^2-10x\)
    • \(y=(x-1)^2\)
    • \(y=5x^2-3x-5\)
    • \(y=(x+1)(x+2)\)

ANSWER: \(y=(x-1)^2\), \(y=(x+1)(x+2)\)

  1. Which function represents the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) shifted 5 units to the right? 
    • \(f(x)=x^2+5\)
    • \(f(x)=x^2-5\)
    • \(f(x)=(x+5)^2\)
    • \(f(x)=(x-5)^2\)

ANSWER: \(f(x)=(x-5)^2\) 

  1. Select three equations whose graphs open downward.
  • \(y=(1-x)^2\)
  • \(y=1-x^2\)
  • \(y=-(x-1)^2\)
  • \(y=-1+x^2\)
  • \(y=(1-x)(x+2)\)

ANSWER: \(y=1-x^2\), \(y=-(x-1)^2\), \(y=(1-x)(x+2)\)
