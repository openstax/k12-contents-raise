In this lesson, you will compute and interpret the correlation coefficient for a bivariate, numerical data set.

When you finish this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe the strength of a relationship between two variables.

  • Use technology to find the correlation coefficient and explain what the value tells you about a linear model in everyday language.

Here are the activities that will help you reach those goals:

  • 3.5.1: Using Bivariate Data in Context
  • 3.5.2: Finding and Using Correlation Coefficient to Interpret the Strength of Linear Relationships
  • 3.5.2: Self-Check
  • 3.5.3: Using Correlation Coefficient to Describe Relationships between Two Variables
  • 3.5.3: Self-Check
  • 3.5.4: Interpreting the Sign and Numerical Value of a Correlation Coefficient
