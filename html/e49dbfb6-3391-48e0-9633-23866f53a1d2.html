Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Select three quadratic expressions written in vertex form.
  • \(y=(x-2)^2 + 1\)
  • \(y=x^2 - 4\)
  • \(y=x(x+1)\)
  • \(y=(x+3)(x+3)\)
  • \(y=(x-4)^2 + 6\)

ANSWER: \(y=(x-2)^2 + 1\), \(y=x^2 - 4\), \(y=(x-4)^2 + 6\)

Examine the following two equations and use them to answer questions 2–5. One defines function \(m\) and the other defines function \(p\).

  • \(m(x)=x(x+6)\)
  • \(p(x)=(x+3)^2−9\)
  1. How do you know that the expressions defining \(m\) and \(p\) are equivalent?
  • Both expressions are equal to \(x^2+6x\).
  • Both expressions are equal to \(x^2-6x\).
  • Both expressions are equal to \(2x+6\).
  • Both expressions are equal to \(x^2-3\).

ANSWER: Both expressions are equal to \(x^2+6x\).

  1. Recall \(m(x)=x(x+6)\) and \(p(x)=(x+3)^2−9\). What is the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph of \(m\)? 

ANSWER: -3

  1. Recall \(m(x)=x(x+6)\) and \(p(x)=(x+3)^2−9\). What is the \(y\)-coordinate of the vertex of the graph of \(m\)? 

ANSWER: -9

  1. Recall \(m(x)=x(x+6)\) and \(p(x)=(x+3)^2−9\). What are the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph of \(p\)? 
  • \((0,0)\) and \((-6,0)\)
  • \((0,0)\) and \((6,0)\)
  • \((0,0)\) and \((0,-6)\)
  • \((0,0)\) and \((0,6)\)

ANSWER: \((0,0)\) and \((-6,0)\). The graphs of \(m\) and \(p\) are the same. The zeros of \(m\) are 0 and -6, which give the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph.
