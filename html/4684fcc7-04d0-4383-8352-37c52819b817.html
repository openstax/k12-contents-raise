Interpreting Negative Exponents in Exponential Growth

Let’s look at a problem involving an exponential growth equation and negative exponents.

A forest fire has been burning for several days. The burned area, in acres, is given by the equation \(y=4800\cdot 2^d\), where \(d\) is the number of days since the area of the fire was first measured.

    To determine the value of \(y\), given \(d\), follow these steps:

    Example 1

    Determine the value for \(y\) when \(d = -2\)

    STEP 1 Substitute the given domain value for d into the expression.

    \(4800 \cdot 2^{-2}\)

    STEP 2 Evaluate the exponential value. Recall that a negative exponent represents how many times the reciprocal of the base should be multiplied.

    \(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^2 = 4800 \cdot \frac14\)

    STEP 3 Evaluate the remaining expression to determine \(y\).

    \(= 1200\)

    Example 2

    Determine the value for \(y\) when \(d = -3\)

    STEP 1 Substitute the given domain value for d into the expression.

    \(4800 \cdot 2^{-3}\)

    STEP 2 Evaluate the exponential value. Recall that a negative exponent represents how many times the reciprocal of the base should be multiplied.

    \(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^3 = 4800 \cdot \frac18\)

    STEP 3 Evaluate the remaining expression to determine \(y\).

    \(= 600\)

    Example 3

    Determine the value for \(y\) when \(d = -5\)

    STEP 1 Substitute the given domain value for d into the expression.

    \(4800 \cdot 2^{-5}\)

    STEP 2 Evaluate the exponential value. Recall that a negative exponent represents how many times the reciprocal of the base should be multiplied.

    \(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^5 = 4800 \cdot \frac{1}{32}\)

    STEP 3 Evaluate the remaining expression to determine \(y\).

    \(= 150\)

    Example 4

    What does the value of \(y\) tell you about the area burned in the fire when \(d=-1\)?

    The \(y\) value at \(d= -1\) represents the fact that 2,400 acres had burned 1 day before the first measurement was taken.

    Example 5

    How much area had the fire burned a week before it measured 4,800 acres? Explain your reasoning.

    Since there are 7 days in a week, to find the area burned a week before the fire was measured, we need to determine the value of \(y\) when \(d = -7\).

    STEP 1 Substitute the given domain value for d into the expression.

    \(4800 \cdot 2^{-7}\)

    STEP 2 Evaluate the exponential value. Recall that a negative exponent represents how many times the reciprocal of the base should be multiplied.

    \(= 4800 \cdot \frac12 ^7 = 4800 \cdot \frac{1}{128}\)

    STEP 3 Evaluate the remaining expression to determine \(y\).

    \(= 37.5\)

    \(37.5\) acres had burned a week before the fire measured \(4,800\) acres. The value is found by substituting \(-7\) for d since that represents \(7\) days before the first measurement of \(4,800\) acres.
