For 1-2, use this situation:

Jada has p pennies and n nickels that add up to more than 40 cents. She has fewer than 20 coins altogether.

1. Which two inequalities could make up a system of inequalities that represents how many pennies and nickels that Jada could have? Select two inequalities.

Correct! \(p+n<20\), \(0.01p + 0.05n >0.40\)

Try again. Revisit activity 2.14.3.

Compare your answer: \(p+n<20\), \(0.01p + 0.05n >0.40\), \({p+n<20, 0.01p + 0.05n >0.40}\).

\(p+n < 20\) represents the total number of coins. The problem states that she has fewer than 20 coins. The number of pennies plus the number of nickels added together will be less than 20 coins.

\(0.01p+0.05n > 0.40\) represents the value of each coin. The value of a penny is 1 cent (0.01) the value of a nickel is 5 cents (0.05). The number of pennies multiplied by its value of 0.01 plus the number of nickels multiplied by its value 0.05 will give the total amount of money she has. The problem states that she has more than 40 cents.
