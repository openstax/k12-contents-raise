Solving Equations and Creating Equivalent Equations

One way to create equivalent equations is to solve the equation. In each step of solving, an equivalent equation is created.

Here is a general strategy for solving linear equations:

Step 1 - Simplify each side of the equation as much as possible.

Use the Distributive Property to remove any parentheses.
Combine like terms.

Step 2 - Collect all the variable terms on one side of the equation.

Use the Addition or Subtraction Property of Equality.

Step 3 - Collect all the constant terms on the other side of the equation.

Use the Addition or Subtraction Property of Equality.

Step 4 - Make the coefficient of the variable term equal to 1.

Use the Multiplication or Division Property of Equality.
State the solution to the equation.

Step 5 - Check the solution.

Substitute the solution into the original equation to make sure the result is a true statement.


Example 1

Step 1 - Simplify each side of the equation as much as possible.

Use the Distributive Property to remove any parentheses.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}-6(x+3)&=&24 \\ -6x-18&=&24 \end{array}\)

Step 2 - Collect all the variable terms on one side of the equation.

Nothing to do. All the variables of \(x\) are on the left side.

Step 3 - Collect all the constant terms on the other side of the equation.

Use the Addition or Subtraction Property of Equality.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}-6x-18 {\style{color:red}+} {\style{color:red}1}{\style{color:red}8}&=&24 {\style{color:red}+} {\style{color:red}1}{\style{color:red}8} \\ -6x &=&42\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Make the coefficient of the variable term equal to 1.

Use the Multiplication or Division Property of Equality.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\frac{-6x}{6}&=&\frac{42}{-6} \\ x&=&7 \end{array}\)

Step 5 - Check the solution.

Substitute the solution into the original equation to make sure the result is a true statement.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}-6(x+3)&=&24 \\ -6({\style{color:red}-}{\style{color:red}7}+3)&=&24 \\ -6(-4)&=&24 \\24&=&24 \checkmark \end{array}\)


Example 2

Find the solution for \(3(4x - 1) - 2 = 8x + 3\).

Step 1 - Simplify each side of the equation as much as possible.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}3(4x - 1) - 2&=&8x + 3 \\ 12x - 3 - 2&=&8x + 3 \\ 12x - 5&=&8x + 3 \\\end{array}\)

Step 2 - Collect all the variable terms on one side of the equation.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}12x - 5 - 8x&=&8x + 3 - 8x \\ 4x - 5 &=& 3\\\end{array}\)

Step 3 - Collect all the constant terms on the other side of the equation.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}4x - 5 + 5 &=& 3 + 5 \\ 4x &=& 8\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Make the coefficient of the variable term equal to 1.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\frac{4x}{4} &=& \frac84 \\ x &=& 2\end{array}\)

Step 5 - Check the solution.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}3(4x - 1) - 2&=&8x + 3 ? \\ 3(4(2) - 1) - 2&=&8(2) + 3 ? \\ 3(8 - 1) - 2&=&16 + 3 ? \\ 3(7) - 2&=&19 ? \\ 21 - 2&=&19 ? \\ 19&=&19 \checkmark\end{array}\)


Example 3

Solve \(12x + 3(x + 7) = 10x - 24\).

Step 1 - Simplify each side of the equation as much as possible.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}12x + 3(x + 7) = 10x - 24 \\ 12x + 3x + 21 = 10x - 24 \\ 15x + 21 = 10x - 24 \end{array}\)

Step 2 - Collect all the variable terms on one side of the equation.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}15x + 21 -10x&=&10x-24-10x \\ 5x + 21&=&- 24\end{array}\)

Step 3 - Collect all the constant terms on the other side of the equation.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}5x+21-21&=&-24-21 \\ 5x&=&-45\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Make the coefficient of the variable term equal to 1.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\frac{5x}{5}&=&-\frac{45}{5} \\ x &=& -9\end{array}\)

Step 5 - Check the solution.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}12x + 3(x + 7)&=&10x - 24 ? \\ 12(-9) + 3(-9 + 7)&=&10(-9) - 24 ? \\ -108 + 3(-2)&=&-90 - 24 ? \\ -108 + -6&=&-86 ? \\ -114&=&-114 \checkmark\end{array}\)
