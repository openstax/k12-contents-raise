Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For #1–3, match each sequence with one of the definitions. Note that only the part of the definition showing the relationship between the current term and the previous term is given so as not to give away the solutions.

  1. 6, 12, 18, 24

ANSWER: \(c(n)=c(n−1)+6\)

  1.  2, 14, 98, 686

ANSWER: \(a(n)=7 \cdot a(n−1)\)

  1. 160, 80, 40, 20

ANSWER: \(b(n)=\frac12 b(n−1)\)

For #4–5, select the first five terms of each sequence. Determine whether each sequence is arithmetic, geometric, or neither.

  1. \(a(1)=7\), \(a(n)=a(n−1)−3\) for \(n\geq2\)
    1. 7, 4, 1, –2, –5, geometric 
    2. 7, –21, 63, –189, geometric
    3. 7, –21, 63, –189, arithmetic
    4. d. 7, 4, 1, –2, –5, arithmetic

ANSWER: d. 7, 4, 1, –2, –5, arithmetic

  1. \( c(1)=3\), \(c(n)=10 \cdot c(n−1)\) for \(n\geq2\)
    1. a. 3, 30, 300, 3000, 30000, arithmetic
    2. b. 3, 30, 300, 3000, 30000, geometric
    3. c. 3, 13, 23, 33, 43, geometric
    4. d. 3, 13, 23, 33, 43, arithmetic

 ANSWER: b. 3, 30, 300, 3000, 30000, geometric
