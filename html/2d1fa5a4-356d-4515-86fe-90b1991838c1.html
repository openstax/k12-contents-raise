To help students’ understanding of key points from the lesson, discuss each of the following ways to factor trinomials:

  • To factor a trinomial of the form \(x^2 + bx + c\), undo FOIL to find the binomial factors:
    • Write the factors as two binomials with the first terms \(x\).
    • Find two numbers that multiply to \(c\) and add to \(b\).
    • Use these numbers as the last terms of the binomial factors.
    • Check by multiplying the factors together.
  • To factor a trinomial of the form \(ax^2 + bx + c\), use the trial and error method:
    • Factor any GCF.
    • Find all the factor pairs of the first term.
    • Find all the factor pairs of the third term.
    • Test all the possible combinations of the factors until the correct product is found.
    • Check by multiplying the factors together.
  • For another way to factor a trinomial of the form \(ax^2 + bx + c\), use the “\(ac\)” method:
    • This method works on any trinomial of the form \(ax^2 + bx + c\).
    • Factor any GCF.
    • Find the product \(ac\).
    • Find two numbers, \(m\) and \(n\), that multiply to \(ac\) and add to \(b\).
    • Split the middle term using \(m\) and \(n\): \(ax^2 + mx + nx + c\).
    • Factor by grouping.
    • Check by multiplying the factors together.
  • To factor a polynomial that does not appear to be written in the form \(ax^2 + bx + c\), use substitution:
    • Factor any GCF.
    • Substitute \(u\) for a common expression in the first terms.
    • Factor as if the trinomial is of the form \(ax^2 + bx + c\).
    • Replace \(u\) with the original value.
