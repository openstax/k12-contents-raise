For questions 1-3, determine whether each ordered pair is a solution to the inequality \(y\;\gt\;x − 1.\)

  1. \((0,1)\)

Answer: Yes

\(1 > 0 − 1\)
\(1 > − 1\)

  1. Which inequality represents the graph?


  • \( y \leq 3x − 4 \)
  • \( y < 3x − 4 \)
  • \( y > 3x − 4 \)
  • \( y \geq 3x − 4 \)

Answer: \( y < 3x − 4 \)

The graph has a dotted line, so any answers that have \(\geq\) or\(\leq\) can be eliminated.
The two remaining options are \(y>3x−4\) and \(y>3x−4\).
If we set \(x\) to 0, we can see that the \(y\)-coordinate is -4. Look at the shading in the graph to determine if \(y>-4\) or \(y>-4\). The graph is shaded to the right and down so \(y>-4\). Therefore the equation \(y>3x−4\) is the correct answer.

  1. Consider the linear equation \(2y − 3x = 5\). The pair \((-1,1)\) is a solution to the equation. Which of the following is also a solution to the equation?
