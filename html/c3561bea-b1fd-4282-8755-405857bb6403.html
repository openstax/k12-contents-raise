Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Here is the graph of quadratic function \(f\).

Andre uses the expression \((x−5)^2+7\) to define \(f\).

Noah uses the expression \((x+5)^2−7\) to define \(f\).

Do you agree with either of them? 

Answer: No. The vertex of the graph is \((5,-7)\). The graph of Andre’s function would have a vertex at \((5,7)\), and the graph of Noah’s function would have a vertex at \((-5,-7)\).

  1. Here are the graphs of \(f(x)=x^2\), \(f(x)=x^2−5\), and \(f(x)=(x+2)^2−8\).

    1. How do the three graphs compare?
      • They all have the same vertex.
      • The shape is the same but they are in different locations.
      • They all have a negative a coefficient in the vertex form of the equation.
      • All three graphs contain the point \((0,0)\). 

Answer: The shape is the same but they are in different locations.

    1. Compare the graphs of \(f(x)=x^2\) and \(f(x)=x^2−5\). What role does the -5 play in the comparison?
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph left by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph right by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph up by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 5 units.

Answer: Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 5 units.

    1. Compare the graphs of \(f(x)=x^2\) and \(f(x)=(x+2)^2−8\). What roles do the \(+2\) and \(-8\) play in the comparison?
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) to the left by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) to the up by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph left by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) to the right by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph up by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) to the down by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph right by 8 units.

Answer: Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(f(x)=x^2\) to the left by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 8 units.

  1. The graph representing \(f(x)=x^2\) is shifted 4 units to the left, 16 units down, and flipped so it opens downward (reflects over the \(x\)-axis). Which equation defines the curve?
  • \(f(x)=(x+4)^2-16\)
  • \(f(x)=-(x-4)^2-16\)
  • \(f(x)=-(x+4)^2-16\)
  • \(f(x)=(x-4)^2-16\)

Answer: \(f(x)=-(x+4)^2-16\)

  1. The graph of \(f(x) = x^2\) is transformed by a horizontal dilation of \(\frac12\). What is the equation of the function that results from this translation? 
  • \(f(x) =\frac 12 x^2\)
  • \(f(x) = x^2 + \frac12\)
  • \(f(x) = (x-\frac 12)^2\)
  • \(f(x) = (\frac12x)^2\) 

Answer: \(f(x) = (\frac12x)^2\) 

  1. A function \(g(x) = (2x)^2 - 5\) was transformed from the parent quadratic function. Which of the following answer choices describes the transformations that were applied? 

Select the two descriptions that apply.

  • A horizontal shift 2 units to the right
  • A horizontal dilation by a factor of 2
  • A horizontal shift left 5 units
  • A vertical shift down 5 units
  • A vertical dilation by a factor of 2

Answer: A horizontal dilation by a factor of 2 and A vertical shift down 5 units
