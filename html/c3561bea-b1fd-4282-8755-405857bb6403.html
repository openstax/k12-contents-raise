Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. Here is the graph of quadratic function \(f\).

Andre uses the expression \((x−5)^2+7\) to define \(f\).

Noah uses the expression \((x+5)^2−7\) to define \(f\).

Do you agree with either of them? 

ANSWER: No. The vertex of the graph is \((5,-7)\). The graph of Andre’s function would have a vertex at \((5,7)\), and the graph of Noah’s function would have a vertex at \((-5,-7)\).

  1. Here are the graphs of \(y=x^2\), \(y=x^2−5\), and \(y=(x+2)^2−8\).

    1. How do the three graphs compare?
      • They all have the same vertex.
      • The shape is the same but they are in different locations.
      • They all have a negative a coefficient in the vertex form of the equation.
      • All three graphs contain the point \((0,0)\). 

ANSWER: The shape is the same but they are in different locations.

    1. Compare the graphs of \(y=x^2\) and \(y=x^2−5\). What role does the -5 play in the comparison?
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph left by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph right by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph up by 5 units.
      • Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 5 units.

ANSWER: Subtracting 5 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 5 units.

    1. Compare the graphs of \(y=x^2\) and \(y=(x+2)^2−8\). What roles do the \(+2\) and \(-8\) play in the comparison?
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(y=x^2\) to the left by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(y=x^2\) to the up by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph left by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(y=x^2\) to the right by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph up by 8 units.
      • Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(y=x^2\) to the down by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph right by 8 units.

ANSWER: Adding 2 to \(x\) before squaring shifts the graph of \(y=x^2\) to the left by 2 units and subtracting 8 from the squared term shifts the graph down by 8 units.

  1. Select three equations with a graph whose vertex has both a positive \(x\) and a positive \(y\).
    • \(y=x^2\)
    • \(y=(x−1)^2\)
    • \(y=(x−3)^2+2\)
    • \(y=2(x−4)^2−5\)
    • \(y=0.5(x+2)^2+6\)
    • \(y=-(x−4)^2+3\)
    • \(y=-2(x−3)^2+1\)

ANSWER: \(y=(x−3)^2+2\), \(y=-(x−4)^2+3\), \(y=-2(x−3)^2+1\)

  1. Which equation represents the graph shown?



    • \(y=-(x+7)^2+3\)
    • \(y=-(x-7)^2+3\)
    • \(y=(x+7)^2+3\)
    • \(y=(x-7)^2+3\)

ANSWER: \(y=-(x+7)^2+3\)

  1. The graph representing \(y=x^2\) is shifted 4 units to the left, 16 units down, and flipped so it opens downward (reflects over the \(x\)-axis). Which equation defines the curve?
    • \(y=(x+4)^2-16\)
    • \(y=-(x-4)^2-16\)
    • \(y=-(x+4)^2-16\)
    • \(y=(x-4)^2-16\)

ANSWER: \(y=-(x+4)^2-16\)
