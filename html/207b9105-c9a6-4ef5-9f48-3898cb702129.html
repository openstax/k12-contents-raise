6. Now, write the equation of a line perpendicular to \( y = 3x -1 \) that goes through \( (1,2) \).

Enter your answer here:

Check your answer:

\( y= -\frac{1}{3}x+\frac{7}{3} \) is an equation of a line that is perpendicular to \( y = 3x -1 \) and goes through \( (1,2) \).

First, find the slope of the given line: \( m = 3 \).

Next, find the opposite reciprocal of this slope: \( m = - \frac{1}{3} \).

This will be the slope of the perpendicular line. 

Now use this slope, \( m = - \frac{1}{3} \) and the given point, \( (1,2) \) to write an equation of a line with point-slope form: \( y - 2 = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 1) \).

Simplify and write in slope-intercept form: \( y= -\frac{1}{3}x+\frac{7}{3} \)

\( y= -\frac{1}{3}x+\frac{7}{3} \)
