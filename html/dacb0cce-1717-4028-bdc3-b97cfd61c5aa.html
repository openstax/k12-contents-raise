Try It: Solving a System of Equations by Elimination Involving Fractions

Solve the following system of equations.

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac13\;x\;-\;\frac12y\;=\;1\\\frac34x\;\;-\;y\;=\;\frac52\end{array}\right.\)

Write down your answer. Then select the Solution button to check your work.

Here is how to solve this system of equations by elimination:

Write both equations in standard form. If any coefficients are fractions, clear them.

\(\frac13x-\frac12y=1\\\frac34x-y=\frac52\)

To clear the fractions, multiply each equation by its LCD.

\({\style{color:red}6}(\frac13x-\frac12y) ={\style{color:red}6}(1)\\{\style{color:red}4}(\frac34x-y)={\style{color:red}4}(\frac52)\)

Simplify.

\(2 x − 3 y = 6\)
\(3 x − 4 y = 10\)

To get equivalent coefficients of x, we will need to multiply the first equation by 3 and the second equation by 2.

\({\style{color:red}3} (2 x – 3 y ) = {\style{color:red}3} ( 6 ) \)
\({\style{color:red}2} (3 x – 4 y ) = {\style{color:red}2} ( 10 )\)

Simplify.

\(6 x – 9 y = 18\)
\(6 x – 8 y = 20\)

Subtract the equations to eliminate one variable.

 \(\begin{array}{r}6x\;–\;9y\;=\;18\\6x\;–\;8y\;=\;20\\\overline{-y\;=\;-2}\end{array}\)

Solve for the remaining variable.

\(– y = – 2\)
\( y = 2\)

Substitute the solution into any of the equations. Then solve for the other variable.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}2\;x\;–\;3\;y\;&=&\;6\\2\;x\;–\;3\;{\style{color:red}(}\;{\style{color:red}2}\;{\style{color:red})}\;&=&\;6\\2\;x\;–\;6\;&=&\;6\\2\;x\;&=&\;12\\x\;&=&\;6\\&&\end{array}\)

Write the solution as an ordered pair.

\(( 6 , 2 )\)

Check that the ordered pair is a solution to both original equations.

\begin{array}{rcl}\;\;\frac13x\;–\;\frac12y\;&=&\;1\\\frac13{\style{color:red}(}{\style{color:red}6}{\style{color:red})}\;–\;\frac12{\style{color:red}(}{\style{color:red}2}{\style{color:red})}\;&=&\;1\\2\;–\;1\;&=&\;1\\1\;&=&\;1{\style{color:red}✔}\;\\&&\end{array}

\begin{array}{rcl}\;\;\frac34x\;–\;y\;&=&\;\frac52\\\frac34{\style{color:red}(}{\style{color:red}6}{\style{color:red})}\;–\;{\style{color:red}(}{\style{color:red}2}{\style{color:red})}\;&=&\;\frac52\\\frac{18}4\;–\;2\;&=&\;\frac52\\\frac52\;&=&\;\frac52{\style{color:red}✔}\;\\&&\end{array}
