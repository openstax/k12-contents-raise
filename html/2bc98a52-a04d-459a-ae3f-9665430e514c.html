Student Activity 

  1. Answer the following questions for expression \(4x^2−28x+c\).
  1. Find the value of \(c\) to make the expression a perfect square.

Answer: 49
Standard form: \(4x^2−28x+49\).

  1. Write an equivalent expression in the form of squared factors.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer: Squared factors: \((2x−7)^2\).

  1. Solve the equation \(4x^2−28x=-33\) by completing the square. Show your reasoning.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer: 

\(\begin{aligned} 4 x^{2}-28 x &=-33 \\ 4 x^{2}-28 x+49 &=-33+49 \\ (2 x-7)^{2} &=16 \\ \sqrt{(2 x-7)^{2}} &=\sqrt{16} \\ 2 x-7 &=4 \\ 2 x-7 &=-4 \\ 2 x &=11 \\ 2 x &=3 \end{aligned}\)

\(x=\frac{11}{2} \text { and } x=\frac{3}{2}\)
