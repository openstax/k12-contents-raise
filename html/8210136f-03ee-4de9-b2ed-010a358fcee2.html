Real-World Applications

In this unit, your student will be learning how to solve quadratic equations using several methods. In the previous unit, students saw how quadratic functions can represent a variety of situations such as the height of a ball thrown into the air over time.

Look at this example.

A ball is thrown straight up, from 3 meters above the ground, with a velocity of 14 meters/second. When does it hit the ground?

The equation \(h = - 5t^2 + 14t + 3\) represents the height, \(h\), for time, \(t\), in seconds since the ball was thrown.

\(h = - 5t^2 +14t + 3\)

And the ball will hit the ground when the height is zero:

\(- 5t^2 + 14t + 3 = 0\)

In this unit, your student will learn to use the quadratic formula. The formula can be used to find the solutions to any quadratic equation in the form of \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are numbers and \(a\) is not 0.

Here is the quadratic formula.

\(x=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2- 4ac}}{2a}\)

The quadratic formula can be used to find the time when the ball hits the ground by solving \(- 5t^2 +14t + 3 = 0\), in which \(a=-5\), \(b=14\), and \(c=3\).

Substitute the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) into the formula.

\(x=\frac{-(14) \pm \sqrt{(14)^2- (4)(-5)(3)}}{2(-5)}\)

Evaluate each part of the expression.

\(x=\frac{-14 \pm \sqrt{196+ 60}}{-10}\)

\(x=\frac{-14 \pm \sqrt{256}}{-10}\)

\(x=\frac{-14 \pm 16}{-10}\)

Write each equation separately.

\(x=\frac{-14+16}{-10}\) and \(x=\frac{-14-16}{-10}\)

Simplify.

\(x=\frac{2}{-10}=-\frac{1}{5}\) and \(x=\frac{-30}{-10}=3\)

Find the solution(s).

The equation has two solutions, \(x=-\frac{1}{5}\) and \(x=3\). The solution \(x=-\frac{1}{5}\) does not make sense for this problem because \(x\) represents time and time cannot be negative. The solution \(x=3\) means the ball hits the ground 3 seconds after being thrown in the air. The solution is \(x=3\).

Here is a task to try with your student:

Solve \(2x^2-13x+20=0\) using the quadratic equation.

Solution:

\(a=2\), \(b=-13\), and \(c=20\).

\(x=\frac{-(-13) \pm \sqrt{(-13)^2- (4)(2)(20)}}{2(2)}\)

\(x=\frac{13 \pm \sqrt{169- 160}}{4}\)

\(x=\frac{13 \pm 3}{4}\)

\(x=\frac{13+3}{4}=4\) and \(x=\frac{13-3}{4}=2.5\)

The solutions are \(x=4\) and \(x=2.5\).
