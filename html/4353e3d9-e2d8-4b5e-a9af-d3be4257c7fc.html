  1. Explain or show your reasoning why you agree or disagree with Kiran.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Compare your Answer:

You should disagree with Kiran.

  • The vertex of the graph of the original equation \(f(x)=x^2+2x+1\) is at \((-1,0)\). The vertex of \(f(x)=(x−5)^2+2x+1\) is at \((4,10)\).
  • Evaluating \(f(x)=(x−5)^2+2x+1\) at \(x = 4\) gives \(f(4)=10\), not \(f(4)=0\).
  • The original equation is not in vertex form, so changing \(x^2\) into \((x−5)^2\) does not change the location of the vertex 5 units to the right.
  1. \(f(x)=(x+4)^2-1\) is a parabola with a vertex at \((-4,1)\) that opens upward.
  1. If we multiply \(f(x)\) by 2, we get \(2f(x)=2[(x+4)^2-1] = 2(x+4)^2-2\). How does this change the graph?

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Compare your Answer:

The parabola is narrower and the vertex is lower. The \(x\)-intercepts stay the same.
