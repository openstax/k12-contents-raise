Warm Up (5 minutes)

This warm-up is an opportunity to practice interpreting statements in function notation. It also draws attention to statements that correspond to the intercepts of a graph of a function (for instance, \(d(0)\) and \(d(m)=0\)), preparing students to reason about them in the lesson (particularly in the second activity).

Student Facing Statement

Diego is walking home from school at a constant rate. This graph represents function \(d\), which gives his distance from home, in kilometers, \(m\) minutes since leaving the school.

Use the graph to find or estimate:

  1. What is \(d(0)\), and what does it represent in the situation?

ANSWER: \(d(0)=2.25\); The moment Diego is leaving school, he is 2.25 kilometers from home (or Diego’s school is a little more than 2 kilometers from his house).

  1. \(d(12) =\)

ANSWER: \(d(12)=0.9\)

  1. What does \(d(m)=1\) mean in this context, and what is the value of \(m\)?

ANSWER: \(m\) is approximately 10.5. At approximately 10.5 minutes, Diego is 1 kilometer from home.

  1. What does \(d(m)=0\) mean in this context, and what is the value of \(m\)?

ANSWER: \(m\) is 20. At 20 minutes, Diego is at home (or it takes Diego 20 minutes to walk the 2.25 kilometers home from school).

Activity Synthesis

Ask students to interpret each statement in function notation before soliciting their response to each question. Make sure students understand, for instance, that \(d(0)\) represents Diego’s distance from school at the time of leaving (or at 0 minutes), and \(d(m)=0\) represents his distance from school being 0 km, \(m\) minutes after leaving his house.

If time permits, discuss with students:

  • “Is the relationship between Diego’s distance from school and time a linear function? How can we tell?” (Yes. The graph is a line, which means the function’s value changes at a constant rate.)
  • “Can we tell from the graph how far away Diego’s house is from school? How?” (Yes. From the graph, we can see that at the time he leaves school, he 2.25 km from home.)
  • “Can we tell from the graph how long it took Diego to get home? How?” (Yes. From the graph, we can see the distance reaching 0 when the time is 20 minutes.)
  • “Why does the graph slant downward (or have a negative slope)?” (As the input, \(m\), increases, the output, \(d(m)\), decreases.)
