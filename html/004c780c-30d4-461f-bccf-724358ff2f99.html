Simplify Expressions Using Properties of Exponents

In the expression \(y^4 \cdot y^2\) :

\(y^4=y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y\)

The exponent 4 means there are 4 factors of \(y\).

\(y^2=y \cdot y\)

The exponent 2 means there are 2 factors of \(y\).

\(y^4 \cdot y^2=y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y\).

There are 6 factors of \(y\), which can be written as \(y^6\).

\(y^4 \cdot y^2=y^6\)

Notice the exponent is the sum of the exponents of the factors.

When you multiply exponential expressions with the same base, you keep the base and add the exponents.

PRODUCT PROPERTY FOR EXPONENTS

If \(a\) is a real number and \(m\) and \(n\) are integers, then

\(a^m \cdot a^n=a^{m+n}\)

To multiply with like bases, add the exponents.


In the expression \(\frac {y^5} {y^3}\):

\(y^5=y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y\)

The exponent 5 means there are 5 factors of \(y\).

\(y^3=y \cdot y \cdot y\)

The exponent 3 means there are 3 factors of \(y\).

y 5 y 3 = y · y · y · y · y y · y · y = y · y

There are 2 factors of \(y\), which can be written as \(y^2\).

\(\frac {y^5}{y^3}=y^2\)

Notice the exponent is the difference of the exponents of the factors.

When you divide exponential expressions with the same base, you keep the base and subtract the exponents.


QUOTIENT PROPERTY FOR EXPONENTS

If \(a\) is a real number, \(a\neq0\), and \(m\) and \(n\) are integers, then

\(\frac{a^m}{a^n}=a^{m-n}\), \(m>n\) and \(\frac{a^m}{a^n}=\frac1{a^{n-m}}\), \(n>m\)


Here’s how to simplify expressions using properties of exponents.

  1. \(x^2 \cdot x^3\)

    2. The bases are the same. To multiply, use the Product Property for Exponents.

    \(x^2 \cdot x^3=x^{2+3}=x^5\). To multiply with like bases, add the exponents.

  2. \(y \cdot y^4\)

    3. The bases are the same. To multiply, use the Product Property for Exponents.

    Remember: A variable with no exponent shown has an exponent of \(1\), \(y=y^1\).

    \(y \cdot y^4=y^1 \cdot y^4=y^{1+4}=y^5\). To multiply with like bases, add the exponents.

  3. \(\frac {x^5}{x^2}\)

    4. The bases are the same. To divide, use the Quotient Property of Exponents.

    \(\frac {x^5}{x^2}=x^{5-2}=x^3\). To divide with like bases, subtract the exponents.

  4. \(\frac {x^4yz}{x^2y^2z}\)

    5. The expression contains like bases. Use the Quotient Property of Exponents.

    \(\frac{x^4yz}{x^2y^2z}=\frac{x^4y^1z^1}{x^2y^2z^1}=\frac{x^{4-2}z^{1-1}}{y^{2-1}}=\frac{x^2z^0}{y^1}=\frac{x^2}y\). To divide with like bases, subtract the exponents.

    Remember: Any nonzero number to the zero power is 1.

  2. \(\frac {x^9}{x^3}=x^{9-3}=x^6\). To divide with like bases, subtract the exponents.
