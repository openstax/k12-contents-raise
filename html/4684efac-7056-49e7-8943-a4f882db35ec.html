Cool Down (5 minutes)

Launch

Since this activity was designed to be completed without technology, ask students to put away any devices.

Student Activity

Here are graphs that represent three quadratic functions, defined by:

\(f(x)=x^2-4\)
\(g(x)=1-x^2\)
\(h(x)=x^2+4\)

  1. Which graph represents the equation \(f(x)=x^2-4\)?
  • Green Graph A
  • Blue Graph B
  • Black Graph C

ANSWER: Blue Graph B

  1. Which graph represents the equation \(g(x)=1-x^2\)?
  • Green Graph A
  • Blue Graph B
  • Black Graph C

ANSWER: Black Graph C

  1. Which graph represents the equation \(h(x)=x^2+4\)?
  • Green Graph A
  • Blue Graph B
  • Black Graph C

ANSWER: Green Graph A

  1. Which part of the equation tells us that Graph C opens downward?

ANSWER: \(y=1-x^2\) is the equation. The negative coefficient of \(x^2\) makes the graph open downward.
