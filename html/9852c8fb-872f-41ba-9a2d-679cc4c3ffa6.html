Activity (20 minutes)

In this activity, students encounter quadratic expressions in factored form, where at least one of the factors is a difference. Monitor for students who

  • find it useful to reason with a diagram, such as a rectangular diagram
  • transfer what they learned when multiplying two sums in the preceding lesson—that \((x+p)(x+q)\) is equivalent to \(x^2+(p+q)x+pq\)—and use negative numbers for \(p\) and \(q\)

Either approach is welcome, but it is important to transition students from using rectangular diagrams as a way to organize the terms of the factors to directly applying the distributive property to multiply binomials.

Students see that \((x-1)(x-1)\) can be expressed as \((x+-1)(x+-1)\), so the sides of the diagram can be labeled accordingly. The negative values in the expressions are not problematic when the expressions do not represent side lengths of a rectangle and the partial products don’t represent areas.

Launch

Remind students we have used area diagrams to expand expressions such as \(x(x+5)\) and write equivalent expressions. When dealing with negative numbers, however, thinking in terms of finding area isn’t very helpful. Explain that we can still draw rectangular diagrams, but use them to organize the two factors and the results of applying the distributive property. Note that in Unit 6, students learned how to multiply binomial expressions and square a binomial. They may choose to show that expressions are equivalent algebraically, through strategies like FOIL, rather than by drawing a diagram.

For example, this diagram shows that \(x(x+5)=x^2+5x\)


\(x\) \(5\)
\(x\) \(x^2\) \(5x\)

Explain that such diagrams can be used even when subtraction is involved. To represent \(x(x-5)\), we can rewrite it as \(x(x+-5)\) then label the diagram as follows:


\(x\) \(-5\)
\(x\) \(x^2\) \(-5x\)

The diagram shows that \(x(x-5)=x(x+-5)=x^2+-5x=x^2-5x\)

Arrange students in groups of two. Give students a moment of quiet time to think about the first question, and then ask them to discuss their response with a partner before continuing to the second question.

Anticipated Misconceptions

Some students will write the expression in the last question as \(x^2-2^2\). Remind them that just as \(x^2\) means \(x \cdot x\), the expression \((x-2)^2\) means \((x-2)(x-2)\).

7.9.2: Self Check

After the activity, students will answer the following question to check their understanding of the concepts explored in the activity.

QUESTION:

Which of the following expressions is equivalent to \((x-2)^2\)?

Answers Feedback
\(x^2-4\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This is the square of the first and last term. Remember \((x-2)^2=(x-2)(x-2)\). Distribute to multiply the binomials. The answer is \(x^2-4x+4\).
\(x^2+4\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This is the square of the first and last term. Remember \((x-2)^2=(x-2)(x-2)\). Distribute to multiply the binomials. The answer is \(x^2-4x+4\).
\(x^2-4x+4\) That’s correct! Check yourself: Multiply \((x-2)(x-2)\) and distribute. The answer becomes \(x^2-2x-2x+4\). Combining like terms gives \(x^2-4x+4\).

Multiplying Binomials with Negative Numbers

In a previous lesson, you learned how to square a binomial.

\((a+b)^2=a^2+2ab+b^2\) and \((a-b)^2=a^2-2ab+b^2\)

You can use this to solve the following example.

Example 1

Find the expression equivalent to \((x-3)^2\).

This means to multiply \((x-3)\) by \((x-3)\). So, \((x-3)^2=(x-3)(x-3)\).

Now, distribute. Remember, you can use FOIL.

Distribute the \(x\) to the second binomial, then distribute the \(– 3\) to the second binomial.

Then, combine like terms.

The expression becomes \(x^2-3x-3x+9=x^2-6x+9\).

Example 2

Find the expression equivalent to \((x-4)(x+2)\).

First, distribute. Remember, you can use FOIL.

Multiply the \(x\) by \((x+2)\), then multiply the \(– 4\) by \((x+2)\) and combine like terms:

\(x^2+2x-4x-8=x^2-2x+8\).

Try It: Multiplying Binomials with Negative Numbers

Find the expression equivalent to \((x+5)(x-3)\).

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Solution

Here is how to find an equivalent expression:

Distribute the \(x\) to \((x-3)\), and then distribute the \(5\) to \((x-3)\). Remember, you can use FOIL.

\(x^2-3x+5x-15\)

Next, combine like terms.

\(x^2+2x-15\)
