Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

Acetaminophen is a common pain reliever and fever reducer. Here is a graph showing the amount of acetaminophen, in milligrams, in an adult’s body at different times after they take a normal dose.

    1. What is the vertical intercept?

      Answer: 1,000

    2. What does it mean in this situation?

      Answer: There are 1,000 mg of acetaminophen in the person’s body after taking the medicine (this is the dose size).

  2. What fraction of the medicine remained after one hour?

    Answer: \(\frac34\) of the medicine remained after 1 hour since \(\frac{750}{1000}=\frac{3}{4}\).

  3. Write an equation that represents the amount of medicine, \(a\), after \(t\) hours.

    Answer: \(a=1,000(\frac{3}{4})^t\)

Response to Student Thinking

Points to emphasize

If students are still struggling with writing equations to represent exponential growth or decay, highlight a few examples from cool-downs and use practice problems from this lesson to provide further opportunities to examine the meaning of \(a\) and \(b\) terms and to practice writing equations to represent exponential decay.
