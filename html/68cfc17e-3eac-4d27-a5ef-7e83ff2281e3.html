Lesson Narrative

In a previous lesson, students learned that the solutions to a system of linear inequalities can be represented graphically with overlapping regions.

In this lesson, students take a closer look at whether points on the boundary lines of the system’s solution region are included in the solutions. Analyzing graphs and communicating observations about them require attention to precision. Students also apply these insights to solve more challenging contextual problems. This work involves making sense of the information needed to solve the problems.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Analyze given information about a situation involving multiple constraints to determine what additional information is needed to solve problems.
  • Explain (orally and in writing) how to tell if a pair of values is a solution to the system, given a system of inequalities and their graphs.
  • Practice writing systems of inequalities in two variables and finding the solution sets by reasoning or by graphing.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Explain how to tell if a point on the boundary of the graph of the solutions to a system of inequalities is a solution.
