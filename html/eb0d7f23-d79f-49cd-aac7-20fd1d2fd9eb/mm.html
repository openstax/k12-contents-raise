Warm Up Activity

Tickets to a play cost $12, and for each purchase there is a $2.50 fee. The expression \(12t + 2.50\) represents the cost to purchase tickets for the play, where \(t\) is the number of tickets. Be prepared to explain your response to each question.


  1. A family paid $62.50 for tickets. How many tickets were bought?

Enter the number of tickets.

Compare your answer:

Five tickets were bought. Subtracting 2.50 from 62.50 gives 60. If \(12t\) is 60, then \(t\) is 5.

  1. A teacher paid $278.50 for tickets for her students. How many tickets were bought?

Enter the number of tickets.

Compare your answer:

Twenty-three tickets were bought. Subtracting 2.50 from 278.50 gives 276. If \(12t\) is 276, then \(t\) is 23.
