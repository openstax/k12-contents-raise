Writing Equations Using Graphs in Situations 

An equation that contains two unknown quantities or two quantities that vary is called an equation in two variables. A solution to such an equation is a pair of numbers that makes the equation true. 

Suppose Tyler spends $45 on T-shirts and socks. A T-shirt costs $10, and a pair of socks costs $2.50. If \( t \) represents the number of T-shirts and \( p \) represents the number of pairs of socks that Tyler buys, what is an equation that represents the situation?

The cost is $10 per T-shirt \( (10t) \) plus $2.50 per pair of socks \( (2.50p) \), which equals $45. 

\( 10t +2.50p = 45 \)

Let’s look at the graph of this equation: 

What does the point \( (2, 10) \) mean in this graph in relation to the situation?

Tyler bought 2 T-shirts and 10 pairs of socks for $45.

What does the point \( (4, 6) \) mean on this graph? 

If Tyler bought 4 T-shirts and 6 pairs of socks, it would cost MORE than $45.

Try It: Writing Equations Using Graphs in Situations 

Use Desmos or a graphing calculator to create a graph for \( 40x +20y = 180 \). 

If \(x\) represents the number of pairs of shoes and \(y\) represents the number of pairs of jeans, what is one combination that is a solution, and what does it mean on the graph?

Write down your answer, and then select the Solution button to compare your work.


Sample answer: \( (2, 5) \)
Buying 2 pairs of shoes and 5 pairs of jeans will cost $180. 

Here is how to find a solution and its meaning: 

Find a point on the graph. The point \( (2, 5) \) is on the graph. Since 2 is the \( x \)-coordinate, it represents 2 pairs of shoes. Since 5 is the \( y \)-coordinate, it represents 5 pairs of jeans. The line represents all combinations that equal $180.
