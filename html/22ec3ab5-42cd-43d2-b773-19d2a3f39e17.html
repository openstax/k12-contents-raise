Writing Equations Using Graphs in Situations

An equation that contains two unknown quantities or two quantities that vary is called an equation in two variables. A solution to such an equation is a pair of numbers that makes the equation true.


Example
Suppose Tyler spends $45 on T-shirts and socks. A T-shirt costs $10, and a pair of socks costs $2.50. If \(t\) represents the number of T-shirts and \(p\) represents the number of pairs of socks that Tyler buys, what is an equation that represents the situation?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(10t + 2.50p = 45\) because the cost is $10 per T-shirt (10t) plus $2.50 per pair of socks \((2.50p)\), which equals 45.



Example

Examine the graph of the equation 10t + 2.50p = 45.



What does the point \((2, 10)\) mean in this graph in relation to the situation?

Compare your answer:

If Tyler bought 2 T-shirts and 10 pairs of socks, it costs $45. This is why the point (\(2, 10)\) is on the line.
