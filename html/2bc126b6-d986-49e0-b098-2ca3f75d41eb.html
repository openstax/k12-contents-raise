A car has a 16-gallon fuel tank. When driven on a highway, it has a gas mileage of 30 miles per gallon. The gas mileage (also called fuel efficiency) tells us the number of miles the car can travel for a particular amount of fuel (one gallon of gasoline, in this case). After filling the gas tank, the driver got on a highway and drove for a while.

 1. How many miles has the car traveled if it has the following amounts of gas left in the tank? Enter only integers.

a. 15 gallons

Answer: 30

b. 10 gallons
Answer: 180

c. 2.5 gallons

Answer: 405


2. Choose the equation that represents the relationship between the distance the car has traveled in miles,\(d\), and the amount of gas left in the tank in gallons, \(x\).

  1. \(d=16(30-x)\)
  2. \(x=\frac{d-480}{-30}\)
  3. \(d=30(16-x)\)
  4. \(x=\frac{30}{d-480}\)
Answer:  \(d=30(16-x)\)

4.Choose an equation that makes it easier to find the amount of gas left in the tank, \(x\), if we know the car has traveled \(d\) miles.

  1. \(d=16(30-x)\)
  2. \(x=\frac{d-480}{-30}\)
  3. \(d=30(16-x)\)
  4. \(x=\frac{30}{d-480}\)

5. Noah is helping collect the entry fees at his school’s sports game. Student entry costs $2.75 each, and adult entry costs $5.25 each. At the end of the game, Noah collected $281.25.

Select all equations that could represent the relationship between the number of students, \( s \), the number of adults, \( a \), and the dollar amount received at the game.

  • A: \( 281.25 - 5.25a=2.75s \)
  • B: \( a=53.57- \frac{2.75}{5.25}s \) (Note: 53.57 is rounded; it’s 281.25/5.25.)
  • C:\( 281.25-5.25s=a \)
  • D:\( 281.25+2.75a=s \)
  • E:\( 281.25+5.25s=a \)

Answer:\( 281.25 - 5.25a=2.75s \), ( a=53.57- \frac{2.75}{5.25}s \)


6. Choose the equation that solves the formula \( 6x + 5y = 13 \) for \( y \).

  1. \(y=13+6x/5\)
  2. \(y=\frac{13-5}{6x}\)
  3. \(y=5(13-6x)\)
  4. \(y=\frac{3-6x}5\)
