Warm Up (5 minutes)

Throughout this lesson, students will use a context that involves two variables—the number of games and the number of rides at an amusement park—and a budgetary constraint. This warm-up prompts students to interpret and make sense of some equations in context, familiarizing them with the quantities and relationships. Later in the lesson, students will dig deeper into what the parameters and graphs of the equations reveal.

Student Activity

Jada has $20 to spend on games and rides at a carnival

  • Games cost $1 each.
  • Rides are $2 each.

Screen 1 - If Jada only plays games, how many games can she play?

Answer: If Jada only spends her money to play games, then she can play 20 games.

Screen 2 - If Jada only goes on rides, how many can she ride?

Answer: If Jada only spends her money to go on rides, then she can ride 10 times.

Screen 3 - Create a table of values that represents the number of games and rides in which Jada can participate for $20.

Answer:

No. of games \((x)\) No. of rides \((y)\)
20 0
18 1
16 2
14 3
12 4
10 5
8 6
6 7
4 8
2 9
0 10

Screen 4 - Write a sentence that describes the relationship between the amount of money that Jada spends at the carnival and the number of games and the number of rides in which she participates.

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample: Twenty dollars equals the sum of the number of games times $1 and the number of games times $2.

Screen 5 - Which equation represents the relationship between the number of games, \(x\), and the number of rides, \(y\), that Jada could do if she spends all $20?

  • \(x + y = 20\)
  • \(2x + y = 20\)
  • \(x + 2y = 20\)

Answer: \(x + 2y = 20\)

Screen 6 - Let \(x\) represent the number of games she plays and \(y\) represent the number of rides that she goes on. Describe what the equation \(x + y = 20 could\) mean in this situation.

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here are some samples:

  • Jada spends $20 on games and rides that cost $1 each.
  • The combined number of games and rides that Jada enjoys is 20.

Screen 7 - Let \(x\) represent the number of games she plays and y represent the number of rides that she goes on. Describe what the equation \(2x + y = 20\) could mean in this situation.

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample: Jada spends $20 on games and rides, where games cost $2 each and rides are $1 each.
