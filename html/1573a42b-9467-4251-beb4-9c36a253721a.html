In this lesson, you will determine what expressions would produce graphs with particular vertices. We will look more closely at how changing the \(h\) and the \(k\) in a quadratic expression of the form \(a(x−h)^2+k\) translates the graph, and recall that the coefficient \(a\) affects the direction of the opening of a parabola.

When you finish this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe how changing a number in the vertex form of a quadratic function affects its graph.
