Lesson Narrative

In earlier lessons, students factored the standard form of a quadratic function to find its solutions, or zeros. Students will now be provided the zeros of a quadratic function and convert them into the factored form and standard form of that function.

Students will also distinguish between finding a quadratic function that has certain zeros and finding the specific quadratic function that has certain zeros and passes through a particular point. They will learn to differentiate between these related quadratic functions.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Convert fluently between a quadratic function's zeros, its factored form, and its standard form.
  • Identify the zeros on the graph of a quadratic function and use them to write the standard form of that function.
  • Use the intercept form of a quadratic function, \(f(x) = a(x − p)(x − q)\), to define a specific function given its zeros and a point.

Learning Targets (Student Facing) 

  • Find the standard form of a quadratic function given its zeros.
  • Find the standard form of a quadratic function given its graph.
  • Use the intercept form of a quadratic function to define a specific function given its zeros and a point.

Required Materials

  • Paper and pencil
  • Graphing technology

Required Preparation

  • Acquire devices that can run Desmos (recommended) or other graphing technology. It is ideal if each student has their own device.

Lesson Vocabulary

During this lesson, it is important to:

  • Familiarize students with the vocabulary words they will see throughout the lesson.
  • Encourage students to look for these words and notice their use and meanings.
  • Encourage students to use key vocabulary words in “math talk” and their written and oral explanations.
  • Utilize a word wall. Sample cards are located here: Mathematics Vocabulary Word Wall Cards.
