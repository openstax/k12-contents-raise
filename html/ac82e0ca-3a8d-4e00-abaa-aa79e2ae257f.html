Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1–5, solve each quadratic equation by rewriting it in factored form and using the zero product property.

  1. \(x^2 + 11x + 35 = 7\)
  • \(x = -4\) and \(x = -7\)
  • \(x = -6\) and \(x = -7\)
  • \(x = 14\) and \(x = -3\)
  • \(x = -5\) and \(x = -7\)

Answer: \(x = -4\) and \(x = -7\)

  1. \(x^2− 14x + 20 = -25\)
  • \(x = 3\) and \(x = 15\)
  • \(x = 10\) and \(x = 2\)
  • \(x = -10\) and \(x = -4\)
  • \(x = 9\) and \(x = 5\)

Answer: \(x = 9\) and \(x = 5\)

  1. \(x^2− 6x − 52 = -12\)
  • \(x = -16\) and \(x = 10\)
  • \(x = 8\) and \(x = -8\)
  • \(x = 10\) and \(x = -4\)
  • \(x = -8\) and \(x = 5\)

Answer: \(x = 10\) and \(x = -4\)

  1. \(x^2 − x − 64 = -8\)
  • \(x = 8\) and \(x = -8\)
  • \(x = -7\) and \(x = 8\)
  • \(x = 28\) and \(x = -2\)
  • \(x = 4\) and \(x = -14\)

Answer: \(x = -7\) and \(x = 8\)

  1. \(x^2 + 9x + 13 = -5\)
  • \(x = -3\) and \(x = -6\)
  • \(x = -9\) and \(x = -2\)
  • \(x = 8\) and \(x = 1\)
  • \(x = 3\) and \(x = 6\)

Answer: \(x = -3\) and \(x = -6\)

  1. Without graphing, identify the function whose graph has only one zero.
  • \(f(x) = x^2− 9\)
  • \(f(x) = x^2− 13x + 42\)
  • \(f(x) = x^2 + 6x + 5\)
  • \(f(x) = x^2 − 8x + 16\)

Answer: \(f(x) = x^2 − 8x + 16\)

  1. Without graphing, identify the function whose graph has only one zero.
