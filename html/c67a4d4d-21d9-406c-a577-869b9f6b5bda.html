Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

  1. The number of hours worked, \(x\), and the total dollars earned, \(y\), have a strong positive relationship.

Explain what it means to have a strong positive relationship in this situation.

  1. As the number of hours worked increases, the total dollars earned decreases.
  2. As the number of hours worked increases, the total dollars earned increases.
  3. As the number of hours worked decreases, the total dollars earned increases.
  4. As the number of hours worked decreases, the total dollars earned decreases.

ANSWER: As the number of hours worked increases, the total dollars earned increases.

  1. The number of minutes on the phone and the customer satisfaction rating have a weak negative relationship.

Explain what it means to have a weak negative relationship in this context. 

  1. As the number of minutes the customer waits on the phone increases, the customer satisfaction rating decreases.
  2. As the number of minutes the customer waits on the phone increases, the customer satisfaction rating increases.
  3. As the number of minutes the customer waits on the phone decreases, the customer satisfaction rating decreases.
  4. As the number of minutes the customer waits on the phone increases, the customer satisfaction rating increases.

ANSWER: As the number of minutes the customer waits on the phone increases, the customer satisfaction rating decreases.
