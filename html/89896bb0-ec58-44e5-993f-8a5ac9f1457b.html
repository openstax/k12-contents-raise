The focus of the lesson is methods for writing equations of a line. The method you use is based on the information you are given.

The table can be used as a reference to write the equation of a line, based on the information given.

To Write an Equation of a Line
If give \(n\): Use: Form: Steps
Slope and \( y \)-intercept slope-intercept \( y = mx + b \) Substitute the slope for \(m\) and the \(y\)-intercept for \( b \) into the slope-intercept form.
Slope and a point point-slope \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \) Substitute the slope for \(m\), the \(x\)-value of the point for \( x_1 \), and the \(y\)-value of the point for \( y_1 \).
Two points point-slope \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \) Use the slope formula \( m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \) to find the slope of the line. Then, substitute the slope for \(m\), the \( x \)-value of one of the points for \( x_1 \), and the \(y\)-value of one of the point for \( y_1 \).

Encourage students to check their equations by using a graphing calculator or Desmos. Tell students to look at the graph to make sure it goes through the given points.
