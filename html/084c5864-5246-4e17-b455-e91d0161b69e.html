Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

A rectangular yard is enclosed by 100 meters of fencing. The table shows some possible values for the length and width of the yard.

Length (meters) Width (meters) Area (square meters)
10 40 400
20 30  
25 25 625
35 15 525
40    

  1. Complete the table with the missing values.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer:

Length (meters) Width (meters) Area (square meters)
10 40 400
20 30 600
25 25 625
35 15 525
40 10 400

The missing value in the second row is 600. The missing values in the last row are 10 and 400.

  1. If the values for length and area are plotted, what would the graph look like?

    Answer: The points would form a stretched out upside-down U shape, or an arch.
  1. How is the relationship between the length and the area of the rectangle different from other kinds of relationships we’ve seen before?

    Answer: As one quantity increases, the other quantity first increases and then decreases, instead of always increasing or always decreasing.

Response to Student Thinking

More Chances

Students will have more opportunities to understand the mathematical ideas in this cool down, so there is no need to slow down or add additional work to the next lessons. Instead, use the results of this cool down to provide guidance for what to look for and emphasize over the next several lessons to support students in advancing their current understanding.
