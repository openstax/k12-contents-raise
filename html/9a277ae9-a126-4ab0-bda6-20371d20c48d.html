In this lesson, you will find and interpret the correlation coefficient and use it to understand the strength of a linear relationship. 

When you finish this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe the “goodness of fit” of a linear model using the correlation coefficient.
  • Match the correlation coefficient with a scatter plot and linear model.

Here are the activities that will help you reach those goals:

  • 3.4.1: Comparing Scatter Plots with Linear and Nonlinear Trends
  • 3.4.2: Recognizing Differences in Scatter Plots
  • 3.4.2: Self Check
  • 3.4.3: Matching Correlation Coefficients
  • 3.4.3: Self Check
  • 3.4.4: Using Correlation Coefficient to Determine Best Fit
