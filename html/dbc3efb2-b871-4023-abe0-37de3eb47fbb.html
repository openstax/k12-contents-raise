Establishing a community of learners is an important part of providing students with a safe place to learn and getting the most out of this curricular resource. In practice, this means:

  • setting the tone that each and every student brings something valuable to the community; 
  • honoring the diverse experiences students bring to the classroom; 
  • communicating, both explicitly and implicitly, that students’ ideas are important enough to be heard and, when appropriate, to drive instruction; 
  • supporting student engagement with a variety of strategies; and 
  • approaching each and every student in a way that shows their thinking can grow over time.

In short, it’s crucial to demonstrate a true belief that each and every student can and will be successful.

Many of the activities in this program utilize small groups of 2-4. When students are discussing mathematics, encouraging them to use sentence frames promotes the idea that each and every student can make positive contributions. Here are some examples:

  • “I agree, because…” 
  • “I respectfully disagree, because…” 
  • “I have a question: Why is…?” 
  • “How did you know you needed to…?”

Supporting small groups helps students get along, encourages equitable participation, and provides opportunities for students to rehearse their ideas in preparation for sharing with the whole class.
