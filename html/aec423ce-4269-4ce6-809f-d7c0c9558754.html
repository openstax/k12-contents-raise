Remember that a recursive formula defines terms using one or more of the previous terms. If you need to calculate the \(100^{th}\) term or the \(500^{th}\) term in a sequence, the recursive formula becomes difficult to use if you do not know the values of the terms near \(n = 100\) or \(n = 500\).

Different definitions can often create the same sequence but are more generalizable. These are called general rules or explicit rules. These formulas can be used to find any term in the sequence and may also be referred to as the rule to find the nth term.


Geometric Sequence Formulas

Recursive Formula

\(a_n = a_{n-1} \cdot r\),
Where \(a_1\) is the first term, \(n\) is the term you want, and \(r\) is the common ratio.


Explicit General Formula

\(a_n = a_1 (r)^{n-1} \)
Where \(a_1\) is the first term, \(n\) is the term you want, and \(r\) is the common ratio.
