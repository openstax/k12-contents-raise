Some key takeaways from this lesson are that equations can be written in different forms. Often, one form is better than another based on the information given.

  • Ask students to create their own problems and write the equation three different ways.
  • Show that an equation in all three forms is equivalent by substituting a point into all three equations.
  • Continue to have students use formal mathematical vocabulary around slope and the different forms.

In the next lesson, students will see how slope as rate of change occurs in real-world situations.
