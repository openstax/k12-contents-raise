Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

1. Andre bought a new bag of cat food. The next day, he opened it to feed his cat. The graph shows how many ounces were left in the bag on the days after it was bought.


1. How many ounces of food were in the bag 12 days after Andre bought it?  Enter only integers.

ANSWER: 36

2.  How many days did it take for the bag to contain 16 ounces of food? Enter only integers.

ANSWER: 24

3. How much did the bag weigh before it was opened? Enter only integers.

ANSWER: 56

4. Approximately many days did it take for the bag to be empty? Enter only integers.

ANSWER: 33

For #5-7, use the problem and graph below:

A group of hikers is progressing steadily along an uphill trail. The graph shows their elevation (or height above sea level), in feet, at each distance from the start of the trail, in miles.


5. What is the slope of the graph? Enter only integers.

ANSWER: 250


6.What does the slope tell us about this situation?

  • The change in elevation as the hikers hike more miles.
  • The change in miles as the elevation increases.
ANSWER:  The change in elevation as the hikers hike more miles

7. Choose the equation that represents the relationship between the hikers' distance from the start of the trail, \(x\), and their elevation, \(y\).

  1. \(y=500x+250\)
  2. \(y=250x-500\)
  3. \(y=250x+500\)
  4. \(y=500-250x\)
ANSWER:  \(y=250x+500\)

8. A kindergarten teacher bought $21 worth of stickers and cardstock for his class. The stickers cost $1.50 a sheet, and the cardstock cost $3.50 per pack. The equation \( 1.5s+3.5c=21 \) represents the relationship between sheets of stickers, \(s\), packs of cardstock, \(c\), and the dollar amount the kindergarten teacher spent on these supplies. 


What does the vertical intercept, (0,6), mean in this situation?

  • 0 sheets of stickers and 6 packs of cardstock were bought.
  • 6 sheets of stickers and 0 packs of cardstock were bought.

ANSWER: 0 sheets of stickers and 6 packs of cardstock were bought.
