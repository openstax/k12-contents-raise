Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student-Facing Statement

An art museum opens at 9 a.m. and closes at 5 p.m. The function \(V\) gives the number of visitors in the museum \(h\) hours after it opens.

  1. Explain what this statement tells us about the situation: \(V(1.25) = 28\).
  2. Use function notation to represent each statement:
    1. At 1 p.m., there were 257 visitors in the museum.
    2. At the time of closing, there were no visitors in the museum.
  3. Use the previous statements about the visitors in the museum to sketch a graph that could represent the function.

Student Response

  1. An hour and 15 minutes after the museum opened (at 10:15 a.m.), there were 28 visitors in the building.
  2.  
    1. \(V(4)=257\)
    2. \(V(8)=0\)
  3. A graph showing points at \((1.25,28)\) and \((4,257)\), with or without lines connecting these points. See sample graph.

Response to Student Thinking

More Chances

Students will have more opportunities to understand the mathematical ideas in this cool-down, so there is no need to slow down or add additional work to the next lessons. Instead, use the results of this cool-down to provide guidance for what to look for and emphasize over the next several lessons to support students in advancing their current understanding.
