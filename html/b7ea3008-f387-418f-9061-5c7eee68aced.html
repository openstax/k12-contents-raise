Warm Up (5 minutes)

The purpose of this warm up is to elicit the idea that the values in the table have a predictable pattern, which will be useful when students consider the context of a falling object in a later activity. While students may notice and wonder many things about this table, the patterns are the important discussion points, rather than an attempt to find a rule for the function. Because the rule is not easy to uncover, studying the numbers ahead of time should prove helpful as students analyze the function later.

This prompt gives students opportunities to see and make use of structure. The specific structure they might notice is all the \(y\)-values are multiples of 16 and perfect squares. Some may notice the pattern is not linear and wonder whether it is quadratic.

Launch

Have students write down their thoughts about what they notice and wonder in the table. Then, have them turn and talk with a partner. Ask pairs to share their thoughts with the class and add to a growing list. If some of the sample answers are not present, then ask questions to facilitate their addition to the class list.

Student Activity

Study the table. What do you notice? What do you wonder?

\(x\) 0 1 2 3 4 5
\(y\) 0 16 64 144 256

400


Answer

Things you may notice:

  • The \(x\)-values increment by 1 as you move across the table.
  • The \(y\)-values increase.
  • All \(y\)-values are even and divisible by 4, 8, and 16.
  • The \(y\)-values are all perfect squares.
  • The successive differences between the \(y\)-values are growing.

Things you may wonder:

  • Is there a rule for these numbers?
  • Do the numbers represent the growth of a geometric pattern?
  • Do the numbers represent a quadratic relationship?
  • What is the next number in the table?

Activity Synthesis

Invite students to share their observations and questions. Record and display them for all to see.

After all responses are recorded, tell students that they will investigate these values more closely in upcoming activities.
