Cool Down (5 minutes)

Graphing technology should not be used in this cool-down.

Student-Facing Statement

Here is a system of linear equations: \( \begin{cases} \begin {align} 2x + \frac12y &=7\\6x - \frac12y&=5 \end{align} \end{cases} \)

  1. Which would be a more helpful method for solving the system: adding the two equations or subtracting one from the other? Explain your reasoning.
  2. Solve the system without graphing. Explain your reasoning.

Video: Determining the Best Method for Solving a System of Equations

Watch the following video to learn more about finding the best method to solve a system of equations.

  

Student Response

  1. Adding the equations. Sample explanation: \( \frac12y + (-\frac12y) =0 \), so adding the equations would eliminate the \( y \)-variable and enable solving for \( x \).
  2. \( x = \frac32 \) (or equivalent) and \( y = 8 \). Sample reasoning:

    \( \begin {align}2x + \frac12y &= 7\\ 6x - \frac12y &= 5 \quad+\\ \overline {8x +\hspace{2.5mm}0\hspace{1mm}} &\overline{ \hspace{1mm}= 12 \qquad}\\8x &= 12\\x &=\frac32 \end{align} \)

    \( \begin {align}2x +\frac12 y&= 7\\ 2(\frac32) +\frac12y &= 7\\ 3+\frac12y&= 7\\ \frac12y &= 4\\ y&=8 \end{align} \)

  3. Students will be able to view the following video of this process worked out in Activity 2.3.4: What to Do with This System? – Worked Solution. 

Response to Student Thinking


At the start of the next lesson, consider reviewing the strategy of rewriting equations with subtraction as equivalent equations with addition (and similarly teaching the strategy “always add” and “multiply by a negative”) for students who struggle with subtracting entire equations.
