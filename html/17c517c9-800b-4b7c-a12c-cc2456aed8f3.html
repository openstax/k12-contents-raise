Activity (10 minutes)

In this activity, students encounter equations that are challenging to solve using the methods they have learned, motivating students to seek a more efficient method.

Launch

Arrange students in groups of two. Ask partners to choose the same equation. Give students quiet time to solve the equation and then time to discuss their solutions and strategy. If they finish solving their chosen equation, ask them to choose another one to solve. Leave a few minutes for a whole-class discussion.

Provide access to calculators for numerical computations.

Student Activity

Write down your answer for how to solve each equation. Find the two solutions by rewriting it in factored form or by completing the square. Be prepared to explain your choice of method.

Answer: Completing the square method:

\(\begin{array}{rcl} x^{2}-2 x-1.25 &=&0 \\ x^{2}-2 x &=&1.25 \\ x^{2}-2 x+\left(\frac{-2}{2}\right)^{2} &=&1.25+\left(\frac{-2}{2}\right)^{2} \\ x^{2}-2 x+1 &=&1.25+1 \\ x^{2}-2 x+1 &=&2.25 \\ (x-1)^{2} &=&2.25 \\ \sqrt{(x-1)^{2}} &=&\sqrt{2.25} \\ x-1 &=&\pm \sqrt{2.25} \\ x &=&1 \pm \sqrt{2.25} \\ x &=&1 \pm 1.5 \\ x &=&1+1.5 \\ x &=&1-1.5 \\ x &=&2.5 \\ x &=&-0.5 \\ x&=&2.5 \text { and } x=-0.5 \end{array}\)

Activity Synthesis

Consider arranging students who solved the same equation in groups of two to three to discuss their strategies and then displaying the correct solutions for all to see.

Invite students to share their reflections on the solving process. Discuss questions such as:

  • "What method did you choose and why?"
  • "Did you find yourself choosing one method and then switching to another? If so, what prompted you to do that?"
  • "Did you run into any challenges when rewriting the equation (or completing the square)? What were some of the challenges?"

Acknowledge that all of these equations are cumbersome to solve by either rewriting in factored form or completing the square. The last equation cannot be written in factored form (with rational coefficients), so completing the square is the only way to go. Tell students they are about to learn a formula that gives the solutions to any quadratic equation.

9.6.2: Additional Resources

The following content is available to students who would like more support based on their experience with the self check. Students will not automatically have access to this content, so you may wish to share it with those who could benefit from it. 

Solve by Using Perfect Squares and Completing the Square 

Solve \(2x^2+5x+2=0\) by completing the square.

Step 1
Divide all terms by the leading coefficient.
\(\frac{2x^2+5x+2}{2}=\frac{0}{2}\)
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+1=0\)

Step 2
Move the constant to the right side of the equation.
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x=-1\)

Step 3
Complete the square and add to each side: \((\frac{5}{2} \times \frac{1}{2})^2=\frac{25}{16}\).
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+\frac{25}{16}=-1+\frac{25}{16}\)

Step 4
Simplify.  
(Hint: create a common denominator before adding.)
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+\frac{25}{16}=-\frac{16}{16}+\frac{25}{16}\)
\((x+\frac{5}{4})^2=\frac{9}{16}\)

Step 5
Take the square root of each side.
\(\sqrt{(x+\frac{5}{4})^2}= \sqrt{\frac{9}{16}}\)
\(x+\frac{5}{4}= \pm \frac{3}{4}\)

Step 6
Write two equations and solve.
\(x+\frac{5}{4}=\frac{3}{4}\)          \(x+\frac{5}{4}=-\frac{3}{4}\)
\(x=-\frac{2}{4}=-\frac{1}{2}\)   \(x=-\frac{8}{4}=-2\)

Try It: Solve by Using Perfect Squares and Completing the Square

Solve \(4x^2+36x+37=0\).

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Solution

Here is how to solve using completing the square:

Step 1
Divide all terms by the leading coefficient.
\(\frac{4x^2+36x+37}{4}=\frac{0}{4}\)
\(x^2+9x+\frac{37}{4}=0\)

Step 2
Move the constant to the right side of the equation.
\(x^2+9x=-\frac{37}{4}\)

Step 3
Complete the square and add to each side: \((9 \times \frac{1}{2})^2=\frac{81}{4}\).
\(x^2+9x+\frac{81}{4}=-\frac{37}{4}+\frac{81}{4}\)

Step 4
Simplify.  
\((x+\frac{9}{2})^2=11\)

Step 5
Take the square root of each side.
\(\sqrt{(x+\frac{9}{2})^2}=\sqrt{11}\)
\((x+\frac{9}{2})= \pm \sqrt{11}\)

Step 6
Write two equations and solve.
\(x+\frac{9}{2}=\sqrt{11}\)       \(x+\frac{9}{2}=-\sqrt{11}\)
\(x=-\frac{9}{2}+\sqrt{11}\)       \(x=-\frac{9}{2}-\sqrt{11}\)
