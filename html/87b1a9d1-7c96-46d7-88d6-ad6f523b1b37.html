Warm Up Activity 

In an earlier lesson, you saw the equation \( V + F - 2 = E \), which relates the number of vertices, faces, and edges in a Platonic solid.

1. Write an equation that makes it easier to find the number of vertices in each of the Platonic solids described:


An octahedron (shown here), which has 8 faces
Enter your answer here:

\(V=E−6\) (or equivalent)


2. An icosahedron, which has 30 edges
Enter your answer here:

\( V=32−F \) (or equivalent)


3. A Buckminsterfullerene (also called a “Buckyball”) is a polyhedron with 60 vertices. It is not a Platonic solid, but the numbers of faces, edges, and vertices are related the same way as those in a Platonic solid.

Write an equation that makes it easier to find the number of faces a Buckyball has if we know how many edges it has.

Enter your answer:

\( F=E−58 \) (or equivalent)
