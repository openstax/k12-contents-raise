Cool Down Activity

When studying requires that you think things through yourself instead of waiting to be told the right answer, that effort you put in helps that information stick better.

A movie theater models the revenue from ticket sales in one day as a function of the ticket price, \(p\). Here are two expressions defining the same revenue function.

\(p(120 − 4p)\)

\(120p − 4p^2\)


  1. According to this model, how high would the ticket price have to be for the theater to make $0 in revenue? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Enter the ticket price and your reasoning.

Compare your answer:

$30. If \(p(120 − 4p) = 0\), then either \(p = 0\) or \(120 − 4p = 0\). If the latter is a true equation, \(p\) must be 30.


  1. What equation can you write to find out what ticket price(s) would allow the theater to make $600 in revenue?

Enter an equation.

Compare your answer:

\(p(120−4p) = 600\) or \(120p − 4p^2 = 600\)
