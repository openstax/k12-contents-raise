You are buying chicken and steak for a barbecue. Chicken costs $1.29/lb and steak costs $3.49/lb.

a. Write an equation to show the total cost, T, of the meat.

\( T\;=\;1.29\;c\;+\;3.49s \)

b. You have $100 to spend on the barbecue. Write an equation to find the number of pounds of chicken, c, that you can buy.

\( c= \frac{100-3.49s}{1.29} \)

c. With $100 to spend on the barbecue, write an equation to find the number of pounds of steak, s, that you can buy.

\( s= \frac{100-1.29c}{3.49} \)

Try It: Write Constraint Equations

On a canoeing trip, you walk 4 mph then canoe at 7 mph and cover 30 miles. Write an equation to find how many miles you spend canoeing.

Write down your answer, then select the Solution button to compare your work.


Here is how to write this equation given the constraints:


Identify known variables.

c: miles canoeing

w: miles walked

Write an equation to show the distance covered.

\( 4w + 7c =30 \)

Write an equation that solves for \( c \), the number of miles spent canoeing.

  \( c= \frac{30-4w}{7} \)
