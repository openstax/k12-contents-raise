Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

A school is holding a carnival and hopes to raise $500. Child tickets cost $3, and adult tickets cost $5. If the school sells \( x \) child tickets and \( y \) adult tickets, then the equation \( 3x + 5y = 500 \) expresses the fact that the school raised exactly $500 from ticket sales.

  1. Solve the equation for \( x \).

Answer: \( x=\frac{500-5y}3 \)

  1. Explain when it might be helpful to rewrite the equation this way.

Answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample. Solving for \( x \) helps us find out the number of child tickets to sell if we know the number of adults attending the carnival and we want to make exactly $500 from ticket sales.

Response to Student Thinking

More Chances
