Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1–4, find the factored form of the quadratic expression.

  1. \(3x^2+ 16x + 5\)
  • \((3x − 5)(x − 1)\)
  • \((3x + 1)(x - 5)\)
  • \((3x − 1)(x − 5)\)
  • \((3x + 1)(x + 5)\)

Answer: \((3x + 1)(x + 5)\)

  1. \(2x^2+ 11x − 6\)
  • \((2x + 6)(x − 1)\)
  • \((2x − 6)(x + 1)\)
  • \((2x − 1)(x + 6)\)
  • \((2x + 1)(x − 6)\)

Answer: \((2x − 1)(x + 6)\)

  1. \(4x^2− 13x + 3\)
  • \((4x − 1)(x − 3)\)
  • \((4x + 3)(x + 1)\)
  • \((4x − 3)(x − 1)\)
  • \((4x + 1)(x + 3)\)

Answer: \((4x − 1)(x − 3)\)

  1. \(2x^2−3x −2\)
  • \((2x + 1)(x − 2)\)
  • \((2x + 2)(x − 1)\)
  • \((2x − 2)(x + 1)\)
  • \((2x − 1)(x + 2)\)

Answer: \((2x + 1)(x − 2)\)

  1. The height, in meters, of a baseball being hit is modeled by the function \(h(x) = -2x^2+ 12x + 3\) where \(x\) is the number of seconds since being hit.

  • 4.38 seconds
  • 6.24 seconds

  • 5.221 seconds
