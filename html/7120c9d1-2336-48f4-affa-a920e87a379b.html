Activity

If \(\sqrt[n]{a^m}\) is a real number and \(n\;\geq\;2\), then \(\sqrt[n]{a^m}\;=\;a^\frac mn\).

So radicals can be rewritten as rational exponents.

  1. With a partner, write each radical as a term with rational exponents on your own paper. Then, turn and talk to your partner.

    1. \(\sqrt[8]x\)

Write down your answer, then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(x^\frac18\)

    1. \(\sqrt[3]{x^2}\)

Write down your answer, then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(x^\frac23\)

    1. \(\sqrt[11]{x^5}\)

Write down your answer, then select the Solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

\(x^\frac5{11}\)
