Here is how to solve by substitution:

Step 1: Solve the first equation for \( x \) to isolate a variable.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}\;\;x\;–\;4y\;&=&\;–4\\x\;–\;4y\;{\style{color:red}+}\;{\style{color:red}4}{\style{color:red}y}\;&=&\;–4\;{\style{color:red}+}\;{\style{color:red}4}{\style{color:red}y}\\x\;&=&\;–4\;+\;4y\end{array}\)

Step 2: Replace the \(x\) in the second equation with the expression \( -4 + 4y \).

Step 3: Solve the equation with just one variable.

Step 4: Use the first equation and replace \(y\) with 32.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}x\;–\;4y\;&=&\;–4\\x\;–\;4(\frac32)\;&=&\;–4\\x\;–\;6\;&=&\;–4\\x\;&=&\;2\end{array}\)
