Cool Down (5 minutes)

Launch

Ask students to put away devices for this cool down.

Student Activity

  1. Consider the quadratic equation \(y=x^2-4x\). Consider the graph of the equation when answering questions a and b.
    1. Where are the \(x\)-intercepts located?

    Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

    Answer: The \(x\)-intercepts are at \(x=0\) and \(x=4\).

    1. What is the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex?

    Answer: The correct answer is 2. The vertex is halfway between the intercepts at \(x=2\).

  1. Here is a graph of \(y=x^2\).

Sketch this graph, then sketch a graph of \(y=x^2+5x\) on the same graph. Briefly explain how you know where to sketch the graph.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer: \(x^2+5x\) is equivalent to \(x(x+5)\), so the \(x\)-intercepts are at \(x=0\) and \(x=-5\). Calculate the vertex by finding the \(x\)-coordinate in between \(x = -5\) and \(x = 0\) which is \(x = -2.5\). Substitute \(x=-2.5\) into the equation to find the \(y\)-coordinate: \(y=(-2.5)2+5(-2.5)=-6.188\). The vertex is at \((-2.5, -6.188)\). The squared term has a positive coefficient so the graph opens upward. Adding a positive linear term to \(x^2\) shifts the graph down and to the left.
