Solving a System of Equations by Elimination Using Multiplication

Let’s look at an example where we need to multiply both equations by constants in order to make the coefficients of one variable opposites.

Solve the system by elimination: \(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\;4x-3y=9\\7x+2y=-6\end{array}\right.\)

Solution:

In this example, we cannot multiply just one equation by any constant to get opposite coefficients. So we will strategically multiply both equations by different constants to get the opposites.

Step 1 - Check to see if each equation is in standard form \(Ax+By=C\).

Both equations are in standard form.
\(4x-3y=9\)
\(7x+2y=-6\)

Step 2 - Get the opposite coefficients of \(y\). Simplify the equations.

Multiply the first equation by 2 and the second equation by 3.
\(2(4x-3y=9)= 8x-6y=18\)
\(3(7x+2y=-6) = 21x+6y=-18\)

Step 3 - Add the two equations to eliminate \(y\).

\(\begin{array}{r}8x-6y=18\\21x+6y=-18\\ \hline 29x=0\\x=0\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Solve for \(y\) by substituting \(x\) back into one of the equations.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}7x+2y&=&-6 \\ 7(0)+2y&=&-6 \\2y&=&-6 \\y&=&-3 \\ \end{array}\)

Step 5 - Write the solution as an ordered pair.

The ordered pair is \((0,-3)\).

Step 6 - Check that the ordered pair is a solution to both original equations.

\(\begin{array}{rr} 4x-3y=9 & 7x+2y=-6\\ 4(0)-3(-3) \overset?=9 & 7(0)+2(-3)\overset?=-6 \\ 9=9\checkmark & -6=-6\checkmark \end{array}\)

The solution is \((0,-3)\).

Try It: Solving a System of Equations by Elimination Using Multiplication

Solve the following system of equations using elimination:

\[\left\{\begin{array}{l}7x+8y=4\\3x-5y=27\end{array}\right.\]

Write down your answer. Then select the solution button to compare your work.

Step 1 - Write both equations in standard form. If any coefficients are fractions, clear them.

\(7x+8y=4\)
\(3x–5y=27\)

Step 2 - Check to see if the coefficients of one variable are opposites or equivalent. To get opposite coefficients of \(y\), we will need to multiply the first equation by 5 and the second equation by 8.

\(5(7x+8y)=5(4)\) \(8(3x–5y)=8(27)\)

Step 3 - Simplify.

\(35x+40y=20\)
\(24x–40y=216\)

Step 4 - Add the equations to eliminate one variable.

\(\begin{array}{r}35x+40y=20\\24x–40y=216\\ \hline 59x=236\\\end{array}\)

Step 5 - Solve for the remaining variable.

\(59x=236\)
\(x=4\)

Step 7 - Substitute the solution into one of the original equations. Then solve for the other variable.

\(59x=236\)
\(x=4\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}7x+8y&=&4 \\ 7(4)+8y&=&4 \\28+8y&=&4 \\8y&=&-24 \\y&=&-3 \\ \end{array}\)

Step 8 - Check that the ordered pair is a solution to both original equations.

\(\begin{array}{rr} 7x+8y=4 & 3x–5y=277\\ 7(4)+8(−3) \overset?=4 & 3(4)–5(−3)\overset?=277 \\ 20−24 \overset?=4 & 3(4)–5(−3)\overset?=277 \\ 4=4\checkmark & 27=27\checkmark \end{array}\)

The solution is \((0,-3)\).
