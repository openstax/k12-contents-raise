Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For 1-2, use this situation:

Jada has p pennies and n nickels that add up to more than 40 cents. She has fewer than 20 coins altogether.

Which two inequalities could make up a system of inequalities that represents how many pennies and nickels that Jada could have? Select all that apply.

  • p+n<20

  • p+n>20

  •  0.01p + 0.05n <0.40

  •  0.01p + 0.05n >0.40

ANSWER: p+n<20, 0.01p + 0.05n >0.40

2.  Is it possible that Jada has each of the following combinations of coins? If so, explain or show how you know. If not, state which constraint—the amount of money or the number of coins—it does not meet.

  • 15 pennies and 5 nickels

ANSWER:  No. She has fewer than 20 coins.15+5=20, it is not less than 20

  • 16 pennies and 2 nickels

ANSWER:  No. She has more than 40 cents.16+2(5)=26, it is not more than 40 cents.

  • 10 pennies and 8 nickels

ANSWER:   Yes. She has less than 20 coins.  10+8=18. That is less than 20 coins. She has more than 40 cents.  10+8(5)=50, which is  more than 40 cents.

3. A triathlon athlete swims at an average rate of 2.4 miles per hour and bikes at an average rate of 16.1 miles per hour. At the end of one training session, she has swum and biked more than 20 miles in total.

The inequality \(2.4s+16.1b>20\) and this graph represent the relationship between the hours of swimming, \(s\), the hours of biking, \(b\), and the total distance the athlete could have traveled in miles.

Mai said, "I'm not sure the graph is right. For example, the point (10,3) is in the shaded region, but it's not realistic for an athlete to swim for 10 hours and bike for 3 hours in a training session! I think triathlon athletes generally train for no more than 2 hours a day."

Which  inequality represents Mai's last statement?

  • (s+b\leq2\)
  • \(s+b<2\)
  • \(s+b>2\)
  • \(s+b=2\)

ANSWER:    \(s+b\;\leq\;2\)

Let \(b\) represent the number of bracelets and \(n\) the number of necklaces.

a.     Which inequality represents the number of gift items that Elena needs.
