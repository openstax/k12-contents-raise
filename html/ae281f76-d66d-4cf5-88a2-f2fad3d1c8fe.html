  1. Here is an inequality: \(\frac{7x+6}2\leq3x+2\)
Select two of the values that are a solution to the inequality.
  • \( x = -3 \)
  • \( x = -2 \)
  • \( x = -1 \)
  • \( x = 0 \)
  • \( x = 1 \)
  • \( x = 2 \)
  • \( x = 3 \)
Answer: \(x = -3\), \(x = -2\)

\(\frac{7x+6}{2} \le 3x+2\)
\(7x+6 \le 6x+4\)
\(x+6 \le 4\)
\(x \le -2\)
The solution is any number that is less than or equal to -2.
x=−3x and x=−2 are the two choices that fit the criteria.

For questions 3–4, use the following information:

Scooter rental company A charges $9 per hour plus a $11 initiation fee. Scooter rental company B charges $15 per hour.

  1. A customer writes the following inequality to represent the situation: \(9h + 11 > 15h\).

Which of the following is true about the given inequality?

  • Company A costs more than Company B.
  • Company A costs less than Company B.
  • Company A costs the same as Company B.
  • \( h \) represents the number of scooters rented.

Answer: : Company A costs more than Company B.

Substitute the number 1 in for \(h\) to see how much it would cost to rent a scooter for 1 hour.
\(9(1)+11>15(1)\)
\(20>15\)
To rent a scooter for 1 hour, it costs $20 from Company A and $15 from Company B. Company A costs more than Company B.

  1. For which number of hours does Company B cost less?
    • 1 hour
    • 2 hours
    • 3 hours
    • 4 hours

Answer : 1 hour

Substitute the number 1 in for \(h\) to see how much it would cost to rent a scooter for 1 hour.
Company A: 9(1)+11=$20
Company B: 15(1)=$15
To rent a scooter for 1 hour, it costs $20 from Company A and $15 from Company B.

Substitute the number 2 in for \(h\) to see how much it would cost to rent a scooter for 2 hours.
Company A:
9(2)+11=$29
Company B: 15(2)=$30
To rent a scooter for 2 hours, it costs $29 from Company A and $30 from Company B.

Substitute the number 3 in for \(h\) to see how much it would cost to rent a scooter for 3 hours.
Company A: 9(3)+11=$38
Company B: 15(3)=$45
To rent a scooter for 3 hours, it costs $38 from Company A and $45 from Company B.

Substitute the number 4 in for \(h\) to see how much it would cost to rent a scooter for 4 hours.
Company A: 9(4)+11=$47
Company B: 15(4)=$60
To rent a scooter for 4 hours, it costs $47 from Company A and $60 from Company B.

Company B costs the least for 1 hour.

For questions 5–7, use the following information:

  1. The equation \( 20x = 5x + 60 \) represents a possible constraint about the situation.
    Solve for \(x\).
    \(x =\)  __?__

Answer: The correct answer is 4.

\(20x=5x+60\)
\(15x=60\)
\(x=4 \)

  1. Which shop offers more membership points if you rent a bike for longer than the constraint you found in the previous question?
    • Cycle Rentals
    • Bikes Unlimited

Answer: Cycle Rentals

The constraint from question 5 is \(x=4\).
Since a number larger than 4 is needed to determine which shop offers more membership points if you rent a bike for longer than the constraint, choose to substitute in the number 5 for \(x\) to calculate how many membership points can be earned in 5 days.
Cycle Rentals: \(20x=20(5)=100\) membership points
Bikes Unlimited: \(5x+60=5(5)+60=25+60=85\) membership points
