Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1–4, solve each equation. Identify every correct answer.

  1. \(x(3x+12)=0\)
  • \(x=0\) and \(x=-12\)
  • \(x=0\) and \(x=-3\)
  • \(x = 4\) and \(x=-4\)
  • \(x=0\) and \(x=-4\)

Answer: \(x = 0\) and \(x=-4\)

  1. \((x−7)(x+9)=0\)
  • \(x=7\) and \(x=-7\)
  • \(x=0\), \(x=7\), and \(x=-9\)
  • \(x=7\) and \(x=-9\)
  • \(x=-7\) and \(x=9\)

Answer: \(x=7\) and \(x=-9\)

  1. \((x−8)(4x+15)=0\)
  • \(x=8\) and \(x=- \frac {15}{4}\)
  • \(x=8\) and \(x=-\frac {4}{15}\)
  • \(x=8\) and \(x=\frac {4}{15}\)
  • \(x=8\) and \(x=\frac {15}{4}\)

Answer: \(x=8\) and \(x=-\frac {15}{4}\)

  1. \(x(x−6)(5x−20)=0\)
  • \(x=0\), \(x=-4\), and \(x=6\)
  • \(x=0\), \(x=4\), and \(x=6\)
  • \(x=4\) and \(x=6\)
  • \(x=0\) and \(x=6\)

Answer: \(x=0\), \(x=4\), and \(x=6\)

For questions 5 and 6, use the following.

The functions below model the height of a projectile in feet, \(t\) seconds after launch.

\(h(t) = -7t^2 + 34t + 48\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;h(t) = (-7t − 8)(t − 6)\)

  1. Without graphing, determine at what time the height of the object is 0 feet.
  • \(\frac {8}{7}\) seconds
  • 6 seconds
  • \(-\frac {8}{7}\) seconds
  • 0 seconds

Answer: 6 seconds

  1. Determine the height of the object before it is launched.
  • 0 feet
  • \(\frac {8}{7}\) feet
  • 6 feet
  • 48 feet

Answer: 48 feet
