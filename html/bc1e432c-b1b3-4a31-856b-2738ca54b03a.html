This year, students in the 9th grade are collecting dimes and quarters for a school fundraiser. They are trying to collect more money than the students who were in the 9th grade last year. The students in 9th grade last year collected $143.88.

  1. Using \(d\) to represent the number of dimes collected and \(q\) to represent the number of quarters, which statement best represents this situation?
  •  \( 0.25d+0.1q≥143.88 \)
  • \( 0.25q+0.1d≥143.88 \)
  • \( 0.25d+0.1q>143.88 \)
  • \( 0.25q+0.1d>143.88 \)

Answer:\( 0.25q+0.1d>143.88 \)

For exercises 3-4, use the passage below to answer the questions:

A farmer is creating a budget  for planting soybeans and wheat. Planting soybeans costs $200 per acre and planting wheat costs $500 per acre. He wants to spend no more than $100,000 planting soybeans and wheat.

  1. Let s represent the number of acres of soybeans and \(w\) represent the number of acres of wheat. Write an inequality to describe the situation.
  • \(500s + 200w < 100,000\)
  • \( 500s + 200w ≤ 100,000\)
  • \( 200s + 500w ≤ 100,000\)
  • \( 200s + 500w > 100,000\)

Answer: \( 200s + 500w ≤ 100,000 \)

  1. Name one solution to the inequality and explain what it represents in that situation.
  • \((200, 130)\)
  • \((250, 100)\)
  • \((170, 140)\)
  • \( (300, 90) \)

Answer: \((250, 100)\)

For exercises 5-7, use the passage below to answer the questions:

Amira is ordering dried chili peppers and corn husks for her cooking class. Chili peppers cost $16.95 per pound and corn husks cost $6.49 per pound.

Amira spends less than $50 on \( d \) pounds of dried chili peppers and \( h \) pounds of corn husks.

Here is a graph that represents this situation.


  1. Write an inequality that represents this situation.
  • \(16.95d + 6.49h ≥ 50\)
  • \(16.95d – 6.49h < 50\)
  • \(16.95d + 6.49h < 50\)
  • \(16.95d + 6.49h > 50 \)

Answer: \( 16.95d + 6.49h < 50 \)

  1. Can Amira purchase 2 pounds of dried chili peppers and 4 pounds of corn husks and spend less than $50? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. She would be spending \( 16.95(2) + 6.49(4) \), or $59.86.

  1. Can Amira purchase 1.5 pounds of dried chili peppers and 3 pounds of corn husks and spend less than $50? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: Yes. She would be spending \( 16.95(1.5) + 6.49(3) \), or $44.90.

For exercises 8-10, use the passage below to answer the questions:

A beach store rents surfboards for $15 per hour and beach umbrellas for $10 per hour. The store wants to make at least $200 per day on renting beach equipment. Let \( s \) be the number of hours that a surfboard is rented and \( u \) be the number of hours an umbrella is rented.

  1. Which inequality represents this situation?
  • \(15s – 10u ≥ 200\)
  • \(15s + 10u ≤ 200\)
  • \( 15s + 10u ≥ 200\)
  • \(15s + 10u < 200\)

Answer: \(15s + 10u ≥ 200\)

  1. Can the beach store rent surfboards for 7 total hours and rent umbrellas for 9 total hours and reach their goal of making $200? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. They would be making \( 15(7) + 10(9) \), or $195.

  1. Can the beach store rent surfboards for 10 total hours and rent umbrellas for 4 total hours and reach their goal of making $200? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. They would be making \( 15(10) + 10(4) \), or $190.
