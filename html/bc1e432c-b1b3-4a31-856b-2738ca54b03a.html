Amira is ordering dried chili peppers and corn husks for her cooking class. Chili peppers cost $16.95 per pound and corn husks cost $6.49 per pound.

Amira spends less than $50 on \( d \) pounds of dried chili peppers and \( h \) pounds of corn husks.

5. Write an inequality that represents this situation.

a.     16.95d + 6.49h ≥ 50
b.     16.95d – 6.49h < 50
c.     16.95d + 6.49h < 50
d.     16.95d + 6.49h > 50

Answer: \( 16.95d + 6.49h < 50 \)

6. Can Amira purchase 2 pounds of dried chili peppers and 4 pounds of corn husks and spend less than $50? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. She would be spending \( 16.95(2) + 6.49(4) \), or $59.86.

7. Can Amira purchase 1.5 pounds of dried chili peppers and 3 pounds of corn husks and spend less than $50? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: Yes. She would be spending \( 16.95(1.5) + 6.49(3) \), or $44.90.

For exercises 8-10, use the passage below to answer the questions:

A beach store rents surfboards for $15 per hour and beach umbrellas for $10 per hour. The store wants to make at least $200 per day on renting beach equipment. Let \( s \) be the number of hours that a surfboard is rented and \( u \) be the number of hours an umbrella is rented.

8. Which inequality represents this situation?

a.     \(15s – 10u ≥ 200\)
b.     \(15s + 10u ≤ 200\)
c.    \( 15s + 10u ≥ 200\)
d.     \(15s + 10u < 200\)

Answer: \(15s + 10u ≥ 200\)

9. Can the beach store rent surfboards for 7 total hours and rent umbrellas for 9 total hours and reach their goal of making $200? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. They would be making \( 15(7) + 10(9) \), or $195.

10. Can the beach store rent surfboards for 10 total hours and rent umbrellas for 4 total hours and reach their goal of making $200? Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: No. They would be making \( 15(10) + 10(4) \), or $190.
