Extending Your Thinking 

Express every number between 1 and 20 at least one way using exactly four 4s and any operations. For example, 1 could be written as \( \frac{4}{4}+4-4 \).

Write your answer here:

For example:

\(1= \frac{4}{4}+4-4 \)

\(2= \frac{4}{4}+\frac{4}{4} \)

\(3= \sqrt4+\sqrt4-\frac{4}{4} \)

\(4= \sqrt4+\sqrt4+4-4 \)

\(5= \sqrt4+\sqrt4+\frac{4}{4} \)

\(6= 4+\frac{4+4}{4} \)

\(7= 4+4-\frac{4}{4} \)

\(8= \sqrt4+\sqrt4+\sqrt4+\sqrt4 \)

\(9= 4+4+\frac{4}{4} \)

\(10= (4+\frac{4}{4})\sqrt4 \)

\(11= \frac{4!\sqrt4-4}{4} \)

\(12= 4(4-\frac{4}{4}) \)

\(13= \frac{4!\sqrt4+4}{4} \)

\(14= 4\cdot4-\frac{4}{\sqrt4} \)

\(15= 4\cdot4-\frac{4}{4} \)

\(16= 4+4+4+4 \)

\(17= 4\cdot4+\frac{4}{4} \)

\(18= 4\cdot4+4-\sqrt4 \)   
   
\(19= 4!-(4+\frac{4}{4}) \)

\(20= 4(4+\frac{4}{4}) \)
