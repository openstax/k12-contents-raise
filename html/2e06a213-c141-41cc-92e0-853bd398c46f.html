The area of a rectangle with a perimeter of \(25\) meters and a side length \(x\): \(A(x)=x \cdot \frac {(25-2x)}{2}\)

\(0<x<12.5\). All positive rational numbers make sense as an input. Having a length or area equal to \(0\) doesn’t make sense either.

Having a length or area equal to 0 doesn’t make sense either. The range gives the minimum and maximum values possible for the area from a perimeter of 25.

Use the following information to answer questions 5-8.

The number of squares as a function of step number \(n\): \(f(n)=n^2+4\)


    1. What is an inequality that represents the domain?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(n \geq 0\) where \(n\) is a whole number. Step numbers that are negative or decimal don’t make sense here. The graph should show points at non-negative integer values (instead of a continuous curve). Everything to the left of the vertical axis does not have meaning here.


    1. What is an inequality that represents the range?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(f≥4\). The number of squares starts with 4 and increases.


    1. What is the vertex of the graph?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The vertex is \((0,4)\). It tells us the number of squares at Step \(0\).


    1. What are the zeros of the graph?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

There are no zeros for this function. There is not a time when there are no squares in the pattern.


Use the following information to answer questions 9-12.

The distance in feet that an object has fallen \(t\) seconds after being dropped: \(g(t)=16t^2\)


    1. What is an inequality that represents the domain?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(t \geq 0\) or only positive numbers and \(0\). Negative values of time don’t make sense here, so the part of the graph to the left side of the vertical axis has no meaning.


    1. What is an inequality that represents the range?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(g≥0\). The distance fallen increases as time increases and would start at \(g=0\).


    1. What is the vertex of the graph?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The vertex is \((0,0)\). It tells us the distance fallen at \(0\) seconds, which is \(0\) feet.


    1. What are the zeros of the graph?

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

The vertex also tells us the zero of the function. It’s the only time when the distance fallen is \(0\) feet.
