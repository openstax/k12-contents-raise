Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Facing Statement

A squirrel runs up and down a tree.

The graph shows the height of the squirrel, \(h\) (feet), as a function of time, \(t\) (seconds).

  1. What is the highest point the squirrel reaches?
  2. Solve \(h(t)=0\). What does this solution tell you about the squirrel?
  3. Find the vertical intercept of the graph. What does it tell you about the squirrel?

Student Response

  1. 14 feet
  2. \(t=4\) means that after 4 seconds, the squirrel is at the bottom of the tree on the ground.
  3. \((0,9)\) means that at the beginning, the squirrel is 9 feet high in the tree.

Response to Student Thinking

More Chances

Students will have more opportunities to understand the mathematical ideas in this cool-down, so there is no need to slow down or add additional work to the next lessons. Instead, use the results of this cool-down to provide guidance for what to look for and emphasize over the next several lessons to support students in advancing their current understanding.
