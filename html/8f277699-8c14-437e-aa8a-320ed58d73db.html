Activity (18 Minutes)

Students wrote the equation of a line in slope-intercept form given the slope and the \( y \)-intercept. In this lesson, students will find the equation of a line given the slope and a point that is not the \( y \)-intercept. Point-slope form is new to students. Deriving the formula from the formula for slope can help students see that there is a connection to the slope, which they already know how to find. Allow students to use a graphing calculator or Desmos where appropriate to check their answers. Point out to students that most graphing calculators require the equations to be in slope-intercept form to graph them.

Launch

Have students work in pairs. When deriving the formula, watch that students do not distribute \( m \) to the \( x \)-variables. While students are working, check to see that they are substituting the correct values into the equation. Remind students they can graph a line given a point and the slope by plotting the given point and using the rise over run to find another point. Remind them the rise is the change in \(y\) and the run is the change in \( x \). For example, a slope of 3 can be written as \( \frac{3}{1} \): the rise is 3, and the run is 1.

Activity Synthesis

Students may get a little confused because \(x\) and \(y\) appear twice in the formula. Point out that the \(x\) and \(y\) with the subscript indicate the \(x\) and \(y\) values that are given to you. The \(x\) and \(y\) with the subscript are replaced by the known values in the given point. Point out to students that writing the equation in the slope-intercept form allows them to graph the equation using a graphing calculator.

Write the Equation of a Line Given the Slope and a Point

Writing an equation using the slope-intercept form works well when you are given the slope and \(y\)-intercept. If you are given the slope and another point that is not the \(y\)-intercept, we need another form of the equation of a line.

The point-slope form of an equation of a line can be derived from the formula for slope. Suppose you have a line that has slope \(m\) and that contains some specific point \( (x_1, y_1) \) as point 1 and some other point, \( (x, y) \), as point 2. You can write the slope of this line and then change it to a different form.

The point-slope form of an equation of a line with slope \(m\) and containing the point \( (x_1, y_1) \) is: \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \). You can use this form to find the equation of a line when you are given the slope and a point.

Example 1

Write the equation of a line with slope \( m = -\frac{1}{3} \) that contains the point \( (6, -4) \). Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

Step 1: Identify the slope.
The slope is given.
\( m = \frac{1}{3} \)

Step 2: Identify the point.
The point is given.
\( (x_1, y_1) \)
\( (6, -4) \)

Step 3: Substitute the values into the point-slope form, \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \). Simplify.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}y-y_1&=&m(x-x_1)\\y-(-4)&=&\frac13(x-6)\\y+4&=&\frac13x+2\end{array}\)

Step 4: Write the equation in slope-intercept form.
\( y = \frac{1}{3}x -2 \)


To find an equation of a line given the slope and a point, follow these steps:


Step 1: Identify the slope.

Step 2: Identify the point.

Step 3: Substitute the values into the point-slope form, \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \).

Step 4: Write the equation in slope-intercept form.


Example 2

Find the equation of a horizontal line that contains the point \( (–2, –6) \). Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

Step 1: Identify the slope. Every horizontal line has slope 0.
\( m = 0 \)

Step 2: Identify the point.
\( (x_1, y_1) \)
\( (–2, –6) \)

Step 3: Substitute the values into the point-slope form, \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \). Simplify.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}y-y_1&=&m(x-x_1)\\y-(-6)&=&0(x-(-2))\\y+6&=&0\end{array}\)

Step 4: Write the equation in slope-intercept form.
\( y = -6 \)
\( y = 0x - 6 \)


Try It: Writing the Equation of a Line Given the Slope and Point

Write the equation of a line with slope \( m = -\frac{2}{5} \) that contains the point \( (10, −5) \). Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

Write down your answer, and then select the solution button to compare your work.

Solution

Here is how to write the equation of a line with a point and slope using a general strategy.

Step 1: Identify the slope.
\( m = -\frac{2}{5}\)

Step 2: Identify the point.
\( (x_1, y_1) \)
\( (10, -5) \)

Step 3: Substitute the values into the point-slope form, \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \). Simplify.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}y-y_1&=&m(x-x_1)\\y-(-5)&=&-\frac25(x-10)\\y+5&=&-\frac25x+4\end{array}\)

Step 4: Write the equation in slope-intercept form.
\( y = -\frac25x - 1 \)
