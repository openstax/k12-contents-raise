Warm Up (10 minutes)


This warm up recalls how to graph linear functions and which lines are parallel or perpendicular. It also introduces how one linear function can be shifted to another.

Later in the lesson, students will graph linear functions only using knowledge of transformations to prepare them for later units when they graph other functions with transformations.

For students who can benefit from a more accessible model, use geoboards and rubber bands. Use one color rubber band for the \(x\)- and \(y\)-axes. Use a different color for each line graphed. Work with students to count spots on the geoboard and discuss the graphs and their relationships.

Launch

Arrange students in groups of 2. Allow students to compare their graphs with their partners and discuss the relationships of the lines. 

Student Activity


  1. Use the Desmos graphing tool or technology outside the course. Graph the following lines given in slope-intercept form: (Students were provided access to Desmos)
  • \(y=3x +4\)
  • \(y=3x - 2\)

Answer:


  1. What is the relationship between the two lines you graphed in question 1?

Answer: They are parallel lines.


  1. When you examine the equations of the lines, what do you notice is the same about the equations?
  • The slopes of the lines are equal.
  • The numbers in front of the variables are the same.

  1. When you examine the equations of the lines, what do you notice is different about the equations?
  • The constant term is different in each equation.
  • The number that is added to the equation is different.
  • The \(y\)-intercepts for each equation is different.

  1. When you examine the graph of the lines, how many vertical spaces are between the two lines?

Answer: There are 6 vertical spaces between the two lines.


  1. On your coordinate grid, use the slope and \(y\)-intercept of each equation to graph each equation. Use a different color for each equation: (Students were provided access to Desmos)
  • \(y=3x-2\)
  • \(y=-\frac13 x-3\)

Answer:


  1. When you examine the graph of the lines in question 6, explain if the lines appear to be perpendicular.
  • No, the lines don't look like they intersect at right angles - so they don't look like they are perpendicular. (Check the settings of your graph, this is incorrect.)
  • Yes, the lines look like they intersect at right angles - so they appear perpendicular. (This is the correct answer.)

Remember to identify perpendicular lines algebraically, examine their slopes. If the slope values are opposite reciprocals, then the lines intersect at right angles and are perpendicular.

Activity Synthesis

Invite students to share their interpretations of the given equations and the new equations they wrote. Then, discuss with students:

  • “Do you notice any other relationships between pairs of lines?”
  • “How many vertical spaces are between lines \(a\) and \(b\)?”
