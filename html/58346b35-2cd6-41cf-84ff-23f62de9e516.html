Solving a System of Equations by Elimination

Let’s reinforce the steps to solve a system of equations using elimination.


Step 1 - Read the problem.

Step 2- Identify the variables and known values. If needed, sketch a picture of the scenario.

Step 3- Write a sentence using the relationship among the values.

Step 4 - Translate the sentence into an equation.


Example

Let’s look at an example. Solve the following system of equations using elimination:

\( \left\{ \begin{array}{c l} 3x - 3y = -24 \\ -3x + 5y = 30 \end{array}\right. \)

Step 1 - Write both equations in standard form. If any coefficients are fractions, clear them.
\( \left\{ \begin{array}{c l} 3x - 3y = -24 \\ -3x + 5y = 30 \end{array}\right. \)

Step 2 - Check to see if the coefficients of one variable are opposites or equivalent.
The coefficients on the \(x\)-variables are opposites. We can add the equations.

Step 3 - Add the equations to eliminate one variable.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}3x-3y&=&-24\\-3x+5y&=&30\\2y&=&6\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Solve for the remaining variable.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}2y&=&6\\y&=&3\end{array}\)

Step 5 - Substitute the solution from Step 4 into one of the original equations. Then solve for the other variable.
\begin{array}{rcl}3x-3y&=&-24\\3x-3(3)&=&-24\\3x-9&=&-24\\3x&=&-15\\x&=&-5\end{array}

Step 6 - Write the solution as an ordered pair.
\( (-5, 3) \)

Step 7 - Check that the ordered pair is a solution to both original equations.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}3x-3y&=&-24\\3(-5)-3(3)&=&-24\\-15-9&=&-24\\-24&=&-24\checkmark\end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}-3x+5y&=&30\\-3(-5)+5(3)&=&30\\15+15&=&30\\30&=&30\checkmark\end{array}\)

Here is how to solve this system of equations using elimination:

\(\begin{array}{rcl}4x-6y&=&8\\x+6y&=&17\end{array}\)

Step 2 - Check to see if the coefficients of one variable are opposites or equivalent.
The coefficients on the \(y\)-variables are opposites. We can add the equations.

Step 3 - Add the equations to eliminate one variable.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}4x\;-\;6y\;&=&\;8\\x\;+\;6y\;&=&\;17\\5x\;&=&\;25\end{array}\)

Step 4 - Solve for the remaining variable.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}5x\;&=&\;25\\x\;&=&\;5\end{array}\)

Step 5 - Substitute the solution from Step 4 into one of the original equations. Then solve for the other variable.
\(\begin{array}{rcl}4x\;-\;6y\;&=&\;8\\\;4(5)\;-\;6y\;&=&\;8\\20\;-\;6y\;&=&\;8\\-6y\;&=&\;-12\\y\;&=&\;2\end{array}\)

Step 6 - Write the solution as an ordered pair.
\( (5, 2) \)

Step 7 - Check that the ordered pair is a solution to both original equations.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}4x-6y&=&8\\4(5)-6(2)&=&8\\20-12&=&8\\8&=&8\checkmark\end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}x+6y&=&17\\(5)+6(2)&=&17\\5+12&=&17\\17&=&17\checkmark\end{array}\)
