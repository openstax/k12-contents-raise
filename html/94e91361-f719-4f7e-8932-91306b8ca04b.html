  1. A sequence is defined recursively by \(f_1=-6, f_n=f_{n-1}-2\), for \(n≥2\). Which of the following gives the nth term for \(n≥0\)?
    1. \(a_{n}=-2+(n-1)(-6) \)
    2. \(a_{n}=-6+(n-1)(-2) \)
    3. \(a_{n}=-6+(n-1)+(-2)\)
    4. \(a_{n}=-6+(n)(-2)\)

ANSWER: \(a_{n}=-6+(n-1)(-2) \)

  1. A sequence is defined recursively by \(f_1=-20, f_n=f_{(n-1)}+5\) for \(n≥2\). Which of the following is the nth term?
    1. \(a_{n}=-20+(n-1)+5\)
    2. \(a_{n}=-20+(n-1)(-2)\)
    3. \(a_{n}=-20+(n-1)(5)\)
    4. \(a_{n}=5+(n-1)(-20)\)

ANSWER: \(a_{n}=-20+(n-1)(5)\)

  1. A sequence is defined recursively by \(f_1=3\), \(f_n=2 \cdot f_{n-1}\), \(n\geq2\). Which of the following gives the nth term for \(n\geq0\)?
    1. \(f_n=3 \cdot 2^{n-1}\)
    2. \(f_n=3^n\)
    3. \(f_n=3+2n\)
    4. \(f_n=3+2^n\)

ANSWER: \(f_n=3 \cdot 2^{n-1}\)

  1. A sequence of numbers is given by \(a_2=1\), \(a_3=5\), \(a_4=9\), \(a_5=13\), \(a_6=17\). Which equation can be used to find the nth term of the sequence?
    1. \( 4n-3\)
    2. \(4n-7\)
    3. \(7n-4\)
    4. \(3n-4\)

ANSWER: b. \(4n-7\)
