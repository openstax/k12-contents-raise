Jada received a gift of $180. In the first week, she spent a third of the gift money. She continues spending a third of what is left each week thereafter.

  1. Which equation best represents the amount of gift money \(g\), in dollars, she has after \(t\) weeks?
  1. \(g=180−\frac{1}{3}t\)
  2. \(g=180 \cdot (\frac{1}{3})^t\)
  3. \(g=\frac{1}{3} \cdot 180^t\)
  4. \(g=180 \cdot (\frac{2}{3})^t\)

\(g=180 \cdot (\frac{2}{3})^t\)

  • Jada's initial gift is $180. Then, she spends a third \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the whole amount each week. This means that she has \(\frac{2}{3}\) left after each week because \(1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \) (the whole amount minus a third).
  • When writing the equation, \(a = 180 \) because that is the starting amount she was given. Then, because she spends \(\frac{1}{3}\) each week, that means only \(\frac{2}{3}\) is leftover. so, \(b = \frac{2}{3}\). The equation that results is \(g = 180 \cdot (23)^t\).
