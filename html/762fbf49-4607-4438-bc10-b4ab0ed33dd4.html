Cool Down (5 minutes)

Launch

In case students are unfamiliar with antibiotics, explain that they kill bacteria and keep bacteria from reproducing.

Student Activity

The graph shows the bacteria population on a petri dish as a function \(f\) of the days \(d\) since an antibiotic is introduced.

  1. What is the approximate value of \(f(4.5)\)?

    Answer: The exact value is 622,431, but reasonable approximations should be greater than 600,000 and less than 650,000.

  2. Approximately what is \(d\) when \(f(d)=400,000\)?

    Answer: The value to one decimal place is 8.7, but reasonable approximations should be greater than 8.5 and less than 9.

  3. Explain what you would do, using your usual graphing technology, to be able to see \(f(15)\) on the graph.

    Answer: Answers vary based on what graphing technology is used.

Response to Student Thinking

Points to emphasize:

Students will have more opportunities to interpret graphs, so use the results from this cool-down to choose points to emphasize in subsequent lessons and highlight and reinforce strategies for identifying points on graphs.
