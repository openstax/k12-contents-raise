Vertex Form with a Leading Coefficient Besides 1

Write \(2x^2+8x+4\) in vertex form (of a quadratic expression).

Original expression
\(2x^2+8x+4\)

Step 1
Rewrite the expression with 2 as a factor.
\(2(x^2+4x+2)\)

Step 2
Add 4 to complete the square.
Subtract 4 to keep the value of the expression the same.
\(2(x^2+4x+4+2−4)\)

Step 3
Combine like terms so we can see the perfect square, \(x^2+4x+4\).
\(2(x^2+4x+4−2)\)

Step 4
Rewrite \(x^2+4x+4\) as a squared expression.
\(2((x+2)^2−2)\)

Step 5
Apply the distributive property to put the expression in vertex form.
\(2(x+2)^2−4\)
