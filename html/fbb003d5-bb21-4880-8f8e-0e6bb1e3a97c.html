Linear models can also be found by hand in some situations when the data follows a linear pattern since the slope remains the same between data points. 

Linear Models from Tables


For questions 1-3, use the scenario and table below:

The table below shows the number of songs Marcus will have in his collection as he adds new songs each month.

Number of months, \(t\) 0 1 2 3
Number of songs, \(N\) 200 215 230 245

  1. What is the initial amount of songs, or the \(y\)-intercept of this situation?

Enter your answer here:

Correct! 200

The correct answer is 200.

  1. What is the slope, or rate of change?

Enter your answer here:

Correct! 15

The correct answer is 15.


  1. Write the equation of the linear model that represents the situation in slope-intercept form.

Enter your answer here:

Compare your answer:

\(N = 15t + 200\)


  1. How many songs will Marcus have in 8 months?

Enter your answer here:

Correct! 320

Compare your answer: 
\(N = 15(8) + 200=320\)

The correct answer is 320.
