When you write a quadratic in factored form, that helps you find the \(x\)-intercepts.
\(y=x^2+2x-3\)
\(y=(x+3)(x-1)\)

When we set \(y=0\), we find the places where the parabola crosses the \(x\)-axis.

\(0=(x+3)(x-1)\)

So, \(0=x+3\) and \(0=x-1\)
\(x=-3\) and 1.

-3 and 1 are called the real solutions to the quadratic equation.

We can also start with the real solutions and use them to write quadratic equations.

When we know that \(x=-3\) and 1, then that means \(0=x+3\) and \(0=x-1\).

So, \(0=(x+3)(x-1)\).

And, using the FOIL method to multiply this out, we come to
\(0=x^2+2x-3.\)

When \(y\) is not equal to zero, we have the following quadratic equation

\(y=x^2+2x-3\), which is exactly where we started!
