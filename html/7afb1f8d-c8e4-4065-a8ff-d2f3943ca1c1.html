Activity

For questions 1 and 2, use the function \(f(x)=x^2\) that is shifted to create the function \(g(x)\) that has a vertex at \((3, -2)\).

  1. Write \(g(x)\) in vertex form.

Answer: \(g(x)=(x-3)^2-2\)
The vertex form of a quadratic is \(f(x)=a(x-h)^2+k\).
Since there is not a vertical stretch or compression or reflection mentioned, the vertex values are substituted for \((h, k)\).
\(g(x)=(x-3)^2+-2\) becomes \(g(x)=(x-3)^2-2\).

  1. Write \(g(x)\) in standard form.

Answer: Compare your answer: \(g(x)=x^2-6x+7\)
\(g(x)=(x-3)^2-2\)
\(g(x)=(x-3)(x-3)-2\)
\(g(x)=x^2-3x-3x+9-2\)
\(g(x)=x^2-6x+7\)
