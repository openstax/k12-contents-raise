For questions 5–9, use the following information.

The path of a toy rocket after launch is modeled by the following data set.

Desmos: Toy Rocket.

The height of the rocket, in feet, \(y\), corresponds to the number of seconds passed, \(x\).

  1. What is the equation for the curve of best fit that models the flight of the toy rocket?
  • \( y = -6.1x^2+ 31x + 1.24\)
  • \(y = -11.9x^2+ 30.2x + 3.64\)
  • \(y = -4.2x^2 + 34x + 6.34\)
  • \(y = -9.2x^2+ 46x + 3.51\)

Answer: \(y = -9.2x^2+ 46x + 3.51\)

  1. Approximately how high will the rocket be 2 seconds after launch?
  • 45.91 feet
  • 53.60 feet
  • 58.71 feet
  • 61.00 feet

Answer: 58.71 feet

  1. At what approximate times after launch will the rocket have a height of 20 feet?
  • 0.26 seconds after launch and 4.74 seconds after launch
  • 0.39 seconds after launch and 4.61 seconds after launch
  • 0.52 seconds after launch and 4.48 seconds after launch
  • 0.82 seconds after launch and 4.18 seconds after launch

Answer: 0.39 seconds after launch and 4.61 seconds after launch

  1. Approximately how long will it take the rocket to reach its maximum height?
  • 1.73 seconds
  • 2.50 seconds
  • 3.20 seconds
  • 5.08 seconds

Answer: 2.50 seconds

  1. Approximately how long will it take for the rocket to land on the ground?
  • 2.50 seconds
  • 4.63 seconds
  • 5.08 seconds
  • 6.12 seconds

Answer: 5.08 seconds
