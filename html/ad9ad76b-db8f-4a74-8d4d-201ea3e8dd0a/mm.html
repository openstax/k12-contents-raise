  1. \(4x^2+8x+25\)

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Compare your answer:

No, not a perfect square. Suggested changes include:

  • Changing the coefficient of the linear term to 20 gives \(4x^2+20x+25\), which is equivalent to \((2x+5)^2\).
  • Changing the coefficient of the linear term to -20 gives \(4x^2−20x+25\), which is equivalent to \((2x−5)^2\).
  • Changing the constant term to 4 gives \(4x^2+8x+4\), which is equivalent to \((2x+2)^2\).
