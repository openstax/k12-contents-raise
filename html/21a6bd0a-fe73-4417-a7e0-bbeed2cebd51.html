Activity (20 minutes)

In this activity, students will work to make a table of values from a given situation, then write an equation for each option. They then graph the equations on the same graph and discuss what the intersection means. 

Launch

Ask students if they have ever belonged to a pool or if their parents belong to a gym. Maybe they have a streaming service at home. These types of memberships cost money and often people are given options with the plans that they choose. It is helpful to understand how to compare the plans and decide which one is the better deal.

Allow students time to read the passage to themselves, then ask them to brainstorm the questions with a partner to make predictions and support their choices. Do the partners agree or disagree?

As students talk with partners, ensure they are recognizing elements of the English sound system in newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters in words such as slope and intercept.
For number 13, practice 3-reads with students and monitor students as they read silently with increasing ease and comprehension for longer periods and demonstrate English comprehension and expand reading skills by employing inferential skills such as predicting, making connections between ideas, drawing inferences and conclusions from text and graphic sources, and finding evidence.

For number 13, consider practice 3-reads with ELL students. Students will read their partner’s paragraph to themselves, then have their partner read the paragraph aloud, then they will read the paragraph aloud. 

Monitor ELL students for the following:

When writing their own paragraph:

  • spell familiar English words with increasing accuracy, and employ English spelling patterns and rules with increasing accuracy as more English is acquired
  •  edit writing for standard grammar and usage, including subject-verb agreement, pronoun agreement, and appropriate verb tenses commensurate with grade-level expectations as more English is acquired

When reading their partner’s paragraph (to themselves and aloud):

  • recognize directionality of English reading such as left to right and top to bottom
  • practice producing sounds of newly acquired vocabulary such as long and short vowels, silent letters, and consonant clusters to pronounce English words in a manner that is increasingly comprehensible specifically for the words slope and \(y\)-intercept
  • distinguish sounds and intonation patterns of English with increasing ease
  • learn relationships between sounds and letters of the English language and decode (sound out) words using a combination of skills such as recognizing sound-letter relationships and identifying cognates, affixes, roots, and base words

Anticipated Misconceptions

Students may need to reactivate the meaning of slope and where to find the slope in the table. When writing the equations, students often switch the slope and \(y\)-intercept values in the slope-intercept formula.

Representing a Linear Function in Tabular Form

A system of equations is two or more equations. Often, each equation in a system of equations represents a situation. Like other equations, the equations that make up the system can be represented by a table. When writing any equation from a table into slope-intercept form, two things are needed: a slope and point, often the \(y\)-intercept.

Example: Suppose a maglev train travels a long distance and maintains a constant speed of 83 meters per second for a period of time once it is 250 meters from the station. Here is a table that shows this constant speed.

Step 1 - Find the \(y\)-intercept.

Notice that when \(t = 0, D(t)=250\). This is the \(y\)-intercept.

Step 2 - Find the slope.

To find the slope, find the change of \(y\) over the change of \(x: m=\frac{83}{1}=83\).

Step 3 - Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

\(y=mx +b\), where \(m\) is the slope and \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept.

\(y =83x +250\)


Try It: Representing a Linear Function in Tabular Form

A new plant food was introduced to a young tree to test its effect on the height of the tree. The table shows the height of the tree, in feet, \(x\) months since the measurements began. Write a linear function, \(H(x)\), where \(x\) is the number of months since the start of the experiment.

\(x\) 0 2 4 8 12
\(H(x)\) 12.5 13.5 14.5 16.5 18.5

Answer:

Here is how to write an equation from the table.

Step 1 - Find the \(y\)-intercept.

Notice that when \(x = 0\), \(H(x)=12.5\). This is the \(y\)-intercept.

Step 2 - Find the slope.

To find the slope, find the change of \(y\) over the change of \(x\): \(m=\frac{1}{2}=0.5\).

Step 3 - Write the equation in slope-intercept form.

\(y=mx +b\), where \(m\) is the slope and \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept.

\(y =0.5x +12.5\)
