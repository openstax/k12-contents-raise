Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

  1. Explain what it means to factor a polynomial completely.

    Answer: To factor a polynomial completely means that every factor you have found, or the polynomial itself, is prime.

For questions 2 - 7, determine if each polynomial is completely factored. If the answer is no, write the completely factored form.

  1. \(3x(8x^2 − 9x − 3)\)

    Answer: yes
  1. \( 60x^2y − 75xy + 30y\)

    Answer: no;
    \(15y(4x^2 − 5x + 2)\)
  1. \(2c(4c^2 + 20cd + 81d^2)\)

    Answer: yes
  1. \(5n^2 + 5n + 12\)

    Answer: yes
  1. \(5q^2 − 15q − 90\)

    Answer: no;
    \(5(q + 3)(q − 6)\)
  1. \(64x^2 + 16xy + y^2\)

    Answer: no;
    \((8x + y)^2\)

Response to Student Thinking

Points to Emphasize

If students are still struggling to identify polynomials that are prime, encourage them to develop a set of questions they can pose to themselves like a checklist. For instance, “Is there one number that has been multiplied into every term of the polynomial?” like in question 6. Or, “Is there a variable (or two) that has been multiplied into every term?” like the y-variable we see in question 3. Is there a number that also has a common number? For trinomials, they might ask a question like, “Is there a set of factors for the constant term that might sum to be the middle term? Or combine with factors of the first term to sum to be the middle term?” or “Is the trinomial ‘perfect’?” etc.

Students will have opportunities to review and practice how to factor polynomial expressions again in Unit 8 when they use factoring to solve quadratic equations.
