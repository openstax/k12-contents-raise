Cool Down (5 minutes)

Student Activity

  1. Explain what it means to factor a polynomial completely.

    ANSWER: To factor a polynomial completely means that every factor you have found, or the polynomial itself, is prime.

Determine if each polynomial is completely factored. If the answer is no, write the completely factored form.

  1. \(3x(8x^2 − 9x − 3)\)

    ANSWER: yes
  1. \( 60x^2y − 75xy + 30y\)

    ANSWER: no;
    \(15y(4x^2 − 5x + 2)\)
  1. \(2c(4c^2 + 20cd + 81d^2)\)

    ANSWER: yes
  1. \(5n^2 + 5n + 12\)

    ANSWER: yes
  1. \(5q^2 − 15q − 90\)

    ANSWER: no;
    \(5(q + 3)(q − 6)\)
  1. \(64x^2 + 16xy + y^2\)

    ANSWER: no;
    \((8x + y)^2\)
