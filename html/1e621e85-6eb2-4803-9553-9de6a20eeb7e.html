Equivalent Forms of Quadratics

Quadratics can be written in different equivalent forms:

  • Standard form: \(y=ax^2+bx+c\)
  • Factored form: \(y = (x + a)(x + b)\)
  • Vertex form: \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\)

Example

Write \(y=(x+2)^2-9\) in standard and factored forms.

Here is how to find the equivalent standard form:

Step 1
Expand the vertex form to multiply the binomials.
\(y=(x+2)(x+2)-9\)

Step 2
Multiply the binomials.
\(y=x^2+2x+2x+4-9\)

Step 3
Combine like terms.
\(y=x^2+4x-5\)

Here is how to find the factored form:

Step 1
From the standard form, find the factors of the trinomial expression \(y=x^2+4x-5\).

Step 2
Examine the trinomial expression.
\(x^2+4x-5\)

Step 3
Factor the trinomial.
\((x + 5)(x - 1)\)

The factored form is \(y = (x + 5)(x - 1)\).

Step 1
Expand the vertex form to multiply the binomials.
\(y=(x-2)(x-2)+4\)

Multiply the binomials.
\(y=x^2-2x-2x+4 + 4\)
