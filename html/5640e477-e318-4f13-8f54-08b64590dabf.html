Students will complete the following questions to practice the skills they have learned in this lesson.

For questions 1 and 2, use this scenario: Two different telephone carriers offer the following plans that a person is considering. Company A has a monthly fee of $20 and charges $0.05/min for calls. Company B has a monthly fee of $5 and charges $0.10/min for calls.

1. Find the model for the entire cost, \( c \), when Company A’s plan is used for \( m \) minutes.

  1. \(c=20 + 0.05m\)
  2. \(c= 5 + 0.10m\)
  3. \(c= 20 + 0.10m\)
  4. \(c=5+ 0.05m\)

ANSWER: \( c=20 + 0.05m \)


2. Find the model for the entire cost, \( c \), when Company B’s plan is used for \( m \) minutes.

  1. \(c=20 + 0.05m\)
  2. \(c= 5 + 0.10m\)
  3. \(c= 20 + 0.10m\)
  4. \(c=5+ 0.05m\)

ANSWER: \( c= 5 + 0.10m \)

3. If a carpenter sawed a 10-ft board into two sections and one section was \( n \) ft long, how long would the other section, \( S \), be in terms of \( n \)?

  1. \(S=\frac{10}n\)
  2. \(S=10-n\)
  3. \(S=10-2n\)
  4. \(S=\frac{10}{2n}\)

ANSWER: \( S=10-n \)

5. Choose the equation that represents the number of teaspoons, \( t \) , contained in a cup, \(C\).

  1. \(c = 48t\)
  2. \(c=t/48\)
  3. \(c = t-48\)
  4. \(c=\frac t3\)

6. The volume of dry goods, like apples or peaches, can be measured using bushels, pecks, and quarts. A bushel contains 4 pecks, and a peck contains 8 quarts.

What is the relationship between the number of bushels, \( b \) , and the number of quarts, \(q\)?

  1. \(b=\frac q{32}\)
  2. \(b=\frac q{12}\)
  3. \(b=\frac q{64}\)
  4. \(b=32q\)

ANSWER:  \(b=\frac q{32}\)
