Thinking back to the last activity, you learned about the \(r\)-value. Write these characteristics about the \(r\)-value in your notebook.

The \(r\)-value is called a correlation coefficient. A correlation coefficient is one way to measure the strength of a linear relationship.

  • The sign of \(r\) is the same as the sign of the slope of the best fit line.
  • The values for \(r\) go from –1 to 1 and include –1 and 1.
  • The closer \(r\) is to 1 or –1, the stronger the linear relationship between the variables.
  • The closer \(r\) is to 0, the weaker the linear relationship between the variables.
