Activity

Here are some situations that each relate two quantities and involve two constraints. For each situation, find the pair of values that meet both constraints and Explain your reasoning.

  1. A dining hall had a total of 25 tables: some long rectangular tables and some round ones. Long tables can seat 8 people. Round tables can seat 6 people. On a busy evening, all 190 seats at the tables are occupied.

How many long tables, \( x \), and how many round tables, \( y \), are there?

Enter your answer here:

There are many ways to solve this: graphing and algebraic solving, guessing and checking, or using tables. Answers are:

20 long tables and 5 round tables. For example:

  • \(x+y=25\)
  • \(8x+6y=190\)
  • Making a table shows that when the total number of tables is 25 and the total number of seats is 190, the number of long tables, x, is 20 and the number of round tables, y, is 5.

2.  A family bought a total of 16 adult and child tickets to a magic show. Adult tickets are $10.50 each, and child tickets are $7.50 each. The family paid a total of $141.

How many of adult tickets, \( a \), and child tickets, \( c \), did they buy?

Enter your answer here:

There are many ways to solve this: graphing and algebraic solving, guessing and checking, or using tables. Answers are:

7 adults and 9 children. For example:

  • \(a+c=16\)
  • \(10.50a+7.50c=141\)
  • When the equations are graphed, the intersection of the two lines is at (7,9).

At a poster shop, Han paid $16.80 for 2 large posters and 3 small posters of his favorite band. Kiran paid $14.15 for 1 large poster and 4 small posters of his favorite TV shows. Posters of the same size have the same price.

Find the price of a large poster, \( \ell \), and the price of a small poster, \( s \).

Enter your answer here:

There are many ways to solve this: graphing and algebraic solving, guessing and checking, or using tables. Answers are:

$4.95 for a large poster and $2.30 for a small poster. For example:

  • When the equations \(2ℓ+3s=16.80\) and \(ℓ+4s=14.15\) are graphed, the intersection of the two lines is at \((4.95, 2.30)\).
