  1. A quadratic function \(f\) is defined by \(f(x)=(x-7)(x+3)\).
    1. Without graphing, identify the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph of \(f\).
      • \((7,0),(-3,0)\)
      • \((-7,0),(3,0)\)
      • \((0,7),(0,-3)\)
      • \((0,-7),(0,3)\)

ANSWER: \((7,0),(-3,0)\). If x is 7, \((7-7)(7+3)\) gives 0 10, which is 0.   If \(x\) is -3, \((-3-7)(-3+3)\) gives -10 0, which is 0.

    1. Use the standard form of \((x-7)(x+3)\) to identify the \(y\)-value of the \(y\)-intercept of the graph of \(f\).

  1. Noah said that if we graph \(y=(x-1)(x+6)\), the \(x\)-intercepts will be at \((1,0)\) and \((-6,0)\). Is Noah correct?
    • Yes
    • No

ANSWER: Yes. We can check by substituting 1 for \(x\) and -6 for \(x\) and see if they produce an output of 0. If \(x\) is 1, \((1-1)(1+6)\) gives 0 7, which is 0. If \(x\) is – 6, \((-6-1)(-6+6)\) gives -7 0, which is 0.
