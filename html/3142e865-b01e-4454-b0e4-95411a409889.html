Student Activity

The scatter plot shows the maximum noise level when different numbers of people are in a stadium. The linear model is given by the equation \(y = 1.5x + 22.7\), where \(y\) represents maximum noise level and \(x\) represents the number of people, in thousands, in the stadium.

\(y = 1.5x + 22.7\)


  1. The slope of the linear model is 1.5. What does this mean in terms of the maximum noise level and the number of people?

Answer: For example: For every additional thousand people in the stadium, the noise level increases by about 1.5 decibels.

  1. A sports announcer states that there are 65,000 fans in the stadium. Estimate the maximum noise level. Be prepared to show your reasoning.

Answer: 120.2 decibels

  1. Is this estimate reasonable?

Answer: Compare your answer: Your answer may vary, but here is a sample.

It is a reasonable value since the data seem to fit a linear model well, so it is probably near this value.
