The value of \(f\) increases by 2 whenever the input increases by 1. For example:

  1. From the graph, we can see the \(y\)-value increasing by 2 when the \(x\)-value increases by 1.
  2. \(f(1)=7\) and \(f(2)=9\). The difference between \(f(1)\) and \(f(2)\) is 2. Likewise, \(f(20)=45\) and \(f(19)=43\), and \(f(20)−f(19)=2\).
