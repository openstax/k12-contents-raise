  1. Which of the following points is the vertex of the quadratic function \(f(x)=x^2+8x+22\)?
  • \((8, 22) \)
  • \( (0, 22) \)
  • \( (-4, 6) \)
  • \( (4, 6) \)

Answer: \((-4, 6)\)

\(f(x)=x^2+8x+16+22-16\)

Vertex: \((-4, 6)\)

For questions 5 and 6, write an equation in vertex form of the quadratic function that has a vertex at \((-4, 1)\) and also goes through the point \((6, -1)\).

  1. Find the value of \(a\) for vertex form in simplest form.

Answer: \(\frac{-1}{50}\)

\(-1=a(6-(-4))^2+(1)\)
\(-1=a(10)^2+1\)
\(-2=100a\)
\(a=\frac{-2}{100}=\frac{-1}{50}\)

  1. Write the equation in vertex form.

Answer: \(y=\frac{-1}{50}(x+4)^2+1\)

For questions 7 and 8, write an equation in vertex form of the quadratic function that has a vertex at \((-2, -3)\) and also goes through the point \((4, 2)\).

  1. Find the value of \(a\) for vertex form in simplest form.

Answer: \(\frac{5}{36}\)

\(2=a(4-(-2))^2+(-3)\)
\(2=a(6)^2+(-3)\)
\(2+3=36a+(-3)+3\)
\(5=36a\)
\(a=\frac{5}{36}\)

  1. Write the equation in vertex form.

Answer: \(y=\frac{5}{36}(x+2)^2-3\)


For questions 9 and 10, use the function \(f(x)=x^2\) that is shifted to create the function \(g(x)\) that has a vertex at \((-4, 6)\).

  1. Write \(g(x)\) in vertex form.

Answer: \(g(x)=(x+4)^2+6\)

Substitute the vertex in for \((h, k)\)
\(g(x)=(x-(-4))^2+6\)
\(g(x)=(x+4)^2+6\)

  1. Write \(g(x)\) in standard form.

Answer: \(g(x)=x^2-6x+7\)

Multiply the binomials then simplify.
\(g(x)=(x+4)^2+6\)
\(g(x)=(x+4)(x+4)+6\)
\(g(x)=x^2+4x+4x+16+6\)
\(g(x)=x^2-6x+7\)
