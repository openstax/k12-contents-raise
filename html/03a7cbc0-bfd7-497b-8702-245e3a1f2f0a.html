Cool Down (5 minutes)


Student Activity

The expression \(16t^2\) represents the distance in feet an object falls after \(t\) seconds. Assume an object is dropped from a height of 906 feet.

  1. What is the height of the object in feet 2 seconds after it is dropped?

    Answer: The object is at 842 feet 2 seconds after it’s dropped. \(906-16(2)^2=906-16(4)=906-64=842\)
  1. Write an expression representing the height of the object in feet \(t\) seconds after it is dropped.

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Answer: \(906-16t^2\)


Response to Student Thinking
