Solve by Using Perfect Squares and Completing the Square 

Solve \(2x^2+5x+2=0\) by completing the square.

Step 1
Divide all terms by the leading coefficient.
\(\frac{2x^2+5x+2}{2}=\frac{0}{2}\)
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+1=0\)

Step 2
Move the constant to the right side of the equation.
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x=-1\)

Step 3
Complete the square and add to each side: \((\frac{5}{2} \times \frac{1}{2})^2=\frac{25}{16}\).
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+\frac{25}{16}=-1+\frac{25}{16}\)

Step 4
Simplify.  
(Hint: create a common denominator before adding.)
\(x^2+\frac{5}{2}x+\frac{25}{16}=-\frac{16}{16}+\frac{25}{16}\)
\((x+\frac{5}{4})^2=\frac{9}{16}\)

Step 5
Take the square root of each side.
\(\sqrt{(x+\frac{5}{4})^2}= \sqrt{\frac{9}{16}}\)
\(x+\frac{5}{4}= \pm \frac{3}{4}\)

Step 6
Write two equations and solve.
\(x+\frac{5}{4}=\frac{3}{4}\)         \(x+\frac{5}{4}=-\frac{3}{4}\)
\(x=-\frac{2}{4}=-\frac{1}{2}\)   \(x=-\frac{8}{4}=-2\)

Try It: Solve by Using Perfect Squares and Completing the Square

Solve \(4x^2+36x+37=0\).

Write down your answer, then select the solution button to compare your work.

Here is how to solve using completing the square:

Step 1
Divide all terms by the leading coefficient.
\(\frac{4x^2+36x+37}{4}=\frac{0}{4}\)
\(x^2+9x+\frac{37}{4}=0\)

Step 2
Move the constant to the right side of the equation.
\(x^2+9x=-\frac{37}{4}\)

Step 3
Complete the square and add to each side: \((9 \times \frac{1}{2})^2=\frac{81}{4}\).
\(x^2+9x+\frac{81}{4}=-\frac{37}{4}+\frac{81}{4}\)

Step 4
Simplify.  
\((x+\frac{9}{2})^2=11\)

Step 5
Take the square root of each side.
\(\sqrt{(x+\frac{9}{2})^2}=\sqrt{11}\)
\((x+\frac{9}{2})= \pm \sqrt{11}\)

Step 6
Write two equations and solve.
\(x+\frac{9}{2}=\sqrt{11}\)       \(x+\frac{9}{2}=-\sqrt{11}\)
\(x=-\frac{9}{2}+\sqrt{11}\)       \(x=-\frac{9}{2}-\sqrt{11}\)
