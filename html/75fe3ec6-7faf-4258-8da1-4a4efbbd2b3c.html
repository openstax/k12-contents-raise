  1. The temperature was recorded at several times during the day. Function \(T\) gives the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit, \(n\) hours since midnight. Here is a graph for this function. For each time interval, decide if the average rate of change is positive, negative, or zero:

    1. From \(n = 1\) to \(n = 5\)

Answer: negative

    1. From \(n = 5\) to \(n = 8\)

Answer: positive

    1. From \(n = 10\) to \(n = 20\)

Answer: positive

    1. From \(n = 15\) to \(n = 18\)

Answer: zero

    1. From \(n = 20\) to \(n = 24\)

  1. The graph shows the total distance, in feet, walked by a person as a function of time, in seconds.

    1. Was the person walking faster between 20 and 40 seconds or between 80 and 100 seconds?

Answer: Between 80 and 100 seconds

    1. Was the person walking faster between 0 and 40 seconds or between 40 and 100 seconds?

Answer: Between 0 and 40 seconds

  1. The height, in feet, of a squirrel running up and down a tree is a function of time, in seconds. Here are statements describing the squirrel’s movement during four intervals of time. Match each description with a statement about the average rate of change of the function for that interval.

      1. The average rate of change is negative.
      2. The average rate of change is zero.
      3. The average rate of change is small and positive.
      4. The average rate of change is large and positive.

    1. The squirrel runs up the tree very fast.

Answer: The average rate of change is large and positive.

    1. The squirrel starts and ends at the same height.

Answer: The average rate of change is zero.

    1. The squirrel runs down the tree.

Answer: The average rate of change is negative.

    1. The squirrel runs up the tree slowly.

Answer: The average rate of change is small and positive.
