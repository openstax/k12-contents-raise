Consider the equation \( 1.5x + 4.5y = 18 \). For each questionexplain your reasoning.

  1. If we graph the equation, what is the slope of the graph?

Answer: The slope is \( -\frac13 \). Rearranging the equation to isolate \( y \) gives \( y=- \frac13 x+4 \).

  1. Where does the graph intersect the \( y \)-axis?

Answer: The graph intersects the \( y \)-axis at \( (0,4) \). The 4 in \( y=- \frac13 x+4 \) tells us where the graph crosses the \( y \)-axis.

  1. Where does it intersect the \( x \)-axis?

Answer: The graph intersects the \( x \)-axis at \( (12,0) \). Substituting 0 for \( y \) in the original equation and solving for \( x \) gives \( x=12 \).
