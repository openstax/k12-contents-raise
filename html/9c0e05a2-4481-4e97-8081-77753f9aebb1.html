Activity (20 minutes)

This activity prompts students to write equations and has them make connections between the different forms of equations. Students will write linear equations in slope-intercept form, standard form, and point-slope form and work to change from one form to the other.

As students work, facilitate discussion about when each form would be helpful to use. Also, make sure that students are entering values in the correct places in the form. When transferring from one form to another, students may need reminders about the use of inverse operations.

Launch

Ask students if they have ever seen any of the forms before. If so, what do they remember? They should be able to at least say what \(m\) stands for, after activity 4.7.2. Place students in pairs to work through the activity. Independent students can go through the activity, but some students may need help working through substituting into the formulas throughout the activity.

Student Activity

In Algebra, equations are commonly written in three different ways: slope-intercept form, point-slope form, and standard form. They are equivalent ways to write the same equation, but each one is useful for different reasons.

Slope-Intercept Form Point-Slope Form Standard Form
\(y = mx + b\) Given: \(m =\) slope \(b =\) \(y\)-intercept \(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\) Given: a point \((x_1,y_1)\) \(m=\) slope \(Ax + By = C\) \(m = - \frac AB = slope\) Where: There are no fractions \(A\) cannot be negative.

For slope-intercept form, the slope and \(y\)-intercept are needed:

  1. Write an equation in slope-intercept form for a line whose slope is -2 and \(y\)-intercept is \((0,4)\).

Answer: \(y = -2x +4\)

Substitute the slope in for \(m\) and the \(y\)-value of the \(y\)-intercept in for \(b\) in the formula \(y= mx + b\).

For point-slope form, the slope and one point are needed:

  1. Write an equation in point-slope form for a line whose slope is \(\frac12\) and goes through the point \((3,-5)\)

Answer: \(y+5=\frac{1}{2}(x-3)\)

Substitute in the formula for \((x_1,y_1)\) and slope.

\(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\)
\(y-(-5)=\frac{1}{2}(x-3)\)
\(y+5=\frac{1}{2}(x-3)\)

Standard form of a linear equation is often used to find \(x\)- and \(y\)-intercepts of graphs.

Often, an equation starts in another form and gets put into standard form.

Standard form does not have fractions or decimals and cannot lead with a negative number.

  1. What is the slope in the equation \(y=2x+3\)?

Answer: Compare your answer: The slope is 2.

  1. Write the equation \( y = 2x + 3\) in standard form.

Answer: \(2x - y = -3\)

Write the equation in the form \(Ax + By = C\)

Step 1 - Rearrange terms so the \(x\) term and the \(y\) term are both on the left side of the equation.
Subtract \(2x\) from both sides:
\(y - 2x = 2x - 2x + 3\)

Step 2 - Write the \(x\) term first and simplify.
\(-2x + y = 3\)

Step 3 - Multiply both sides by -1, so as not to lead with a negative.
\(-1(-2x + y = 3)\)
\(2x - y = -3\)

Work with a partner to write equations in different forms.

  1. What is the slope of \(y-3=2(x+4)\)

Answer: Compare your answer: The slope is 2.

  1. Write the equation \(y-3=2(x+4)\) in slope-intercept form.

Answer: \(y = 2x + 11\)

\(y-3=2(x+4)\)

Step 1 - Distribute.
\(y-3=2x+8\)

Step 2 - Add 3 to each side to solve for \(y\).
\(y-3+3=2x+8+3\)

Step 3 - Simplify.
\(y = 2x +11\)

  1. Write the equation \(y+5=-3(x-2)\) into standard form.

Answer: \(3x + y = 1\)

Step 1 - Distribute.
\(y+5=-3x+6\)

Step 2 - Rearrange terms so the \(x\) term and the \(y\) term are both on the left side of the equation.
Add \(3x\) to both sides.
\(y+5+3x=-3x+3x+6\)

Step 3 - Bring the constants to the right side of the equation.
Subtract 5 from each side.
\(y+5-5+3x=-3x+3x+6-5\)

Step 4 - Simplify.
Make sure that the \(x\) term is in front of the \(y\) term on the left side.
\(3x +y =1\)

  1. Write the equation \(y = \frac{1}{3}x-4\) in standard form.

Answer: \(x -3y=12\)

Step 1- Get rid of the fractions in the equation.
Multiply every term by 3.
\(3y = 3(\frac{1}{3}x-4)\)

Step 2 - Distribute and simplify.
\(3y=x -12\)

Step 3 - Rearrange terms so the \(x\) term and the \(y\) term are both on the left side of the equation.
Subtract \(x\) from both sides.
\(3y - x = x - x -12\)

Step 4 - Simplify.
\(3y - x = -12\)

Step 5 - Write the equation so the \(x\) term is first.
\(-x + 3y = -12\)

Step 6 - Multiply each term by -1, so as not to lead with a negative.
\(-1(-x + 3y = -12)\)
\(x - 3y = 12\)

  1. What is the slope of \(3x + 12y = 24\)?

Answer: Compare your answer:

The slope is \(m = -\frac AB\).
\(m = -\frac{3}{12}\)
\(m = -\frac14\)

Support for English Language Learners

MLR 1 Stronger and Clearer Each Time: Writing, Listening, Conversing

Use this routine to prepare students for the whole-class discussion by providing them with multiple opportunities to clarify their explanations through conversation. Before the whole-class discussion begins, give students time to meet with 1-2 other groups to share their steps for rewriting equations from one form to the other. Invite listeners to press for accurate mathematical language to help speakers move away from informal language such as plus, minus, take away, times, etc. Encourage the use of more formal language such as add, subtract, multiply, etc.

Design Principle(s): Optimize output (for explanation); Cultivate conversation

Provide support for students

Distribute graphic organizers to the students to assist them with participating in this routine.


Anticipated Misconceptions

If students have trouble with where to substitute values into the formulas, it can be helpful to have them highlight variables and the matching value, or use colored pencils to color code the value that will substitute into the formula. For example, in \(y = mx + b\), if \(m = 3\) and the \(y\)-intercept is -2, highlight the \(m\) and the 3 in pink in the formula and the given value, then highlight the \(b\) and the \(y\)-intercept value in yellow.

Activity Synthesis

Invite pairs to show how they got each answer. Ask if they followed the same order of steps as the feedback or if a different set of steps work.

Ask students:

  • Which form do you find the easiest to work with? The hardest?
  • What do you need to watch out for in each formula?

Sample answer might include:

  • Slope-intercept form: be careful to substitute the slope and \(y\)-intercept in the correct places
  • Point-slope form: be sure to substitute the point into \((x_1,y_1)\), not \((x,y)\).
  • Standard form: make sure to get rid of decimals and fractions, and write the \(x\) term first. Be sure not to lead with a negative number in the front.

If time allows, have students create their own equations in one form and ask a partner to write it in a different form.

4.7.3: Self Check

After the activity, students will answer the following question to check their understanding of the concepts explored in the activity.

QUESTION:

Write the equation of the line in point-slope form that has a slope of 7 and goes through \((-1,2)\).

Answers Feedback
\(y = 7i + 9\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: This is slope-intercept form. The correct answer is \(y-2=7(x+1)\).
\(y-2=7(x+1)\) That’s correct! Check yourself: Substitute into point-slope form \(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\), where \(m\) is 7 and 1 and \(y_1=2\)
\(y-2=7(x-1)\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: Recall that \(x - (-1) \) becomes \(x + 1\). The correct answer is \(y-2=7(x+1)\).
\(2-y=7(-1-x)\) Incorrect. Let’s try again a different way: Be sure to substitute the point into \(x_1\) and \(y_1\). The correct answer is y-\(2=7(x+1)\).

Example 2

Find the equation of a line with slope −9 and \(y\)-intercept \((0,−4)\) in slope-intercept form.

Step 1 - Identify the \(m\) and \(b\).
\(m = -9\), \(b = -4\)

Step 2 - Substitute into the formula.
\(y = -9x - 4\)


Example 3

Find the equation of a line in point-slope form given a slope of \(-\frac13\) and the point \((6,-4)\)

Step 1 - Identify the \(m\) and \((x_1,y_1)\).
\(m = -\frac{1}{3} x_1=6 y_1= -4\)

Step 2 - Substitute into the formula.
\(y-(-4)=-\frac{1}{3}(x-6)\)

Step 3 - Simplify
\(y+4=-\frac{1}{3}(x-6)\)


Example 4

Write the equation \(y+4=-\frac{1}{3}(x-6)\) in standard form.

Step 1 - Multiply to get rid of the fraction.
Remember, standard form cannot have decimals or fractions.
Multiply every term by 3.
\(3(y+4)=3(-\frac{1}{3}(x-6))\)
\(3y+12=-(x-6)\)

Step 2 - Distribute.
\(3y+12=-x+6\)

Step 3 - Rearrange terms so the \(x\) term and the \(y\) term are both on the left side of the equation.
Add \(x\) to both sides.
\(3y+12+x=-x+x+6\)

Step 4 - Simplify.
\(3y+12+x=6\)

Step 5 - Bring the constants to the right side.
Subtract 12 from both sides.
\(3y+12+x-12=6-12\)

Step 6 - Simplify.
\(3y+x=-6\)

Step 7 - Write the \(x\) term before the \(y\) term.
\(3x+y=-6\)


Try It: Writing Linear Equations in Different Forms

Write the equation \(y-3=2(x+5)\) in standard form.

Answer: \(2x-y=-13\)

Step 1 - Distribute.
\(y-3=2x+10\)

Step 2 - Rearrange terms so the \(x\) term and the \(y\) term are both on the left side of the equation.
Subtract \(2x\) from both sides.
\(y-3-2x=2x-2x+10\)

Step 3 - Simplify.
\(y-3-2x=10\)

Step 4 - Bring the constants to the right side.
Add 3 to both sides.
\(y-3-2x+3=10+3\)

Step 5 - Simplify.
\(y-2x=13\)

Step 6 - Write the \(x\) term before the \(y\) term.
\(-2x+y=13\)

Step 7- Multiply each term by -1.
Standard form cannot lead with a negative.
\(-1(-2x+y=13)\)

Step 8 - Simplify.
\(2x-y=-13\)
