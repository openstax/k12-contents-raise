Real-World Applications

In this unit, your student will learn that a monomial is an algebraic expression with exactly one term. A polynomial is a monomial or two or more monomials combined by addition or subtraction. Each monomial is a term and the terms are separated by addition or subtraction. You can classify a polynomial by the number of terms and by the degree, which is the value of the highest power of the variable of its individual terms.

Here are some examples of classifying polynomials by the number of terms and the degree of the polynomial.

Classify each polynomial by number of terms and by degree.

  1. \(5x^2\)
    Exactly one term: monomial
    Highest power of the variable: \(2\)
  1. \(4x + 6y\)
    Exactly two terms: binomial
    Highest power of the variable: \(1\)
  1. \(3x^2 + 8y^3 –z\)
    Exactly three terms: trinomial
    Highest power of the variable: \(3\)

You can add and subtract polynomials by combining like terms. Like terms have the same variables to the same exponent. 

Add or subtract.

  1. \((3k^2+6k+2)+(9k^2-7k-4)\)
    Use the commutative property to move like terms and then the associative property to regroup them.
    \((3k^2+9k^2)+(6k-7k)+(2-4)\)
    Combine like terms.
    \( 12k^2-k-2\)
  1. \((3x^2+7xy-3y^2)-(5x^2-4y^2)\)
    Use the distributive property to remove parentheses from the subtracted terms.
    \((3x^2+7xy-3y^2)-5x^2+4y^2\)
    Use the commutative property to move like terms and then the associative property to regroup them.
    \((3x^2-5x^2)+7xy+(-3y^2+4y^2)\)
    Combine like terms.
    \(-2x^2+7xy+y^2\)

You can multiply polynomials by using the distributive property.

Multiply.

  1. \(2(p+6)\)
    Use the distributive property to multiply 2 by each term in parentheses.
    \(2p+2 \cdot 6\)
    Multiply.
    \(2p+12\)
  1. \(6x(x-5)\)
    Use the distributive property to multiply 6x by each term in parentheses.
    \(6x \cdot x-6x \cdot 5\)
    Use the commutative property to rearrange factors.
    \(6x \cdot x-6 \cdot 5 \cdot x\)
    Multiply. Use the property of exponents to multiply \(x^1 \cdot x^1=x^2\).
    \(6x^2-30x\)

Here is a task for you to try with your student:

A rectangular garden plot has a length that can be expressed as \(x + 5\) feet and a width expressed as \(x\) feet.

  1. Write a polynomial expression to represent the perimeter of the garden.
  2. Write a polynomial expression to represent the area of the garden.
  3. What is the perimeter if \(x = 15\)?
  4. What is the area of \(x = 15\)?

Solution:

  1. The perimeter of a rectangle is the sum of the lengths of the sides. \(2 \cdot \) length \( + 2 \cdot \) width
    \(2(x+5)+2(x)\)
    Use the distributive property to multiply \(2\) by each term in parentheses.
    \(2 \cdot x+2 \cdot 5+2(x)\)
    Multiply.
    \(2x+10+2x\)
    Use the commutative property to gather like terms.
    \(2x+2x+10\)
    Combine like terms.
    \(4x+10\)
    The perimeter is \((4x+10)\) feet.
  1. The area of a rectangle is length times width.
    \((x+5)(x)\)
    Use the distributive property to multiply \(x\) by each term in parentheses.
    \((x \cdot x)+(5 \cdot x)\)
    Multiply.
    \(x^2+5x\)
    The area is \((x^2+5x)\) square feet.
  1. The expression for perimeter is \(4x+10\).
    Substitute \(15\) for \(x\) into the expression for perimeter.
    \(4(15)+10\)
    Multiply.
    \(60 + 10\)
    Add.
    \(70\)
    The perimeter is \(70\) feet.
  1. The expression for area is \(x^2+5x\).
    Substitute \(15\) for \(x\) into the expression for area.
    \((15)^2+5(15)\)
    Evaluate the exponent and multiply.
    \(225+75\)
    Add.
    \(300\).
    The area is \(300\) square feet.
