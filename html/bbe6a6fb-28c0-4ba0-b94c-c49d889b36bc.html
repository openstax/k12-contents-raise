Write Linear Functions from a Graph

You can determine the slope and intercept of a line if the equation is written in slope-intercept form, \(y = mx + b\). You can also do the reverse—identify the slope and \(y\)-intercept and use them to find the equation of the line.

Write the linear function shown in the graph.

To find the slope, choose two points on the graph. The \(y\)-intercept is \((0, -4)\), and the graph passes through \((3, -2)\).

Step 1
Find the slope by counting the rise and run.
\(m = \frac {rise}{run}\)

Step 2
\(m = \frac23\)

Step 3
Find the \(y\)-intercept.
\(y\)-intercept \((0,-4)\)

Step 4
Substitute the values into \(y=mx+b\).
\(y=mx+b\)

Step 5
\(y = \frac{2}{3}x=4\)

Step 6
Rewrite the equation using function notation.
\(f(x) = \frac{2}{3}x - 4\)

Here is how to write a linear function from a graph.

Step 1
Choose two points from the graph.
\((0, 3)\) and \((3, -1)\)

Step 2
Find the slope by counting the rise and run.
\(m=\frac{rise}{run}\)

Step 3
\(m=-\frac43\)

Step 4
Find the \(y\)-intercept.
\((0, 3)\)

Step 5
Substitute the values into \(y = mx + b\).
\(y = mx + b\)

Step 6
\(y=-\frac{4}{3}x+3\)

Step 7
Rewrite the equation using function notation.
\(f(x)=-\frac{4}{3}x+3\)
