1. Lin is solving this system of equations: 

\( \begin {cases} 6x-5y=34\\3x+2y=8 \end {cases} \)


She starts by rearranging the second equation to isolate the \(y\) variable: \( y = 4 - 1.5x \). She then substitutes the expression \( 4 - 1.5x \) for \( y \) in the first equation, as shown:

\( 6x-5(4-1.5x)=34 \)
\( 6x-20-7.5x=34 \)
\( -1.5x=54 \)
\( x=-36 \)


\( y=4-1.5x \)

\( y=4-1.5 \bullet(-36) \)

\( y=58 \)

Does Lin's solution of (–36, 58) makes both equations true?

  1. yes; Her solution makes both equations true.
  2. No; Her solution does not make both equations true.

ANSWER: No; Her solution does not make both equations true.

2. Which of the following is true of Lin’s solution?

  1. Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 4 and y = –2, or (4,–2)
  2. Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 9 and y = 4, or (9,4)
  3. Lin made an addition error. The correct answer is x = 0 and y = 4, or (0,4)
  4. Lin’s solution of (–36, 58) is correct.

ANSWER: Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 4 and y = –2, or (4,–2)
