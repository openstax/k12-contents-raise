\( \begin {cases} 6x-5y=34\\3x+2y=8 \end {cases} \)

\( 6x-5(4-1.5x)=34 \)
\( 6x-20-7.5x=34 \)

\( -1.5x=54 \)
\( x=-36 \)


\( y=4-1.5x \)

\( y=4-1.5 \cdot(-36) \)

\( y=58 \)

Does Lin’s solution of \((-36, 58)\) makes both equations in the system true?

  • yes; Her solution makes both equations true.
  • No; Her solution does not make both equations true.

3. \(\left\{\begin{array}{l}2x\;+\;y\;=\;-2\\3x\;-\;y\;=\;7\end{array}\right.\)

  • (−1,0)
  • (0,−2)
  • (1,−4)
  • (2,6)

Answer: \( (1,-4) \)

\(\begin{array}{rcl}2x+y&=&-2 \\ 2(1)+(-4)&=&-2 \\ 2-4&=&-2 \\ -2&=&-2\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)


\(\begin{array}{rcl}3x-y&=&7 \\ 3(1)-(-4)&=&7 \\ 3+4&=&7 \\ 7&=&7\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)

(1,-4) Makes both equations in the system true, therefore it is a solution.

4. \(\left\{\begin{array}{l}x\;-\;3y\;=\;-9\\2x\;+\;5y\;=\;4\end{array}\right.\)

  • (−6,1)
  • (2,0)
  • (0,3)
  • (−3,2)

Worked Solution.

\(\begin{array}{rcl}x−3y&=&−9 \\ (-3)−3(2)&=&−9 \\ -3−6=&−&9 \\ -9&=&-9\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)


\(\begin{array}{rcl}2x+5y=4 \\ 2(-3)+5(2)&=&4 \\ -6+10&=&4 \\ 4&=&4\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)

(-3,2) Makes both equations in the system true, therefore it is a solution.

11. Tyler and Han are trying to solve this system by substitution:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}x+3y=-5\\9x+3y=3\end{array}\right.\)

{ y = − 3 x − 6 6 x + 2 y = − 12 .

Tyler’s first step is to isolate \(x\) in the first equation to get \( x = -5 - 3y \).

Han’s first step is to isolate \(3y\) in the first equation to get \( 3y = -5 - x \).

Solve using both methods to show that both first steps can be used to solve the system and will yield the same solution.

What is the solution to the system?

  • (1,-2)
  • (0,1)
  • (4,-3)
  • (-5,0)

Answer: (1,-2)

Worked Solution.

\(\begin{array}{rcl} x+3y&=&−5 \\ 1+3(-2)&=&−5 \\ 1-6&=&−5 \\ -5&=&-5\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)


\(\begin{array}{rcl} 9x+3y&=&3 \\ 9(1)+3(-2)&=&3 \\ 9-6&=&3 \\ 3&=&3\checkmark \\ \end{array}\)

(1,-2) Makes both equations in the system true, therefore it is a solution.
