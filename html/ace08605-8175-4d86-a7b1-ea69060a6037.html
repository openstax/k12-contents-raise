1. Lin is solving this system of equations: 

\( \begin {cases} 6x-5y=34\\3x+2y=8 \end {cases} \)

She starts by rearranging the second equation to isolate the \(y\) variable: \( y = 4 - 1.5x \). She then substitutes the expression \(4 - 1.5x\) for \( y \) in the first equation, as shown:

\( 6x-5(4-1.5x)=34 \)
\( 6x-20-7.5x=34 \)

\( -1.5x=54 \)
\( x=-36 \)


\( y=4-1.5x \)

\( y=4-1.5 \cdot(-36) \)

\( y=58 \)

Does Lin’s solution of \((-36, 58)\) makes both equations in the system true?

  • yes; Her solution makes both equations true.
  • No; Her solution does not make both equations true.

Answer: No; Her solution does not make both equations true.

2. Which of the following is true of Lin’s solution?

  • Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 4 and y = -2, or (4,-2)
  • Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 9 and y = 4, or (9,4)
  • Lin made an addition error. The correct answer is x = 0 and y = 4, or (0,4)
  • Lin’s solution of (-36, 58) is correct.

Answer: Lin made a multiplication error. The correct answer is x = 4 and y = -2, or (4,-2)
