  1. In Step 2, what might be a good reason for multiplying each side of the equation by 4?
  • You must always multiply both sides by 4 when completing the square.
  • If the value of \(c\) is positive and you multiply by 4, it will be easier to divide the \(b\) term by 3 before you square it.
  • Multiplying by 4 makes the coefficient of the \(b\) term a perfect square, which makes it easier to complete the square.
  • Multiplying by 4 makes the coefficient of the squared term a perfect square, which makes it easier to complete the square.

Answer: Multiplying by 4 makes the coefficient of the squared term a perfect square, which makes it easier to complete the square.
