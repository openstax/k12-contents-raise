Warm Up (5 minutes)

This warm-up prepares students to work with expressions involving negative exponents. Students have studied exponents and their properties in grade 8 and encountered negative exponents at that time. The goal here is to review the fact that for integer exponents \(m\) and \(n\) and a non-zero base \(b\), this property holds: \(b^m\cdot b^n=b^{m+n}\).

Student Activity

How would you rewrite each of the following as an equivalent expression with a single exponent?

  1. \(2^4\cdot 2^0\)

    Answer: \(2^4\)

  2. \(2^4\cdot 2^{-1}\)

    Answer: \(2^3\)

  3. \(2^4\cdot 2^{-3}\)

    Answer: \(2^1\)

  4. \(2^4\cdot 2^{-4}\)
    Answer: \(2^0\)

Activity Synthesis

Review, if needed, the conventions that \(2^0=1\) and \(2^{-1}=\frac12\), emphasizing that these conventions guarantee that \(2^a\cdot 2^b=2^{a+b}\) for any two integers \(a\) and \(b\).
