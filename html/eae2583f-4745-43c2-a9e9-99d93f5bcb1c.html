Lesson Narrative 

Prior to this point, students have worked primarily with equations in one and two variables. In this lesson, they shift their attention to inequalities. Here and in the next two lessons, students interpret, write, and find solutions to inequalities in one variable. The activities here are grounded in contexts, and the ideas build on the work students have done in grade 7 (TEKS 7.10).

The focus of this lesson is on interpreting and writing inequalities that represent the constraints in various situations. As students consider key quantities in a situation, select variables to represent them, and decide whether multiple inequalities (and which ones) are needed, they engage in aspects of mathematical modeling.

In future lessons, students will use the insights from this lesson to find and interpret the solutions to inequalities that model situations.

Learning Goals (Teacher Facing)

Students will be able to:

  • Interpret and write inequalities that represent the constraints in a situation.

Learning Targets (Student Facing)

  • Write inequalities that represent the constraints in a situation.

Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS)

TEKS

Explanation of Coverage

A5(B) solve linear inequalities in one variable, including those for which the application of the distributive property is necessary and for which variables are included on both sides.

Foundational: Lesson provides foundational content necessary for students to be able to perform this TEKS in later lessons.
