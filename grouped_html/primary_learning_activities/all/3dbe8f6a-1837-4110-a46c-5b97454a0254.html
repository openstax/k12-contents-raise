Recall that the slope-intercept form of the equation of a line is \(y=mx+b\), where \(m\) is the slope and \(b\) is the \(𝑦\)-intercept. The \(𝑦\)-intercept is the point where the graph crosses the \(𝑦\)-axis. For \(𝑦\)-intercept \(𝑏\), the coordinates of the point are \((0,𝑏)\).

In the following activity, you will complete a series of questions regarding this information.
