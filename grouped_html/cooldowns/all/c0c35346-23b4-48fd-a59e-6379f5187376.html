Here are three methods for solving \(3x^2+8x+5=0\).

Carefully examine each method.

Method 1: Method 2: Method 3:

\(\begin{array}{rcl} 3 x^{2}+8 x+5 &=&0 \\ (3 x+5)(x+1) &=&0 \\ x+1 &=&0 \\ 3 x+5 &=&0 \\ x &=&-1 \\ 3 x &=&-5 \\ x &=&-\frac{5}{3} \end{array}\)

\(x=-\frac{5}{3} \text { and } x=-1\)

\(\begin{aligned} 3 x^{2}+8 x+5 &=0 \\ 9 x^{2}+24 x+15 &=0 \\ (3 x)^{2}+8(3 x)+15 &=0 \\ U^{2}+8 U+15 &=0 \\ (U+5)(U+3) &=0 \\ U+5 &=0 \\ U+3 &=0 \\ U &=-5 \\ U &=-3 \\ 3 x &=-5 \\ 3 x &=-3 \end{aligned} \)

\(x=-\frac{5}{3} \text { and } x=-1\)

\(\begin{aligned} 3 x^{2}+8 x+5 &=0 \\ 9 x^{2}+24 x+15 &=0 \\ 9 x^{2}+24 x+16 &=1 \\ (3 x+4)^{2} &=1 \\ 3 x+4 &=1 \\ 3 x+4 &=-1\end{aligned}\)

\(x=-\frac{5}{3} \text { and } x=-1\)

\(\begin{array}{rcl} 10x^{2}+37x &=&36 \\ \frac{(10x^{2}+37x)}{10} &=&\frac{36}{10} \\ x^{2}+\frac{37}{10}x &=&\frac{36}{10} \\ x^2+\frac{37}{10}x+((\frac{1}{2})(\frac{37}{10}))^{2} &=&\frac{36}{10}+((\frac{1}{2})(\frac{37}{10}))^{2} \\ x^2+\frac{37}{10}x+\frac{1369}{400} &=&\frac{36}{10}+\frac{1369}{400} \\ x^2+\frac{37}{10}x+\frac{1369}{400} &=&\frac{2809}{400} \\ (x+\frac{37}{20})^{2} &=&\frac{2809}{400} \\ \sqrt{(x+\frac{37}{20})^{2}} &=&\sqrt{\frac{2809}{400}}\\ x+\frac{37}{20} &=&\frac{53}{20} \\ x &=&\frac{16}{20} \\ x &=&\frac{4}{5} \\ x+\frac{37}{20} &=&-\frac{53}{20} \\ x &=&-\frac{90}{20} \\ x &=&-\frac{9}{2} \end{array} \)
