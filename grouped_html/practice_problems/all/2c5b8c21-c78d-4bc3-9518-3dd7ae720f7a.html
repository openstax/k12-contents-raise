In questions 1 - 5, choose an equation of a line parallel to the given line and contains the given point. The choices are in slope-intercept form.

  1. Line \(y = 4x + 2\)
    Point \((1, 2)\)

Correct! \(y=4x-2\)

Try again. Revisit activity 1.14.2.

The correct answer is \(y = 4 x -2\).

The slope of a line parallel to \(y = 4x + 2\) is 4. The line contains the point (1,2).

\(\begin{aligned}y − y_1 &= m(x − x_1)\\ y − (2) &= 4(x − (1))\\ y − 2 &= 4(x − 1)\\ y − 2 &= 4x − 4\\ y &= 4x − 2 \end{aligned}\)
